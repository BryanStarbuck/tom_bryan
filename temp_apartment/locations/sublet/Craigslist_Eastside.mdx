# Craigslist Seattle — Eastside

**Worth Tom's time, but Bryan should drive it.** This is the highest-volume free channel on
the Eastside and the one place private landlords who will actually flex on term post. It is
also where **two active rental scams were confirmed during this research** — both targeting
exactly the profile of buyer Tom is. Use it, but never let Tom transact here alone.

## What it is and how it works

Craigslist has no vetting, no escrow, no identity check, and no fee. That is simultaneously
why the inventory is the cheapest available and why the fraud rate is the highest of any
channel in this search except Facebook.

The important structural fact is that Craigslist splits housing into separate sections, and
**the section you pick determines whether you get a private unit or a roommate situation**:

* `est/apa` — apartments and housing for rent. **Whole units.** This is the section to use.
* `est/sub` — sublets and temporary. Mixed, but this is where term flexibility lives.
* `est/roo` — **rooms and shares. Never use this.** Every listing here is a roommate
  situation, which is the one thing Tom's charter rules out.

The `est` subarea maps almost exactly onto our target geography: Bellevue, Redmond,
Kirkland, Bothell, Issaquah.

Of the ~17 live Eastside sublet posts found, only about **6 touch our cities**, and only
about **3 are whole units**. The sublet section is **room-dominant**. Do not expect volume;
expect to scan `apa` manually instead.

## How to search it effectively

Whole units, our geography:

```
https://seattle.craigslist.org/search/est/apa
```

Sublets and temporary:

```
https://seattle.craigslist.org/search/est/sub
```

**Post a free wanted ad** — this is the highest-leverage move on Craigslist and costs nothing:

```
https://seattle.craigslist.org/search/sbw
```

There is no lease-length filter. Search the keywords `month-to-month`, `sublet`,
`short term`, and `furnished` in the search box, but treat keyword search as narrow — see
[Craigslist as a search surface](../search_site/Craigslist_search.mdx) for the mechanics and
the postal-radius test results.

Listings turn over in hours. This channel needs **daily scanning**, not a weekly check.

## Scam risk

This is the point of this page. Read it before spending a dollar.

### Two confirmed live scams, by address

**1. 12505 NE 143rd St, Unit B303, Kirkland.** Advertised as a furnished 1BR at
**$1,200/mo**. The unit is not for rent — it is **actively FOR SALE at $335,000**
(MLS# 2487950). The ad copies the MLS description verbatim: vaulted ceilings, wood-burning
fireplace, butcher-block counters. The scammer scraped the sale listing and reposted it as a
rental.

**2. 9904 NE 142nd Pl, Unit C302, Kirkland.** Same **$1,200** pitch. Redfin shows this unit
**off-market** (last sold $281,000 in 2018). The identical unit next door at 9912 NE 142nd Pl
rents for **$2,995**.

### The tell

A furnished Kirkland 1BR at $1,200 is roughly **40% of market**. **That gap is the scam.**
There is no such thing as a legitimate furnished Eastside 1BR at that price. Treat any
Eastside furnished 1BR under ~$1,800/month as presumptively fraudulent until physically
walked.

### The playbook these scammers run

The "owner" is out of state or overseas. They will not show the unit. They have a sympathetic
story about why. They demand a **wire, Zelle, Venmo, or gift-card** deposit to "hold" it,
with urgency attached. **None of those payment rails are reversible.** Once sent, the money
is gone and there is no chargeback.

### The rule

**Never send money before physically walking the unit, meeting the person face to face, and
reading the actual lease.** No exceptions, no matter how good the story is. If the person
cannot meet you at the unit, the listing is fake.

### One more flag

A legitimate-looking whole unit does exist on this channel — a **Vue Kirkland condo at
$450/week (≈ $1,950/mo)**. But it is posted by **"Tony at Apartment Hunters LLC"** — that is
a **broker, not an individual owner**. Not automatically a scam, but a different transaction
than the ad implies. **Verify their Washington real-estate license before paying anything.**
