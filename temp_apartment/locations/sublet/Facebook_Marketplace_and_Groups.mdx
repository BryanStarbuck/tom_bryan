## What it is and how it works

Two different surfaces sit under one name:

**Marketplace** (`facebook.com/marketplace/category/propertyrentals`) — an open listing board
with no vetting whatsoever. Anyone with an account can post anything. This is where the >50%
fraud figure lives. The FTC has repeatedly warned about rental fraud on social platforms.

**Groups** — moderated communities. Admins approve members, and members have real social
graphs attached to their names. This is structurally safer than Marketplace, and it is the
only reason this page is not a pure "skip."

## How to search it effectively

**The one bright spot — Seattle Summer Intern Housing:**

```
https://www.facebook.com/groups/577160885692636/
```

This group genuinely fits Tom's need, for a specific reason. **Redmond is Microsoft
headquarters.** Intern leases run mid-June to late August. When interns leave, they sublet
**whole studios and 1BRs** — not rooms — at or below their own rent, typically
**$1,200–3,000 furnished**, with utilities often bundled. Mid-July is decent timing to catch
an August departure.

**The other groups are a poor fit** — Seattle Sublets, Seattle Housing Rooms Apartments
Sublets, Seattle WA Housing are all Seattle-proper and room-dominant. Wrong side of the lake,
wrong product.

**Start now:** you must request to join each group, and approval takes hours to days. If this
channel is going to work at all, the request has to go in today.

## Scam risk

**Recommend Tom not transact here unaccompanied.** This is the strongest warning on any page
in this search.

### The signature pattern

* **Stolen photos** lifted from a real Zillow or Redfin listing
* **Fake or newly-created landlord profile** — no history, no friends, no tagged photos
* **Refusal to meet in person** — always a reason, always sympathetic
* **Insistence on Cash App, Apple Pay, Venmo, or Zelle** — **none of these are reversible**
* **Manufactured urgency** — "three other applicants, I need the deposit today"

That last one is the emotional lever. It exists to stop you from doing the one thing that
would expose the fraud: going to the unit.

### Even in the good group, verify

The intern group is lower risk — moderated, real social graphs — but lower is not zero:

1. **Verify the person's employer.** An intern subletting should be able to prove they work
   where they say.
2. **Meet at the unit.** Always. No exceptions.
3. **Get the primary tenant's landlord to acknowledge the sublease IN WRITING.** This is not
   optional paperwork. Washington leases commonly require landlord consent to sublet. An
   unauthorized sublet can get **Tom evicted through no fault of his own** — he pays, he
   moves in, and the building removes him because the tenant never had the right to sublet.
