## What it is and how it works

A City of Redmond senior and community center. **No sublet board exists** — do not call
expecting a list of available units. What they provide is **referrals and resource guides**.

**Set expectations correctly on what they will offer versus what Tom needs.** Nearly
everything in the formal senior-housing space they can point to is **age-restricted 62+,
income-qualified housing with waitlists**. That is a fundamentally different product than
what Tom is looking for:

* It is **permanent housing**, not a 1–2 month bridge.
* It is **income-qualified**, with paperwork.
* It has **waitlists** — which makes it **far too slow**. Tom needs a place in weeks, not
  after a waitlist clears.

So the formal answer they give will not fit. **The informal one might.**

## How to search it effectively

There is nothing to search. **Call:**

```
425-556-2314
```

Resource page:

```
https://www.redmond.gov/206/Senior-Services-and-Resources
```

**What to actually ask.** Do not ask "do you have senior housing" — that question routes
straight to the 62+ waitlist answer, which is useless here. Ask instead:

> "My father is a senior moving to Redmond and needs a private place for one to two months
> while he looks for a permanent apartment. Do you know of anyone locally — a member, a
> neighbor — with an empty unit, ADU, or mother-in-law apartment they rent short-term?"

**That is the whole play.** Senior center staff sit at the center of a local social network.
They hear about the widow with an empty basement apartment and the couple whose ADU is
vacant between tenants — units that are **advertised nowhere**, on no website, in no
database. Same logic that makes [Nextdoor](./Nextdoor.mdx) valuable, but with a human doing
the matching.

One call. Ten minutes. Then move on to the calls that can actually produce a quote.

## Scam risk

**Essentially zero — and that is why this page exists.**

There is no listing board, no anonymous poster, no payment rail, and no marketplace. A
referral from municipal senior-center staff is a referral from a named person at a city
facility with a public phone number and a physical address in Redmond. There is no mechanism
here for the fraud that dominates
[Craigslist](./Craigslist_Eastside.mdx) and
[Facebook](./Facebook_Marketplace_and_Groups.mdx).

**Given who the tenant is, that matters more than the low expected yield.** Every other free
channel in this search requires Bryan to actively defend his father against fraud. This one
does not.

One caveat: a referral is a lead, not a vetted unit. The person on the other end is still a
stranger. **Tour the unit in person, meet the owner, read the lease before paying anything** —
the rule does not bend just because the introduction was warm.
