## What it is and how it works

A sublet listing marketplace with a **pay-to-contact model**: you can see listings for free,
but contact information for the lister sits behind a **$12–19.95 paywall** that
**auto-expires after 90 days**. Both research agents agree on this model. That is the one
undisputed fact on this page.

### The conflicting reports — read both

**Agent 1 found the best structured whole-unit source in the search:**

* **9 Redmond listings, 6 of them whole units**
* A **$2,300 1BR, month-to-month**
* A **$2,950 open-ended 2BR**

Both furnished, both whole units, both under budget, both with terms that flex to 1–2 months.
On paper that is a better fit than most of the Top 20.

**Agent 2 found a site with a serious reputation problem:**

* **2.0/5 rating across 50 reviews**
* **0 of 14 ComplaintsBoard complaints ever resolved — 100% unresolved**
* Documented **stale listings**
* **Redirects demanding additional fees after payment**
* **Locked accounts after purchase**

### The verdict that reconciles them

**The paywall is not the risk.** Twenty dollars is nothing against a $3,300/month decision.

**The risk is what you get for the twenty dollars:** the "landlord" you unlock is
**unverified**, and the listing may be **months dead**. You are not buying a vetted contact.
You are buying a phone number that may ring nowhere, attached to a person nobody checked.

The two agents are not actually contradicting each other. Attractive listings can coexist
with a broken business — in fact, stale attractive listings are exactly what a site with
100% unresolved complaints would show.

## How to search it effectively

```
https://www.sublet.com/apartments-for-rent/seattle-area/redmond/
```

Browse free, and **filter out the room listings** — the Redmond set is 9 listings, of which 6
are whole units. Only the whole units matter for Tom.

If a specific unit looks real, the $12–19.95 unlock is a rational spend **on that one unit**.
Do not buy access speculatively and do not assume the 90-day window means 90 days of value.

## Scam risk

**Apply the same test used on the fake Kirkland condos.**

The **$1,450 Redmond 1BR** at the bottom of this site's range deserves exactly the skepticism
the [Craigslist scams](./Craigslist_Eastside.mdx) earned. Redmond's average rent is roughly
**$2,420**. A furnished Redmond 1BR at $1,450 is **well under market** — and on the
Craigslist page, that same below-market gap turned out to be the entire scam. Nothing about
Sublet.com's paywall prevents a scraper from posting a fake listing here; it just charges you
to reach them.

The site's own documented behavior compounds it: **payment-triggered upsells** and **locked
accounts** are patterns you would flag as fraudulent if an individual landlord did them.

**Rules for this channel:**

* Never pay a deposit to anyone unlocked here without **physically walking the unit** and
  **meeting the person**.
* Assume any listing may be months old. **Confirm it is still available before paying the
  unlock fee**, if you can.
* Treat the sub-$1,800 listings as presumptively fake until seen in person.
