# Nextdoor

**Worth Tom's time, and the most underrated channel in this search.** It is structurally the
**lowest-scam-risk free channel** available, because members are verified against a physical
street address. Volume is tiny, but the inventory that does appear — ADUs and mother-in-law
units — is precisely the whole-private-unit product Tom needs.

## What it is and how it works

Nextdoor is a neighborhood social network. **Members verify against a physical street
address** — you have to actually live where you say you live. That single design decision is
what separates it from Craigslist and Facebook, where anyone anywhere can post anything.

The trade-off: **there is no dedicated rentals tab.** Housing posts are unstructured, mixed
into general neighborhood chatter, and low volume. You cannot filter. You browse and search.

The inventory skews toward **ADUs and mother-in-law units** — the detached or
basement-converted studios attached to a single-family home, typically **$1,800–2,800**.
These are whole private units with their own entrance. They are exactly the product Tom
wants, and they are almost invisible on the commercial listing sites.

## How to search it effectively

```
https://nextdoor.com/
```

Search these terms across Redmond, East Bellevue (Crossroads, Lake Hills), and Kirkland:

* `sublet`
* `rental`
* `ADU`
* `mother-in-law`

**But the best use of this channel is POSTING, not browsing.** This is the key insight.
A neighbor with an empty ADU frequently **advertises nowhere else** — no Craigslist ad, no
Zillow listing, no sign. They mention it to their neighborhood and it fills. The only way to
reach that inventory is to be the person asking.

Post a short, human "housing wanted" note in Redmond, East Bellevue, and Kirkland: retired
father, needs a private 1–2 month whole-unit rental, budget under $3,300, quiet, no pets
issue (adjust), can provide references. That post costs nothing and reaches the exact people
who own the ADUs.

## Scam risk

**Lowest of any free channel — but not zero.**

The address verification is a real barrier. A scammer in another country cannot casually
create a verified Redmond account the way they can create a Facebook profile or a Craigslist
post. That structural friction is why this channel is recommended for a senior when Facebook
is not.

However: **address verification can be gamed.** It is a speed bump, not a wall. The same rule
applies here as everywhere else:

**Never pay before touring the unit in person, meeting the owner, and seeing the lease.**

A legitimate neighbor with a real ADU will have no problem showing it to you — they live on
the property. Any resistance to an in-person tour is disqualifying, on this channel as much
as any other.
