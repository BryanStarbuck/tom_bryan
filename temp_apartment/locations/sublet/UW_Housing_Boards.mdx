## What it is and how it works

University of Washington housing boards — the official intern housing program plus an
off-campus listing board. Two separate reasons this does not work for Tom:

**1. Eligibility and term.** The program is **restricted to students and interns**, and the
term is **fixed: June 21 to late August**. That is not month-to-month. It is a
take-it-or-leave-it summer block, and Tom would need to qualify as a student or intern to
take it at all. Since today is 2026-07-15, the term is already underway — he would be paying
for a block that started three weeks ago and ends in about six.

**2. Geography.** The U District is **~25 minutes** from Redmond, outside the 20-minute rule
from the charter, and on the wrong side of the lake from Redmond, East Bellevue, and South
Kirkland.

**3. Product.** It is **room-dominant** — dorm and apartment-style shared arrangements. The
charter rules out roommates. The $1,100–1,800 price is attractive precisely *because* it is
shared housing, which is the same trap the coliving category falls into.

Any one of these would be disqualifying. All three together make this a completeness entry.

## How to search it effectively

If checking it anyway, the off-campus board is the only surface with any chance of a whole
unit:

```
https://offcampushousing.uw.edu/listing
```

The intern housing program itself:

```
https://hfs.uw.edu/seattle-intern-housing/
```

Filter aggressively for **entire unit** and ignore anything listed as a room or a shared
apartment. Expect almost nothing to survive that filter, and nothing at all on the Eastside.

**Better use of the same effort:** the
[Facebook Seattle Summer Intern Housing group](./Facebook_Marketplace_and_Groups.mdx)
captures the same intern-sublet dynamic but **on the Eastside**, near Microsoft in Redmond,
and with **whole studios and 1BRs** rather than dorm rooms. That is the version of this idea
that actually fits.

## Scam risk

Lower than the open boards — the intern program runs through the university, and the
off-campus board has some institutional association. But the off-campus listing board is
**not vetted by UW**, and anyone can post to it.

The standard rules still apply: **never pay before touring the unit in person and meeting the
person**, and get written landlord consent for any sublet.

Realistically, scam risk is moot here. The eligibility, term, and geography rule this out
before fraud ever becomes the deciding question.
