## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Start with the structural fact, because it reframes the whole category:**

**Travelers Haven was acquired by Blueground in February 2023.** It now operates as **Blu Corporate Housing**. So what looks like three separate vendors in this research — Zeus Living, Travelers Haven, Blueground — is **now one company: Blueground**. (Zeus Living is defunct outright.)

That consolidation is a key finding, not a footnote. The venture-funded flexible-rental category **collapsed**. Competitors merged or died, competition thinned, and the pricing that survived reflects a market with fewer players in it. When you notice that the corporate-housing quotes all come in high, this is part of why. You are not shopping three vendors against each other. You are shopping one vendor wearing three names.

**How the model actually works — it's a broker, not a marketplace.** You cannot browse inventory. The process:

1. You call.
2. They consult with you on criteria — location, size, dates, budget.
3. They email a curated set of options with photos and pricing.
4. You pick one.
5. They send a lease.
6. You pay deposit + first month through them.

They guarantee **move-in-ready**: furnished, equipped kitchen, internet and utilities already live, 24/7 support. For a senior, "walk in, everything works, someone answers at 3am" has real value.

**The con, and it's the same one that afflicts this whole category:** Travelers Haven is built for **corporate relocation accounts**, not retirees paying out of pocket. Tom is an atypical customer. He may be deprioritized behind an employer with a hundred placements a year, or quoted a premium because there's no volume relationship to protect.

Worth exactly one exploratory call — not more.

## The price math

**No public rates. Nothing to compute.**

There is no published nightly rate, no monthly rate, and no budget band — which is the tell. Firms that quote through a consultation are pricing to the customer, and their default customer is an employer's relocation budget.

**The useful move here is to skip the guessing and go to [Blu](./Blu_Corporate_Housing.mdx) instead**, because Blu — the same company — **does publish budget bands**, and the lowest is **$3,000–3,499**. That is the only real number available for this company. If that band is achievable in Bellevue, it's achievable through either brand.

| Blu's published low band *(same company)* | $3,000–3,499 | $6,000–6,998 |

Ask for that band by name on the call. It's their own published number.

## When you call

**(866) 601-1301** — this is the **individuals line**, and it's the right one. The general line (877) 591-6640 and the main number 720-833-5333 route toward corporate accounts. Mon–Fri, 8–5.

Or email **lease@executivecorporatehousing.com**.

Ask:

1. **"Are you Blu now?"** Confirm which entity is actually quoting and contracting. The brands are merged; make sure you're not being double-worked by two names for one company.
2. **"I want the $3,000–3,499 band"** — Blu publishes it. Name it.
3. **Studio or 1BR**, Bellevue (East Bellevue preferred), **30 to 60 days**.
4. **This is an individual, not a corporate account, paying out of pocket.** Say it early. It determines whether you get a real quote or a relocation quote, and you want to find out fast.
5. **Send me the actual options with addresses and pricing by email.** That's their stated process — hold them to it.
6. Parking, pets, and housekeeping — none are published. Get all three as line items.

**If they quote $4,500+, thank them and go call [Between Moves](./Between_Moves.mdx).**
