## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The price signal is the headline.** Blu is the **only provider in this category whose site exposes actual budget bands**, and the lowest band starts at **$3,000–3,499**. Every other corporate provider here hides pricing behind an intake form. Blu telling you a $3,000–3,499 band exists implies **real Bellevue inventory in the low $3,000s** — which straddles Tom's $3,300 ceiling rather than blowing through it.

In a category where the honest expectation is $4,500+, that is genuinely the best news available.

**What's included** is a strong bundle for a senior on a 1–2 month stay:

* Fully furnished
* High-speed WiFi
* Fully equipped kitchen
* **In-unit washer/dryer** — worth calling out; several cheaper options in this search make you use a shared laundry room
* Fitness center access
* Covered parking
* Private whole apartments — no roommate risk

**Payment is flexible, and this matters.** You can Pay in Full at move-in, **or Pay Monthly** through their portal. For a 1–2 month bridge stay where you don't want to front the entire lease before the long-term place is even found, the Pay Monthly option is a real advantage. Ask for it by name.

**The caveat — the 720 area code is Denver.** This is not a local shop. It's a national-ish firm with Bellevue inventory, which means whoever picks up the phone may have never been to the Eastside. They will not know that Eastgate is not Downtown Bellevue, or what a 12-minute Redmond commute means in practice. **Push for the actual unit address before committing to anything.** "Bellevue" from a Denver call center can mean a lot of things.

Also worth knowing: Blu is now the operating brand for [Travelers Haven](./Travelers_Haven.mdx), which Blueground acquired in Feb 2023. Blu, Travelers Haven, and [Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx) are all the same company now.

## The price math

**Using the published low band ($3,000–3,499):**

| | |
|---|---|
| 30 days at the low end of the low band | **$3,000** — under budget by $300 |
| 30 days at the high end of the low band | **$3,499** — over budget by $199 |
| 60 days at the low end | **$6,000** — $3,000/mo |
| 60 days at the high end | **$6,998** — $3,499/mo |

**Using the full published range:**

| | 30 days | 60 days |
|---|---|---|
| Low | $3,000 | $6,000 |
| High | $4,500 | $9,000 |

The low band **straddles the ceiling** — the bottom of it clears budget, the top of it doesn't. This is the whole reason to call: the answer to "does Blu work?" is entirely about which end of that band a Bellevue studio or 1BR actually lands on for Tom's dates.

Utilities, WiFi, and covered parking are included, so these numbers are close to all-in — unlike [Preferred](./Preferred_Corporate_Housing.mdx), where parking is a separate add-on. That makes Blu's $3,000 a better $3,000 than it looks.

*Bands are published on Blu's site as filters, not as quotes for a specific unit. Confirm the real number by phone.*

## When you call

**720-833-5333.**

Ask for exactly this — be specific, because a Denver call center will otherwise quote you their default:

1. **"I want the $3,000–3,499 band."** Name it. It's on your own site.
2. **Studio or 1BR** — not a 2BR. Don't pay for space Tom won't use.
3. **Downtown Bellevue or East Bellevue** — East Bellevue is the preference.
4. **30 to 60 days.** Confirm the actual minimum stay — it isn't published, and 30 days is only the corporate convention, not a promise.
5. **Pay Monthly**, not Pay in Full. Ask for the portal.
6. **What is the unit's actual street address?** Get this before committing. The person on the phone is in Denver.
7. Confirm: parking included at no extra charge? Pets — allowed or not? (Not published.)
