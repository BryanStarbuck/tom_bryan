## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The scope is right.** Express serves the Seattle metro including **Redmond and Bellevue** (plus Everett and Tacoma), and markets explicitly to people *"temporarily relocating to Seattle for a month or longer."* The 1–2 month term is squarely in scope — stated, not inferred. The minimum is **1 month**.

**The bundle is the best-specified in this category**, and unusually complete for a senior:

* Fully furnished
* **Washer and dryer in unit**
* High-speed internet
* **Parking — included**, not an add-on (contrast [Preferred](./Preferred_Corporate_Housing.mdx), where parking is a separate line item)
* **All utilities**
* Equipped kitchen — utensils, tableware, cookware
* Cable/satellite TV
* **Pet-friendly options**
* Private whole apartments

Booking is by phone or web form with a "concierge-level" consultation. And unlike [Aplus](./Aplus_Housing.mdx) and [National](./National_Corporate_Housing.mdx), **a real phone number exists and a human answers it.**

**Now the structural problem, and it's disqualifying on value:**

The address is **110 Vintage Park Blvd STE 250, Houston, Texas.** Express is a **Houston-based broker with no Seattle office.** They don't own Eastside inventory and they don't master-lease it. They **source it from local suppliers** — quite possibly the same [Aplus](./Aplus_Housing.mdx) and [Blu](./Blu_Corporate_Housing.mdx) units already in this list — **and mark it up**.

That's the wrong end of the chain. If Express finds Tom a Redmond unit, that unit almost certainly came from a local provider you could have called directly, at the price before the broker's margin. You'd be paying a Houston company to make a phone call you can make yourself.

Fallback if the local calls come up empty.

## The price math

**Nothing published. Nothing to compute for 30 or 60 days.**

But the broker structure lets you reason about the *shape* of the number even without a quote:

```
What Express quotes  =  local supplier's price  +  Express's margin
```

The local supplier's price is what [Aplus](./Aplus_Housing.mdx) ($5,550–9,750/mo estimated) or [Blu](./Blu_Corporate_Housing.mdx) ($3,000–4,500/mo published) charges. **Express is that, plus a markup.** By construction, Express cannot beat the supplier you could call directly — the margin has to come from somewhere, and it comes from Tom.

There is one legitimate counterweight worth naming: the bundle really does include parking, utilities, and pets, and a bundled price can occasionally beat an unbundled one where parking is a $200–300/month add-on. That's a real effect. It is not usually worth a broker margin.

*All of the above is structural reasoning, not a quote. Express publishes no rates.*

## When you call

**888-899-7829** or **281-894-0600.** A human answers — that's the genuine advantage here.

Ask, in this order:

1. **"Who is your on-the-ground supplier in Redmond and Bellevue?"** Ask it point-blank, early. If they name a local provider, thank them and call that provider directly. This single question is the highest-value thing on this page.
2. **All-in monthly for a 1BR**, Redmond or East Bellevue, **30 days and 60 days**. Get both.
3. **Confirm what's bundled** — you advertise parking, all utilities, in-unit washer/dryer, and pet-friendly options. Which of those apply to *this specific unit*?
4. **What is the actual street address of the unit?** They're in Houston. They may not know why Eastgate ≠ Downtown Bellevue, or what a 20-minute Redmond drive means.
5. **Confirm the 1-month minimum** applies to an individual, not just corporate accounts.
6. Say plainly: **individual paying out of pocket, no company relocation budget.**
