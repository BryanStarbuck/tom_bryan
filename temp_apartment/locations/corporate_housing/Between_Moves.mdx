## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

On paper this is the best-shaped lead in the search:

* **21–25 years serving the Eastside** — Redmond, Kirkland, Bellevue, Bothell, Woodinville, Issaquah. That is Tom's exact geography, not a national footprint that happens to include Bellevue.
* **Month-to-month is the stated term.** Not "30 days minimum, corporate convention." Actually month-to-month.
* **Furnished condos, private homes, and apartments** — all whole private units. No roommate risk.
* **Utilities, cable, and WiFi included.**
* **One dedicated contact: Coral.** For a senior, this matters more than it looks on paper. Every other corporate provider in this category routes you to a call center or a web form. Here, one person answers, knows the inventory, and remembers you called.

**The blocking problem:** their site currently states *"At this time, we have NO units available to rent."* And there is no published pricing, no stated minimum stay details, no amenity list, and no online booking — everything runs through the phone.

**Call anyway.** A one-person operation with no website inventory is precisely the kind of shop whose availability turns over by phone before it's ever posted online. The website not being current is a feature of the business model, not evidence of an empty pipeline. And the pricing is likely the most reasonable in this category *because* there is no national sales organization taking a cut and no relocation-budget pricing baked in.

Low cost to call. Potentially the best-value lead in the whole search.

## The price math

**Nothing to compute — no rates are published.** Not for 30 days, not for 60.

That is not a red flag here the way it is at [National](./National_Corporate_Housing.mdx) or [Preferred](./Preferred_Corporate_Housing.mdx). Those firms hide pricing behind a corporate intake form because they're quoting to an employer's relocation budget. Between Moves doesn't publish pricing because it's a small local shop that prices each unit on the phone.

What you can reason about: utilities, cable, and WiFi are bundled, so whatever number Coral quotes is closer to all-in than most. There's no corporate markup layer. Get real numbers for both 30 and 60 nights on the call — 60 nights may price better per month.

## When you call

**(425) 822-0533 — ask for Coral.** Or email Coral@BetweenMoves.com.

Ask her, in this order:

1. **Do you have anything at all coming available?** The site says no — but ask about the pipeline, not just today's inventory. What turns over in the next 2–6 weeks?
2. **Put Tom on the waitlist.** Give her the move-in date and the 1–2 month term up front.
3. **What does a furnished 1BR or studio run per month** — in Redmond, East Bellevue, or South Kirkland? For 30 nights and for 60.
4. **What's actually included** — you list utilities, cable and WiFi; is parking included or extra?
5. **Pets** — it's case-by-case; ask what the case is.
6. **The most important question: do you know other Eastside owner-operators?** Coral has been doing this on the Eastside for two decades. She is the **referral node in this market**. Even if she has nothing, she knows who does — and those operators are invisible to every search that produced this list. This one question may be worth more than the call itself.
