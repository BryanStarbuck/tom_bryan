## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Everything about Aplus is right except the price, and the price is disqualifying.**

What's right:

* **Locally owned, Seattle area since 1998** — 30+ years of principal experience. Not a national firm with a Bellevue page.
* **Serves exactly Tom's geography**: Seattle, Bellevue, Kirkland, Redmond, Bothell.
* **30+ days minimum only — no vacation rentals.** This is a meaningful quality signal. It means no nightly-turnover churn, no party bookings, no revolving door of weekend guests in the building. The whole business is built for stays like Tom's.
* **The inclusion list is the best in the category**: furniture, kitchenware, linens, WiFi, laundry, **all utilities**, monthly or bi-monthly cleaning, **grocery delivery at check-in**, and 24/7 emergency service. For a senior arriving in a new city, groceries already in the fridge and a 24/7 number to call are genuinely valuable — not marketing filler.
* **All private whole units.** No roommate risk.

What's wrong: **it costs roughly double the budget.**

Also awkward: **there is no phone number published anywhere** — not on the site, not in directory listings. Everything runs through a "Create Your Stay" 10-step intake quiz. Several listings say "call for pricing," but there is nothing to call. For a senior, a 10-step web quiz with no human on the other end is a poor front door.

## The price math

**From their posted nightly rates:**

| Market | Posted nightly | × 30 days | × 60 days | vs. $3,300 budget |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bellevue | $185/night | **≈ $5,550** | ≈ $11,100 | **+68% over** |
| Kirkland | $325/night | **≈ $9,750** | ≈ $19,500 | **+195% over** |
| Seattle | $175–250/night | ≈ $5,250–7,500 | ≈ $10,500–15,000 | +59% to +127% over |

**68% to 195% over budget.** Bellevue is the least-bad case and it still overshoots by more than $2,200/month. Kirkland is nearly triple.

*These are estimates: they're posted nightly rates multiplied by 30 and 60. Aplus does not publish monthly totals.*

**The one reason not to write it off completely:** posted nightly rates at corporate-housing firms are usually the **30-day rack rate** — the worst number anyone pays. A 60-day stay sometimes negotiates down, because a 60-day booking is more valuable to the operator than two 30-day ones. So the real 60-day quote may come in below $5,550/month.

But "below $5,550" is not "$3,300." Closing a 68% gap through negotiation is not realistic. Submit the inquiry, see the number, move on.

## How to book

**There is no phone number.** The only path is the **"Create Your Stay" 10-step intake quiz** on https://aplusnw.com/.

If you submit it:

1. State **60 days**, not 30 — the longer term is your only negotiating lever, and it's the one thing that might move the number.
2. Ask for a **studio or 1BR** in **Redmond or East Bellevue** — Bellevue is $185/night, Kirkland is $325/night. Do not let this become a Kirkland quote.
3. Ask directly: **"Is the posted nightly rate the 30-day rate, and is there a lower 60-day rate?"**
4. Ask for a **monthly all-in number**, not a nightly rate. Nightly rates hide the total.
5. Ask whether there's **a phone number for a human** — worth having for a senior even if the price fails.

**Expected outcome: over budget.** File this as a completeness call, not a real candidate. Put the effort into [Between Moves](./Between_Moves.mdx) and [Blu](./Blu_Corporate_Housing.mdx) instead.
