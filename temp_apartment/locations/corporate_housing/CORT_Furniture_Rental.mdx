## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The structural insight:** every furnished corporate unit in this search charges a **furnished premium of roughly $1,500–2,500/month** over the equivalent unfurnished apartment. That premium is what pushes [Aplus](./Aplus_Housing.mdx) to $5,550/mo and [Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx) to an estimated $3,500–5,000/mo.

CORT replaces that premium for a few hundred dollars a month. Unfurnished Redmond/East Bellevue 1BR + CORT is a **far cheaper structure** than any furnished corporate unit. That's the unbundling play, and the arithmetic on it is the most favorable in the entire search.

**But here is the problem, and it's the one that matters: the blocker is not furniture. It's the lease.**

Almost no Eastside apartment community will write a 1–2 month term. The research found the shortest conventional terms anywhere are **2 months** ([The Luke](../apartment/The_Luke.mdx), [Redmond Place](../apartment/Redmond_Place.mdx)) — and the few buildings that will go short charge unpublished short-term premiums that tend to erase exactly the savings CORT creates.

So **CORT only works if you FIRST find a landlord who'll do month-to-month.** That points straight back at the private-owner channel: [CHBO's private owners](../platform/Corporate_Housing_by_Owner.mdx) and [Between Moves](./Between_Moves.mdx). Those are the people who can actually write the term. CORT is step two, not step one.

**Do not call CORT first.** Secure a unit, then call.

One useful cross-reference: [Reflections by Windsor](../apartment/Reflections_by_Windsor.mdx) has a **CORT partnership with 48-hour delivery**. That building is a weak fit on term (12-month only), but the partnership shows the model works and delivery can be fast when the pieces line up.

## The price math

**Read the published number carefully — it does not apply to Tom.**

CORT publishes **1BR packages starting at $240–295/month**. Two caveats make that a floor, not an expectation:

1. **That is the 12-MONTH rate.** CORT's rates vary by how long you rent, and a **1–2 month rental prices meaningfully higher**. Tom needs 1–2 months. He will not pay $240.
2. **Delivery and pickup fees are extra and unpublished.** On a 2-month rental those fees are amortized over 2 months instead of 12, so they hit proportionally ~6× harder.

**Treat $240–295 as a FLOOR, not the expected 2-month price.**

**The structural comparison (this is the real point):**

| Path | Monthly |
|---|---|
| Furnished corporate 1BR (Aplus, Blueground tier) | $3,500–5,550 |
| Unfurnished Eastside 1BR + CORT | Base rent + $240–295 *(floor)* + delivery/pickup + short-term premium |

Against an unfurnished Redmond 1BR in the ~$2,000–2,700 range, even a CORT package at 2–3× the published floor still lands the combined structure well under the furnished corporate options. The furnished premium is $1,500–2,500/mo; CORT is a few hundred. That gap is why this play exists.

**What kills it:** the short-term lease premium on the apartment side, which is unpublished at every building that offers 2-month terms. If that premium is $800/month, the CORT savings evaporate. You cannot know until you ask the landlord — which is another reason to secure the unit before calling CORT.

*Every number above except the $240–295 published floor is an estimate. Get a real 2-month quote including delivery and pickup.*

## When you call

**Call CORT LAST — after a unit is secured.** There is nothing to price until you know the apartment, the term, and the move-in date.

Showroom: **2118 148th Ave NE, Redmond** (Overlake Plaza), ~5 minutes from downtown Redmond. Being able to walk in and see the furniture is a real advantage for a senior — Tom can sit in the chair.

**(425) 242-5435** or **888-360-2678**.

Ask:

1. **What does a 1BR package cost for a 2-month rental?** State the term up front. Do not let them quote the 12-month rate.
2. **What are the delivery and pickup fees?** Both directions. These are unpublished and they matter disproportionately on a short rental.
3. **What is the shortest rental term you'll write?**
4. **How fast is delivery?** (48 hours is achievable — that's the Reflections by Windsor partnership standard.)
5. **Does the package include everything** — bed, seating, table, lamps — or are there gaps to fill?
