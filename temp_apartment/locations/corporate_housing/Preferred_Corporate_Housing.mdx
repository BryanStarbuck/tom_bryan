## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The reason this page exists: Eastgate and Somerset.**

Preferred's Bellevue page covers **14+ neighborhoods including Eastgate and Somerset** — both of which are **East Bellevue**, Tom's #2 preferred location. That is a direct hit on the charter. Nearly every other corporate provider in this search means "Downtown Bellevue" when they say Bellevue, and treats East Bellevue as an afterthought if they acknowledge it at all. [Blu](./Blu_Corporate_Housing.mdx), for instance, covers Downtown and West Bellevue.

Terms start at **30 days** with flexible options *"depending on availability and client needs"* — which is soft language, but it's an opening.

**What's bundled into one bill:**

* Professionally selected furniture
* Complete housewares and kitchen supplies
* **Utilities** — electricity, gas, water
* High-speed WiFi
* Community amenity access

One bundled bill is genuinely useful for a 1–2 month stay: no utility accounts to open and close.

**The caveat that costs real money: parking and housekeeping are optional ADD-ONS, not included.** This is a meaningful delta against [Express](./Express_Corporate_Housing.mdx) (parking included) and [Aplus](./Aplus_Housing.mdx) (cleaning included). **Parking in Bellevue is not cheap**, and on a monthly basis it can move the all-in number by a few hundred dollars. A quote that looks competitive before parking may not be competitive after.

Payment: major credit cards, checks, EFT, wire — with **"approved corporate clients"** language that hints at a business-account orientation.

**The honest verdict:** same warning as [National](./National_Corporate_Housing.mdx). No published rates + corporate-client framing usually means the price is set against an employer's relocation budget, not a retiree's $3,300. Call because of Eastgate/Somerset. Don't expect the number to work.

## The price math

**Nothing to compute — pricing is explicitly withheld.** Their own words: *"varies based on location, apartment size, and length of stay."*

What you *can* structure in advance is the arithmetic you'll do **on** the call, because the parking add-on means the quoted number is not the real number:

```
Real monthly  =  quoted rent
              +  parking (add-on, unpublished)
              +  housekeeping (add-on, unpublished, optional)
```

| | 30 days | 60 days |
|---|---|---|
| Quoted rent | *ask* | *ask* |
| **+ Parking** | *ask — this is the one that bites* | *ask* |
| + Housekeeping *(optional — can decline)* | *ask* | *ask* |
| **= Real all-in** | must be **≤ $3,300** | must be **≤ $6,600** |

Housekeeping is declinable. **Parking generally isn't**, if Tom has a car. Get it quoted as a line item before you evaluate anything else.

*No estimate is offered here because there is no published anchor to estimate from — not a nightly rate, not a band. For calibration, peers in this category run $3,000–4,500 ([Blu](./Blu_Corporate_Housing.mdx), published) up to $5,550+ ([Aplus](./Aplus_Housing.mdx), derived from nightly).*

## When you call

**1-800-960-0102.**

Ask for exactly this:

1. **A studio or 1BR in Eastgate or Somerset** — name the neighborhoods. This is the entire reason you're calling, and it's on their own page. Don't let it drift to Downtown Bellevue.
2. **30 to 60 days.** Their site says terms flex "depending on availability and client needs" — find out what that means concretely for a 1-month stay.
3. **Get parking quoted as a LINE ITEM, up front.** Do not accept a bundled "all-in" until parking is broken out. It is an add-on, it is unpublished, and Bellevue parking is expensive.
4. **Decline housekeeping** if it's priced separately — it's optional and Tom doesn't need it for 60 days.
5. **Confirm utilities**: your site says electricity, gas, water. Is anything else billed separately?
6. **Pets** — you say pet-friendly at many properties. Is this one? What's the fee?
7. Say plainly: **individual, paying out of pocket, no corporate account.** The "approved corporate clients" language on their payment page suggests this matters to them. Find out early.
