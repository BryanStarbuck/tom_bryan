## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The coverage is genuinely excellent — better than almost anyone else here.** National explicitly markets **Bellevue, Redmond, and Kirkland** for campus programs, plus Kirkland/Totem Lake, Downtown Bellevue, Redmond, Renton, Belltown, and South Lake Union. That is Tom's exact target geography, named directly, not inferred from a "Seattle" page.

They also claim **"boots on the ground" Seattle staff** and 24/7 service — meaning local people, not just a Denver call center pointed at a map. Their pitch is "hotel-level consistency, at-home comfort," and notably they'll **pivot to hotels** when apartments don't fit the requirement. Utilities and WiFi included; whole private apartments.

**So why is this a low-probability fit?**

Because of what's missing: **no pricing, no minimum stay, no local phone number.** There is a Contact Us form and nothing else. The only address anywhere on the site is corporate HQ in **Greenwood Village, Colorado**.

Here's the honest read: **firms that hide pricing behind a corporate intake form are pricing to an employer's relocation budget, not a retiree's $3,300.** Their business is built around corporate relocation accounts and employer-paid programs. In that world, the customer is a company's HR department placing dozens of people a year, and the price is whatever the relocation policy allows.

An individual booking one 1–2 month stay is **their least attractive customer**, and the quotes will reflect that. Expect to be asked *what company is paying* — and expect the conversation to cool when the answer is "nobody, it's my dad."

The lack of a phone number compounds it. For a senior, a web form with no human on the other end is the wrong front door.

## How to book

**There is no phone number.** The only path is the **Contact Us form** at https://www.nationalcorporatehousing.com/.

**Submit it for completeness — it costs nothing but five minutes.** Just don't wait on it.

If you do:

1. **Studio or 1BR**, in **Redmond or East Bellevue**, for **30–60 days**.
2. State up front: **this is an individual paying out of pocket, not a corporate relocation.** Get the disqualifying conversation over with immediately rather than three emails in.
3. Ask for the **shortest term they'll actually write** — it isn't published, and 30 days is only a convention.
4. Ask for an **all-in monthly number** including parking. Parking is unpublished.
5. Ask for a **local Seattle contact** — they claim boots on the ground; find out if that's true.
6. They pivot to hotels when apartments don't fit. If they offer that, note that this search already covers the extended-stay hotels directly and more cheaply.

**Deprioritize this behind [Blu](./Blu_Corporate_Housing.mdx) and [Between Moves](./Between_Moves.mdx).** Both are more likely to produce a usable number, and both have a human who answers a phone.
