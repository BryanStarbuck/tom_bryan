## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is perfect, and that's genuinely rare.** MorningStar **advertises "Low Commitment - Month
to Month"** outright — it's a marketed feature, not something you negotiate for. This is the **second
confirmed month-to-month senior option** in the entire search, alongside
[Merrill Gardens](./Merrill_Gardens_at_Kirkland.mdx). Set against the overview's core finding — that
**no conventional apartment building in Redmond, Bellevue, or Kirkland offers true month-to-month** —
that alone earns this page.

**Privacy and format are right.** 55+ independent living, **152 apartments**, studio through 2BR,
475–979 sqft. Tom gets his own apartment. No roommates, no cohabitation. Utilities included and
furnished, so no furniture rental and no utility setup for a 60-day stay — the same structural
advantage that makes Merrill Gardens attractive.

**Inventory depth is a real advantage over Merrill Gardens.** 152 apartments versus a community where
availability is simply "call to confirm." If Tom's dates are awkward, MorningStar is more likely to
have something open. That is precisely why this is the fallback rather than a scratch.

**But the price breaks budget at the floor, and that's decisive.** **$3,750 is the starting point** —
**$450/month over the $3,300 ceiling** before anything else is added. This isn't a case where a small
unit sneaks under; the cheapest thing they have is already over. The top of the range is **$5,400**,
from which the all-in figure is estimated. There's no configuration of this that fits.

**Distance — flag it.** **Totem Lake, ~16 minutes to Redmond.** Technically inside the 20-minute rule,
but Totem Lake is **north Kirkland, not South Kirkland**, which is the sub-area the charter names.
The overview flags Totem Lake properties as at the edge of the geography. Compare
[Merrill Gardens](./Merrill_Gardens_at_Kirkland.mdx) in downtown Kirkland at 15 minutes — closer,
more walkable, and in the right part of town.

**The honest read:** MorningStar does everything right except cost, and cost is a hard constraint.
**Merrill Gardens offers the same true month-to-month structure, the same all-in bundling, a better
neighborhood, and at least two of its three published price points clear budget.** Call Merrill
Gardens first. Call MorningStar if Merrill Gardens has nothing for Tom's dates — or if the tour goes
badly.

**Nothing clears.** Best case is **$450/month over — $900 across a 60-day stay.**

**The mitigating argument, stated fairly:** that $3,750 includes utilities and furnishings, so there's
no furniture rental, no delivery, no utility setup, and no lease to break. Against an unfurnished
apartment at $3,000 plus furniture plus utilities plus a term you can't actually get, $3,750 all-in is
not the outlier it looks like. **Whether MorningStar's rate also includes meals is not published in
our data — ask, because it materially changes this comparison** (Merrill Gardens' rate does include
meals).

**Still, the direct comparison decides it:**

| Option | 60-day total | Note |

**~$1,310 more across two months, for a worse location.** That's the case for the call order.

## When you call — 425-979-5997

**Call this number only after [Merrill Gardens](./Merrill_Gardens_at_Kirkland.mdx) (425-828-2570).**
If you do call:

1. **"What is the actual starting rate for a studio for a 1-month stay?"** $3,750 is the published
   floor — find out whether that's a real studio rate or an intro number attached to a longer
   commitment.
2. **"Are meals included at that rate?"** Not published in our data, and it's the single biggest
   factor in whether $3,750 is expensive or reasonable.
3. **"Is it truly month-to-month — no buy-in, no community fee, no move-in fee?"** The advertising
   says "Low Commitment." Get the exact terms.
4. **"What's included?"** Housekeeping frequency, which utilities, WiFi, **and parking** *(Unknown in
   our data — get the cost)*.
5. **"With 152 apartments, what's available for Tom's dates?"** This is MorningStar's advantage over
   Merrill Gardens — use it. If Merrill Gardens is full, ask this first.
6. **"Is there any flexibility on the rate for a 60-day stay?"** A community with 152 units and an
   empty studio has some room. It's a $450 gap, not a $2,000 one.
7. **"Can we tour it?"** Same as Merrill Gardens — a 55+ community is not a decision to make from a
   website.
