## Why this is a fit

**The term is exactly right, and almost nothing else in this search can say that.** A **true
month-to-month agreement with NO buy-in.** No lease to break, no 2-month minimum to negotiate around,
no unpublished short-term premium, no buy-in fee to recover. The overview's central finding is that
**no conventional apartment building in Redmond, Bellevue, or Kirkland offers true month-to-month** —
the shortest conventional terms found anywhere are 2 months. This place just says yes. That is the
single hardest requirement in the charter, and it's satisfied outright.

**The total cost is probably the lowest of anything real in this search — and that's the insight the
sticker price hides.** Meals, housekeeping, and utilities are all included. Follow what that
eliminates:

* **Zero furniture cost.** No CORT rental (which quotes $240–295/mo at the *12-month* rate — a 1–2
  month rental prices higher), no delivery, no pickup, no second move.
* **Zero utility setup.** No accounts opened and closed over 60 days.
* **Zero lease-break risk.** Nothing to exit when Tom finds his long-term place.
* **Meals included.** For a 60-day stay this is not a small line item — the alternative is groceries
  or restaurants, and for a senior alone, often restaurants.

Compare a "cheaper" unfurnished apartment: rent + furniture + utilities + a term he can't actually
get. **On all-in 60-day total, this likely wins.**

**Privacy is intact.** 55+ independent living means Tom gets **his own apartment** — studio or 1BR,
460–895 sqft. This is not assisted living and it is not shared housing. No roommates, no cohabitation.

**Location is good.** Downtown Kirkland, Moss Bay — walkable, 15 minutes to Redmond, comfortably
inside the 20-minute rule. South Kirkland is third on the charter's list, but this is a strong
version of it. Same neighborhood as [The 101](../../locations/apartment/The_101_Kirkland.mdx), the
most walkable building in the search.

## The honest con

**It is a senior living community, not a conventional apartment building — and some seniors find that
premature.** This needs saying plainly, because it's the only real objection and it's a legitimate
one. Tom would be moving into a 55+ community with a dining room and a housekeeping schedule, for two
months, while he looks for a normal apartment. For some people that's a comfortable, low-friction
soft landing. For others it feels like a step they weren't ready to take, and two months of it is two
months of feeling that way.

**That's Tom's call, not a spreadsheet's.** But it's worth being direct: everything measurable about
this option is favorable — term, total cost, privacy, location, zero setup — and the objection is
about how it feels. Worth asking him.

**PRICING IS CONTRADICTORY ACROSS SOURCES. Three different numbers appear: $2,595, $3,095, and
$3,995.** The CSV records $3,095–3,995 with confidence marked *conflicting*. **None of these is a

**Two of the three numbers clear budget.** And the comparison that matters is not rent-to-rent — it's
**all-in total to all-in total**:

| Option | 60-day all-in | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| **Merrill Gardens @ $3,095** | **~$6,190** | rent + meals + housekeeping + utilities + furniture |
| **[Blueground Redmond](../blueground/Blueground.mdx)** (est. mid) | ~$8,500 | rent + utilities + furniture (no meals) |
| **[Landing — Peloton](../platform/Landing_Peloton.mdx)** (published high) | ~$6,645 | rent + utilities + furniture (no meals) |

Even at the **$3,995** figure, once you subtract furniture rental, utility setup, and 60 days of food,
the gap narrows sharply. **At $2,595 or $3,095 it is likely the cheapest true 2-month total in the
entire search.**

**The spread between $2,595 and $3,995 is $1,400/month — $2,800 across a 60-day stay.** That is worth
one phone call more than anything else on this page.

## When you call — 425-828-2570

**This is one of the highest-value calls in the whole search. Resolve these, in order:**

1. **"What is the actual monthly rate for a studio, and for a 1BR?"** Three different numbers are
   published — $2,595, $3,095, $3,995. **Get the real one, in writing.** Ask what each tier
   corresponds to (unit size? floor? meal plan?) — the spread probably has a logic to it.
2. **"Is it truly month-to-month with no buy-in, no community fee, and no move-in fee?"** Confirm
   there is no one-time entrance cost. "No buy-in" is the documented claim — verify it.
3. **"What exactly is included at that rate?"** How many meals per day? How often is housekeeping?
   Which utilities? Is WiFi included? Is parking included, and what does it cost? *(Parking is
   Unknown in our data.)*
4. **"Which address is correct — 201 Kirkland Ave or 14 Main St S?"** Both are listed for this
   community. Get the right one before Tom drives there.
5. **"What's available for Tom's dates, in a studio and in a 1BR?"** 460–895 sqft is a wide range —
   find out what he'd actually get.
6. **"Is there any minimum stay, and what notice do I give to leave?"** The whole point is leaving
   cleanly when he finds a long-term place.
7. **"Can we tour it?"** Given the honest con above, seeing it is what settles whether this feels
   right to Tom. This is not a decision to make from a website.
