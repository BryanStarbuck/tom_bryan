## What it is and how it works

Apartment List is structured as a **matching service**, not a listing board. You answer an
onboarding quiz — budget, beds, move-in date, lease length — and it presents "matches" and
routes your contact information to properties.

That design choice has a direct consequence for this search: **the month-to-month filter is
not in the search UI. It lives in the quiz.** You cannot construct a URL that expresses
"month-to-month in Redmond" and hand it to someone. There is no shareable filtered link.
Every user has to walk the funnel themselves, which is the point — the funnel is the product.

It claims **116 short-term Bellevue apartments, $1,350–22,000**. That $22,000 ceiling should
tell you how loosely the category is drawn.

Worth noting: several individual buildings in this search are sourced from Apartment List
property pages, and those pages are useful. It is the **short-term search** that fails, not
the site's building data.

## How to search it effectively

```
https://www.apartmentlist.com/wa/bellevue/c/short-term-apartments-for-rent
```

Realistically there is no efficient path here:

* **You cannot build a filtered URL.** The lease-length preference is quiz-only.
* **You will hand over contact details** to get through the funnel.
* **Expect follow-up calls.** This is a lead-gen business. Once Tom's number is in, properties
  and the platform will call. For a senior, that is a real cost, not a minor annoyance — it
  also means more unsolicited inbound to sort real from predatory.

If you use it at all, use the **individual property pages** it hosts, which carry useful lease
terms and pricing. Skip the short-term category page.

## Does the filter actually work?

**No — and the evidence is on the site's own page.**

**Listings ON the short-term page still display 12- and 13-month terms.** That is not a
subtle inference. A page that promises short-term apartments is showing year-long leases.
The label is applied to inventory that contradicts it.

**What that means:** the "short-term" categorization is **marketing, not data**. There is no
structured lease-length field being queried. The category exists to capture search traffic
from people typing "short term apartments Bellevue" — people like Bryan — and route them into
the lead funnel. The **116 claimed short-term Bellevue apartments** cannot be taken at face
value.

Compare across this category:

Apartment List is near the bottom of that ranking, and it is the only one that also **costs
