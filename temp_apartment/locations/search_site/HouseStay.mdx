## What it is and how it works

A small **furnished-monthly aggregator** covering Bellevue, Kirkland, and Redmond.

**What makes it different from everything else in this category:** there is nothing to filter.
The entire site *is* furnished monthly rentals. You are not asking a general rental database
to find the short-term needle — you are browsing a database where every listing is the thing
you want.

After the [Zillow keyword filter that returns "no month-to-month" listings](./Zillow.mdx), the
[Apartment List short-term page showing 12-month terms](./Apartment_List.mdx), and the
[ForRent count that multiplies buildings by floor plans](./RentPath_Sites.mdx), a site where
the listings simply *are* what they claim is genuinely refreshing.

**The catch is inventory depth.** The CSV marks Eastside availability as **thin**. This is a
Seattle-proper site with suburban spillover — the Eastside is not its market, it is its
overflow. And the price range starts at an estimated **$2,500**, meaning what little exists
sits in the upper half of budget.

**Budget ten minutes.** Check all three cities, note anything under $3,300, move on. The thin
inventory means there is no long tail to dig through — you will exhaust it quickly, which is
itself a kind of efficiency.

## Does the filter actually work?

**There is no filter, and that is the highest compliment available in this category.**

The question this page series asks — *"does the month-to-month filter actually work?"* — does
not apply. HouseStay does not bolt a short-term filter onto a general rental database. Its
entire catalog is furnished monthly rentals, so the failure mode that plagues every other site
here **cannot occur**:

| Site | Failure mode |
|---|---|
| [Zillow](./Zillow.mdx) | Keyword match returns listings saying "no month-to-month" |
| [Apartment List](./Apartment_List.mdx) | Short-term page shows 12–13 month terms |
| [RentPath](./RentPath_Sites.mdx) | 977 "results" = buildings × floor plans |
| [Apartments.com](./Apartments_com.mdx) | Property-level flag; means 3–6 months anyway |
| [HotPads](./HotPads.mdx) | No lease filter exists at all |
| **HouseStay** | **None — everything shown is genuinely monthly and furnished** |

**So the honest verdict:** perfect precision, poor recall. Everything it returns is real;
it just does not return much. That is the exact inverse of
[liv.rent](./liv_rent.mdx) — a perfect filter over an empty database — and strictly better,
because a few real listings beat zero.
