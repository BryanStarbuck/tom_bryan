## What it is and how it works

This page covers Craigslist **as a search tool**. For the fraud analysis and the two
confirmed live scams, see
**[Craigslist — Eastside](../sublet/Craigslist_Eastside.mdx)** — read that page too, not
instead.

**The `est` subarea is exactly our geography.** That is the single best thing about this
channel. Where [Zillow](./Zillow.mdx) and [Apartments.com](./Apartments_com.mdx) make you
fight city boundaries, Craigslist's East subarea is Bellevue, Redmond, Kirkland, Bothell, and
Issaquah — the target zone, pre-drawn.

The section split is what protects Tom's privacy requirement:

* **`est/apa`** — apartments and housing. **Whole units. Use this.**
* **`est/roo`** — rooms and shares. **Never.** Every listing is a roommate situation.

**Listings turn over in hours.** This channel rewards **daily scanning** and punishes weekly
checks. Anything good is gone by the weekend.

## How to search it effectively

Whole units, right geography:

```
https://seattle.craigslist.org/search/est/apa
```

There is **no lease-length filter**. Search these keywords in the box:

* `month-to-month`
* `sublet`
* `short term`
* `furnished`

**But keyword search is narrow — here is the test that proves it.** A postal-radius search
(ZIP **98052**, 5-mile radius, keyword **"month-to-month"**) returned **exactly 1 listing, at
$2,240**. One. That is not a market — that is a keyword failing to match.

The reason: private landlords write ads in plain language. They write "flexible on term,"
"short lease OK," "available through September," or say nothing about term at all and wait
for you to ask. None of those match "month-to-month."

**So: browse `/search/est/apa` and scan manually.** Set a price ceiling and read the listings.
Do not rely on keywords to do the filtering — they will hide the inventory you want. The
keyword searches are worth running as a supplement, never as the primary method.

## Does the filter actually work?

**There is no filter. That is the honest answer, and Craigslist does not pretend otherwise.**

That is worth something. Craigslist offers no lease-length filter and **makes no claim to
have one** — unlike [Zillow](./Zillow.mdx), whose keyword filter silently returns listings
that say "no month-to-month," or [Apartment List](./Apartment_List.mdx), whose short-term page
shows 12-month leases. **A missing filter you know about is safer than a broken filter you
trust.**

What Craigslist has instead:

| | |
|---|---|
| **Geography** | `est` = exactly our target cities. Best in the search. |
| **Privacy** | `apa` vs `roo` is a clean whole-unit / roommate split |
| **Price** | Cheapest surface; the private-owner inventory that flexes on term |
| **Term filter** | **None.** Manual scanning is the method. |
| **Fraud** | **Highest exposure of any search site.** See the linked page. |

The trade is explicit: you accept manual scanning and real fraud risk in exchange for the
private-landlord inventory that no managed-building database contains.
