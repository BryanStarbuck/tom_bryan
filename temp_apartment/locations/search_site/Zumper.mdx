## What it is and how it works

A mainstream rental search site whose distinguishing feature is that **its short-term filter
is a genuine structured field under Lease Length** — not a text search against listing
descriptions. That sounds like a small distinction. It is the single most important
difference between this site and [Zillow](./Zillow.mdx), where the filter matches keywords
and returns listings that explicitly say *"no month-to-month."*

## How to search it effectively

City-level short-term searches:

```
https://www.zumper.com/apartments-for-rent/redmond-wa/short-term
https://www.zumper.com/apartments-for-rent/bellevue-wa/short-term
https://www.zumper.com/apartments-for-rent/kirkland-wa/short-term
```

**Neighborhood sub-paths work** — genuinely useful, since East Bellevue is a target area and
most sites treat Bellevue as one undifferentiated blob:

```
https://www.zumper.com/apartments-for-rent/bellevue-wa/bel-red/short-term
https://www.zumper.com/apartments-for-rent/bellevue-wa/southeast-bellevue/short-term
```

Filter path in the UI: **Lease Length → Short term**.

## Does the filter actually work?

**Yes — and it is the only mainstream site where that is true.**

The Short term option is a real structured field. That is why this page recommends starting
here and why the results are worth treating as leads rather than noise. Compare:

* [Zillow's](./Zillow.mdx) `_att` filters are **keyword matches** against description text.
* [Apartment List's](./Apartment_List.mdx) short-term page still shows **12- and 13-month
  terms**.
* [HotPads](./HotPads.mdx) has **no lease-length filter at all**.

**Two caveats:**

1. **The 25-result caps look like hard limits.** Exactly 25 in each of three cities is not a
   coincidence — it is a cap. You are likely seeing a truncated set, not the full market.
2. **Some listings are syndicated duplicates of [Apartments.com](./Apartments_com.mdx).** The
   same unit can appear on both. Do not count it twice.

And the standing caveat for every site in this category: "short term" on a listing site
usually means **3–6 months**, not the 1–2 Tom needs. Even a working filter cannot conjure
inventory that does not exist. These are a **call list**, not an answer.
