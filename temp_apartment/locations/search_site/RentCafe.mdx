## What it is and how it works

**RentCafe is owned by Yardi**, the property-management software company. This is the
governing fact: **the site only shows buildings that use Yardi's software.**

That is not a market. It is a **curated slice** — a vendor's customer list presented as a
search site. A building that runs on RealPage or Entrata simply does not exist here, no matter
how well it fits. Absence from RentCafe means nothing about a building; presence means only
that its manager buys Yardi.

**Named inventory found on the short-term list:**

* **The Regent at Bellevue Way**
* **Champagne at Bellevue**
* **Meydenbauer**

Each has its own page in this search — see the links below. The site is best understood as a
pointer to those buildings rather than a search tool in its own right.

## How to search it effectively

```
https://www.rentcafe.com/apartments-for-rent/bellevue-wa/short-term/
```

**The genuinely useful feature: the move-in date filter.** Most sites in this category treat
availability as an afterthought. RentCafe lets you filter by when a unit is actually
available, which matters — Tom's move-in date drives every quote in this search, and the
[overview](../../overview.mdx) flags it as the top open question.

Practical approach: run the short-term list, note the named buildings, then work them
individually via their own pages. Do not expect the site to surface anything the
building-level research has not already found.

## Does the filter actually work?

**The filter works. The inventory is the problem — and so is the floor.**

**Note the floor: $2,449.** That is the *bottom* of the Bellevue short-term range, before any
short-term premium is applied. Given that
[Apartments.com found short-term premiums of $300–800/mo](./Apartments_com.mdx), a $2,449
unit realistically lands at **$2,750–3,250**, and anything above ~$2,500 base breaks the
budget entirely. **Most of this site is at or above our ceiling once the premium applies.**

Compare that to [PadMapper](./PadMapper.mdx), where Redmond 1BRs cluster **$2,010–2,730** —
the same product, meaningfully cheaper, because PadMapper is not restricted to one vendor's
customer list.

Two further limits:

1. **The short-term flag has the same property-level ambiguity** as
   [Apartments.com](./Apartments_com.mdx) — it signals a building that *offers* short terms,
   not a 30-day unit available now. And "short term" in managed buildings means **3–6 months**,
   not the 1–2 Tom needs.
2. **The slice is Bellevue and Kirkland**, not Redmond — the preferred city is barely
   represented.

Net: a working filter over a vendor-limited, price-elevated slice. **Worth twenty minutes to
confirm the three named buildings. Not worth an hour.**
