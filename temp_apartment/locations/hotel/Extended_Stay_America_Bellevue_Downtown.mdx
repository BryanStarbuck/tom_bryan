## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

The **Premier** tier is the reason to look here. It is ESA's newer, better-finished product —
a step up from the standard ESA properties at [Factoria](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Factoria.mdx)
and [Overlake](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) — and it offers **1BR suites**, not
just studios. Over 60 days, a bedroom with a door is worth something.

On the room rate alone, budget is fine: **$2,340–$2,430/month estimated**, roughly $900 under
the ceiling, with a full kitchen and utilities included.

**Then there is the parking problem, and it is the whole story of this page.** The CSV records
parking as "on-site, confirm cost" and flags the risk explicitly: **downtown Bellevue routinely
charges $15–25/night, which is $450–750/month.** Add that to $2,430 and the top case is
$3,180 — still technically under $3,300, but with almost nothing left. Add it to a worse rate
and the property is out. Every other ESA on this list parks for free. **This one number decides
whether the page is a contender or a skip, and it is not published anywhere.**

Distance is the other weakness: **~20 minutes to Redmond**, tied with
[Larkspur](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) for the longest drive among the contenders, and
more than double the Overlake ESA's 9 minutes. Downtown Bellevue is also not East Bellevue —
it is outside the stated target sub-area, though Bellevue generally is in scope.

Senior-friendliness is **Unknown**; nothing was documented. Privacy is complete — a private
suite, no roommates.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** This is a nightly rate multiplied by 30 — the worst-case rate a
one-night tourist pays, sourced from Kayak
(https://www.kayak.com/Bellevue-Hotels-Extended-Stay-America---Seattle---Bellevue---Downtown.15442.ksp),
not a quote. **The real monthly quote will likely come in lower.**

* **30 nights, worst case, room only:** ~$78–81/night × 30 = **$2,340 – $2,430**.
* **60 nights, worst case, room only:** **$4,680 – $4,860 total**, or the same $2,340–$2,430/month.
* **30 nights with paid parking, worst case:** $2,430 + $750 = **$3,180/month** — $120 under
  the ceiling. That is not margin, that is a rounding error.
* **60 nights with paid parking, worst case:** **$6,360 total.**

So the honest read: the room is cheap, the parking might not be, and nobody has confirmed which.
Get the parking number first — it is the only figure that matters on this call.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. And ignore the 7-night discount
tier ESA advertises; it is the wrong product.

Worth asking here specifically: **does the exemption apply to the parking charge too, or only to
the room?** At a property where parking might be $750/month, that question has a real dollar
answer.

## When you call

**Call the front desk directly at 425-453-8186 — not the ESA 800 number.** Central reservations
cannot quote monthly. Ask for the General Manager.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front? **Does the exemption cover parking, or only the room?**
3. **This is the call. What does parking cost, per night and per month? Is there any long-stay
   parking rate?** Then: pet fee, and housekeeping frequency.
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit or one with grab bars? Nothing is
   documented about accessibility here.
5. Confirm the full kitchen has a **stove**, and confirm whether the Premier tier includes
   dishes and cookware in the room (standard ESA makes you ask at the desk).

Also ask what the 1BR suite costs versus the studio — at this tier the gap may be small.
