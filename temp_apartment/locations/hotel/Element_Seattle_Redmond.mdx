## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

The rack rate is absurd — **$8,550/month**, 159% over the ceiling. But unlike
[Aloft](./Aloft_Seattle_Redmond.mdx), which shares its building and is priced similarly, Element
is not a completeness-only page. **Element is Marriott's dedicated extended-stay brand.** Full
kitchen, studio and 1BR suites, free hot breakfast. It is built for exactly the stay Tom needs,
and like every extended-stay brand here, **its long-stay rates are unpublished** — the only
number visible online is the tourist number.

The location argument is the interesting one. It sits **steps from the Overlake Village light
rail station**, and the CSV flags that as real senior value: if driving becomes undesirable
during the stay, Tom is not stranded. It is in Redmond, zero drive, and it is rated
**senior-friendly: Yes**. That combination — full kitchen, transit, senior-friendly, in Redmond —
is rare on this list.

**Be realistic though.** Closing a 159% gap requires roughly a 60% discount off rack. Marriott's
standard long-stay discount is 20–30%. Even at 30% off, $8,550 becomes $5,985 — still 81% over.
The sales manager would have to be quoting an entirely different rate structure, not applying a
discount. That happens at extended-stay brands, but expecting it is optimism, not planning.

The honest framing: this is a **low-probability, low-cost call**. If
[Residence Inn Seattle Bellevue](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_Bellevue.mdx) and
[ESA Overlake](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) come back clean, do not bother. If
the Redmond-proper requirement is firm and
[WoodSpring](./WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) is full, make it.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate**, and it is a single number: **$285/night × 30 = $8,550/month**.
That is a rack rate scraped from a Kayak market listing
(https://www.kayak.com/Redmond-Hotels.22196.hotel.ksp) — the worst-case rate a one-night tourist
pays in a Microsoft market at summer peak. **It is not a quote and it is not what a 60-night stay
would cost.**

* **30 nights at rack:** $285 × 30 = **$8,550** — 159% over budget.
* **60 nights at rack:** $285 × 60 = **$17,100 total.**
* **With a 20–30% Marriott long-stay discount:** **$5,985 – $6,840/month.** Still 81–107% over.
* **What clearing budget requires:** a rate at or below **$110/night** — a **61% cut off rack.**
  That is not a discount, that is a different product. Ask whether one exists.
* **The tax exemption** then removes 12–15%, worth ~$500+/month at these rates — meaningful only
  if the base rate first comes down to earth.

The number to compare against: ESA Seattle-Redmond is ~9 minutes away, has a full kitchen, and
estimates at **$2,010–$2,520/month**. Element has to beat that by enough to justify the
difference, and it will not.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month** at typical rates, and considerably more at this one.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

**Call 425-636-9942 and ask for the sales manager** — the CSV specifically flags calling the
sales manager, not the front desk. Marriott.com will only ever show you rack.

1. **Element is an extended-stay brand — what is your actual extended-stay rate for 30 unbroken
   nights, and for 60?** Make clear you are not asking for a discount off the nightly rate; you
   are asking what the long-stay product costs.
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. **What does parking cost?** (Not published — and this is an Overlake Village transit-oriented
   building, where parking is often charged.) What is the pet fee? How often is housekeeping on
   an extended rate?
4. This property is rated senior-friendly — confirm. Elevator? Ground-floor unit? Grab bars?
5. Confirm the suite has a **full kitchen with a stove**, and that dishes and cookware are
   included.
