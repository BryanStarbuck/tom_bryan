## Why this is a fit

This is the closest true extended-stay product to Redmond. Despite the name it is physically
in Bellevue's Overlake neighborhood, but it sits on the Redmond border — **~9 minutes** — which
is less than half the drive from [Larkspur](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx). For a senior
who will be driving back and forth to look at long-term places in Redmond, that difference
compounds over 60 days.

The product is right. Studio suite, full kitchen with a two-burner stove, utilities included,
free parking, pets allowed, no lease, no income test. Tom walks in with suitcases.

On budget it has the most room of any hotel here. The estimate is **$2,010–$2,520/month** —
and that is the *pessimistic* number (see the math below). A real monthly quote should come in
at or below the bottom of that range, leaving $800–1,300/month of cushion against the $3,300
ceiling.

Two things to be honest about. **Housekeeping is only every other week** — that is the ESA
standard-tier trade-off, and over a 60-day stay it means three or four cleanings total. If
that is not acceptable for Tom, the Marriott extended-stay tier
([Residence Inn Seattle Bellevue](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_Bellevue.mdx)) cleans more often but
costs more. And **dishes are not in the room by default** at standard ESA properties — you ask
for the dish kit at check-in. Senior-friendliness is rated only "Partial"; the CSV records no
elevator or grab-bar detail, so ask.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** This number is a nightly rate multiplied by 30 — the worst-case
rate a one-night tourist pays, sourced from a Kayak listing
(https://www.kayak.com/Bellevue-Hotels-Extended-Stay-America---Seattle---Redmond.96717.ksp),
not from a quote. **The real monthly quote will very likely come in lower.** ESA deliberately
does not publish its 30+ night rate; the whole point of the phone call is to find out what it is.

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$67–84/night × 30 = **$2,010 – $2,520**. Under the $3,300
  ceiling even at the top.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$4,020 – $5,040**, or the same $2,010–$2,520/month.
* **Realistic expectation:** ESA's 30+ night tier is meaningfully below its nightly rate. If
  the quote comes back near $2,000/month all-in, that is the expected outcome, not a win.

There is no furniture cost, no utility deposit, no application fee, and no security deposit of
the kind an apartment would charge. The monthly number is close to the true all-in number —
which is not true of the unfurnished apartments, where you must add
[CORT furniture](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) and utility setup on top.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest
becomes "nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly
**12–15%** on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the
tax only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if
you contract 30+ nights up front. That gap is about **$350**, so ask which one this property
does.

**Never book 29 nights** — all the cost, none of the exemption. Note that ESA advertises a
7-night discount tier; do not let that anchor the conversation. Seven nights is the wrong
product. Ask for 30+.

## When you call

**Call the front desk directly at 425-885-6675 — do NOT use the ESA 800 number.** Central
reservations cannot quote a monthly rate; only the property can. Ask for the General Manager.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if
   I contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. What is the pet fee? **Housekeeping is listed as every
   other week — can that be increased, and at what cost?**
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit, or one with grab bars?
5. The suite lists a full kitchen with a two-burner stove — confirm. **And confirm I get a
   dish and cookware kit at check-in**, since it is not in the room by default.
