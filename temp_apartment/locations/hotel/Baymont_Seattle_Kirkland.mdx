## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

Baymont is a legitimate option that mostly loses to its own peers. It is a Wyndham brand, the
same parent as [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx), with a similar product
and a similar estimated price — but La Quinta is in **South Kirkland**, which is the target
sub-area, and is **11 minutes to Redmond** versus Baymont's 15. North Kirkland is neither the
target zone nor closer. That is the whole comparison: same thing, worse location.

What it does have is a decent amenity set for the money: **free breakfast, a seasonal heated
pool, guest laundry, free parking, utilities included.** Guest laundry is a real convenience
over a 60-day stay, and free breakfast is one meal a day Tom does not have to solve.

Budget nominally works — **$2,640–$3,300/month estimated** — but the top of that range is
exactly the ceiling, with nothing left over. And that estimate is a worst-case tourist number,
so the real quote should be lower.

**No full kitchen.** Microwave and fridge only, same as La Quinta. Over 60 days that means
restaurant meals, which is a cost the numbers above do not show. If a kitchen matters, this and
La Quinta are both out and the answer is [ESA](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx),
[WoodSpring](./WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx), or
[Larkspur](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx).

**The pet policy is not confirmed.** La Quinta takes pets free; nobody knows what Baymont does.
If Tom has a pet, that alone likely settles the choice between the two.

Privacy is complete — a private room, no roommates. Senior-friendliness is "Partial"; no
elevator or grab-bar detail is documented.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** This is a nightly rate multiplied by 30 — the worst-case rate a
one-night tourist pays, sourced from Yelp
(https://www.yelp.com/biz/baymont-by-wyndham-seattle-kirkland-wa-kirkland), not a quote. **The
real monthly quote will likely come in lower.**

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$88–110/night × 30 = **$2,640 – $3,300**. At the top, exactly the
  ceiling.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$5,280 – $6,600 total**, or the same $2,640–$3,300/month.
* **Realistic expectation:** lower. The CSV notes that **weekly rates are referenced but never
  published** — which is the tell that a discounted long-stay tier exists and is simply hidden.
  It is a franchise; the owner can price however they want.

**Add the food cost** of two months with no stove before comparing this to a full-kitchen
property. It is not a small adjustment.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**. On a worst case that sits exactly at $3,300, this
exemption is the only thing creating margin.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. No minimum term is published here,
so nothing steers you to 30 — ask for it explicitly.

## When you call

**Call the property at 425-822-2300** and ask for the General Manager or owner. Wyndham's
central line cannot approve a monthly rate; a franchise GM can.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights? **Weekly rates are referenced but
   never published — what is the actual long-stay tier?**
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. **What is the pet policy and fee? This is unconfirmed
   and it matters.** How often is housekeeping?
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit, or one with grab bars?
5. **Is there any unit with a stove — a suite, a kitchenette, anything — or is every room
   microwave-and-fridge only?** If a kitchenette exists, price that.

Also worth confirming: is the heated pool actually open during Tom's dates (it is seasonal), and
is guest laundry free or coin-operated?
