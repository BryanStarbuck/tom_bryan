## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

South Kirkland is the third location preference, and this is **the only real hotel physically in
it at a price Tom can pay.** Everything else in that zone is either an apartment with a 6- or
12-month lease or [The Woodmark](./The_Woodmark_Hotel.mdx) at three to five times budget. If
South Kirkland is where Tom wants to be, this page is the answer by default.

It is also the **shortest Kirkland drive to Redmond at ~11 minutes** — better than
[Quality Inn Kirkland](./Quality_Inn_Kirkland.mdx) at 16 or [Baymont](./Baymont_Seattle_Kirkland.mdx)
at 15, and better than [Larkspur](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) at 20.

**Pets stay free, two maximum.** That is not a small thing. Larkspur charges a pet fee that
the overview pegs at $25/night — **$750/month**, which would wipe out its budget advantage
entirely. If Tom has a pet, La Quinta's economics get dramatically better relative to the field.

Budget works, with less margin than the ESA properties: $2,400–$3,000/month estimated, which
clears $3,300 but only by $300–900 at worst case. Utilities included, free parking.

**The problem is the kitchen — and it is a serious one for a 60-day stay.** There is no stove.
Microwave and mini-fridge only. Over two months that pushes Tom toward restaurant meals or
microwave food, which is both a cost the table above does not show and a quality-of-life
question for a senior. Budget roughly what eating out adds and the gap to a full-kitchen
property like [ESA Seattle-Redmond](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) narrows or
closes. **The overview flags this as an open question: does Tom need a full kitchen? If yes,
this page is out, no matter how good the location is.**

The minimum term is also unpublished, and senior-friendliness is only "Partial" — no elevator
or grab-bar detail is documented.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** This is a nightly rate multiplied by 30 — the worst-case rate a
one-night tourist pays, sourced from Yelp
(https://www.yelp.com/biz/la-quinta-by-wyndham-seattle-bellevue-kirkland-kirkland), not from a
quote. **The real monthly quote will likely come in lower.**

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$80–100/night × 30 = **$2,400 – $3,000**. Clears $3,300 by
  $300–900.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$4,800 – $6,000 total**, or the same $2,400–$3,000/month.
* **Realistic expectation:** lower, once the 30-night tax exemption and any negotiated
  long-stay rate apply. La Quinta is a Wyndham franchise, and franchisees can discount at the
  property level — the person answering that phone may actually have authority.

**Add the food math.** No stove over 60 days is a real recurring cost that does not appear in
any of the numbers above. Compare honestly against a full-kitchen property before deciding this
one is cheaper.

**Subtract the pet math.** If Tom has a pet, pets are free here and $750/month at Larkspur.
That single line can reverse the ranking.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**. On a $3,000/month estimate here, that is real money
against a $300 cushion.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the
tax only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. This property publishes no
minimum term at all, so nothing stops you from booking 30 — but nothing prompts you to, either.
You have to ask for it.

## When you call

**Call the property at 425-828-6585.** This is a franchise, so ask for the General Manager or
owner — the front desk cannot approve a long-stay rate, but the GM often can.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights? **Is there a long-stay or monthly
   rate at all?** (None is published.)
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free and **pets as free, 2 max — confirm both in writing.** How often is
   housekeeping?
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit, or one with grab bars?
5. **The key question: is there any unit with a full kitchen and a stove — a suite, anything —
   or is every room microwave-and-mini-fridge only?** If a kitchenette suite exists, price that,
   not the standard room.
