## Why this isn't a fit

There is nothing wrong with this property. Furnished studio, **full kitchen with cookware
included** (better than the standard ESA setup where you ask for dishes at the desk), utilities
included, free parking, pets allowed, no lease. At an estimated **$2,430–$3,600/month** the
bottom of the range clears budget comfortably.

**It fails on distance.** ~25 minutes to Redmond puts it **outside the 20-minute rule** — the one
hard geographic constraint in the charter. It is not in Redmond, East Bellevue, or South Kirkland,
and it is not close to any of them.

The point of this page is to make the trade explicit. If the 20-minute rule holds, this is out
and so is its [sister property in West Bothell](./Extended_Stay_America_Bothell_West.mdx) at 28
minutes. If the rule relaxes — and the overview flags that as an open question — then Bothell
opens up, and the property to call is **not this one**: it is
[Residence Inn Bothell](./Residence_Inn_Bothell.mdx) at 19 minutes with the most complete kitchen
in the entire search, at a comparable estimated price.

So this page is a fallback to a fallback. The nearest ESA that works is
[Factoria](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Factoria.mdx) at 18 minutes and an estimated
$1,710–$2,400 — **cheaper and closer.** That comparison is why this one does not get called.

* **The comparison that matters:** ESA Factoria estimates at **$1,710–$2,400** and is **7 minutes

* Ignore the 7-night discount tier ESA advertises — wrong product, and it does not reach the tax

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly 12–15%, or
$300–450/month. Note this is **Bothell**, a different jurisdiction — ask what the local lodging tax
rate actually is rather than assuming the Eastside figure. The clock requires **unbroken
occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward;
others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front —
a ~$350 difference, so ask which. **Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

Only if the 20-minute rule is relaxed — and even then, call
[Residence Inn Bothell](./Residence_Inn_Bothell.mdx) first. If you do call, use the front desk at
425-482-2900, **not the ESA 800 number**, which cannot quote monthly.

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days? What is
   Bothell's lodging tax rate?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. Pet fee? How often is housekeeping?
4. Elevator, ground-floor unit, grab bars?
5. Confirm the full kitchen has a **stove**, and that cookware is in the room.
