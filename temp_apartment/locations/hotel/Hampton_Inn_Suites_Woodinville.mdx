## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

This is a fallback and should be treated as one. It is not in the target geography — Woodinville
is neither Redmond, East Bellevue, nor South Kirkland — and at **~19 minutes** it sits right on
the edge of the 20-minute rule, the same drive as
[Residence Inn Bothell](./Residence_Inn_Bothell.mdx) but with a worse product.

Budget nominally works: **$2,700–$3,900/month estimated**, with free parking and utilities
included. But the top of that range is $600 over the ceiling, and the range is wide for a
reason — see below.

**Two specific problems make this weaker than its price suggests.**

**First, the kitchen is conditional.** The CSV is explicit: a kitchenette exists in **select
rooms only**. That is a booking hazard. If Tom books a "room" and arrives to find a microwave
and a mini-fridge, the entire 60-day food plan changes. **Get the kitchenette confirmed in
writing on the reservation** — not verbally, not "we'll try." If they will not put it on the
confirmation, assume it is not there.

**Second, Woodinville is a wine-tourism town.** That is not trivia — it is the pricing model.
Weekend rates spike because the wineries fill the town every Friday through Sunday. A
**continuous 30–60 night booking is genuinely harder here**, because the hotel makes more money
selling those weekends at premium than blocking them out for a long-stay guest. Expect
resistance, and expect the quoted monthly rate to be propped up by weekend value the hotel does
not want to give away.

Hampton is also **not an extended-stay brand**. Unlike Residence Inn, Element, or Hyatt House,
there is no long-stay product line to ask for — you are asking a select-service hotel to invent
one.

Pet policy is not published. Senior-friendliness is "Partial" — nothing documented.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** These are nightly rates multiplied by 30 — worst-case tourist
rates from TripAdvisor
(https://www.tripadvisor.com/Hotel_Review-g58835-d11880266-Reviews-Hampton_Inn_Suites_Seattle_Woodinville_WA-Woodinville_Washington.html),
not a quote. **The real monthly quote may come in lower — but less reliably here than elsewhere,
for the weekend-demand reason above.**

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$90–130/night × 30 = **$2,700 – $3,900**. The bottom clears
  $3,300 by $600; the top misses by $600.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$5,400 – $7,800 total**, or the same $2,700–$3,900/month.
* **Why the range is wide:** that spread is the weekday/weekend gap in a tourism town. A 30-night
  continuous stay includes **8–9 weekends** — the exact nights the hotel prices highest. A
  blended monthly rate here will be pulled toward the top of the range, not the bottom, which is
  the opposite of how most properties on this list behave.
* **Then the tax exemption** removes 12–15%, worth $325–475/month at these rates — which is what
  would have to bring a $3,900 quote back toward budget.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**,
or **$300–450/month**. Note this is **Woodinville**, a different jurisdiction from Redmond or
Bellevue — **ask what the actual local lodging tax rate is** rather than assuming.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. **This is the property
where that is hardest to secure**, because the hotel has a commercial incentive to keep weekends
free for wine tourists. If they propose anything that involves checking out and back in — for a
busy weekend, for a room change, for anything — **the exemption clock resets and the whole thing
is lost.** One reservation, one room, no exceptions.

Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the
first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

**Call the property at 425-788-9247** and ask for the General Manager. The Hilton 800 line will
not help.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights? **Specifically: will you hold one
   room for 30–60 unbroken nights including weekends, and at what rate?**
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front? What is Woodinville's lodging tax rate?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. **What is the pet policy and fee?** (Not published.) How
   often is housekeeping?
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor room, or one with grab bars?
5. **The critical one: the kitchenette is in select rooms only. Confirm a kitchenette room for
   the full stay, and get it written on the reservation confirmation.** Does it have a stove or
   only a microwave?
