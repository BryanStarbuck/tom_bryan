## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**This is the highest-leverage single phone call in the hotel category.** Redmond Inn is
**independent** — no franchise, no corporate rate structure, no revenue-management system. The
person who answers the phone can say yes to a 60-night deal on the spot. Every chain property
on this list has to route a monthly rate through a brand system or a GM's discretion within
brand rules. This one does not. If Tom's stay is 60 unbroken nights in the off-shoulder, an
independent owner filling two months of otherwise-empty inventory has every reason to negotiate
hard.

It is also **in Redmond** — zero drive, the top location preference — and is likely the cheapest
roof in Redmond proper.

**The kitchen is the problem.** Microwave and mini-fridge only. No stove. Over 60 days that
pushes a senior toward restaurant meals every day, which is both a real recurring cost that none
of the numbers below include and a quality-of-life question. The overview flags this exactly:
the full-kitchen question is what separates Redmond Inn and
[La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx) from the
[ESA](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) /
[WoodSpring](./WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) / [Larkspur](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx)
tier. **If Tom needs a kitchen, skip this and call WoodSpring — also in Redmond, full kitchen,
dishwasher, and documented accessibility.**

The published discount structure is also the wrong shape: 15% at 2 nights, 20% at 3+. That is a
weekend-getaway ladder, not a long-stay product. It tells you nothing about what 30 or 60 nights
costs, and it means the monthly conversation is one you have to start yourself.

Privacy is complete. Senior-friendliness is "Partial" — nothing documented about elevators or
grab bars.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate**, and this is the widest and least trustworthy range on any hotel
page here. The numbers are nightly rates multiplied by 30, sourced from Kayak
(https://www.kayak.com/Redmond-Hotels-Redmond-Inn.25024.ksp) — worst-case tourist rates, not
quotes.

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$69–169/night × 30 = **$2,070 – $5,070**. The bottom clears budget
  easily; the top is 54% over it.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$4,140 – $10,140 total**, or the same $2,070–$5,070/month.
* **What that spread actually means:** an independent hotel with heavy nightly rate volatility —
  cheap on a Tuesday in the shoulder, expensive on a summer weekend. **A 30- or 60-night
  continuous stay should be priced nowhere near the top of that range.** Do not accept a quote
  built from peak nightly rates; the correct frame for the owner is "what is your best flat
  monthly number for 60 unbroken nights."
* **Apply the published discount ladder and it still tells you nothing:** 20% off a $169 night
  is $135, or $4,050/month — still over budget. The ladder is not the product you want.

**Then add the food cost.** Two months with no stove is real money on top of every figure above.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption.

**Special note for an independent property:** a small independent owner may not have the
exemption process wired into their system the way a chain does — or may not know the rule well.
That cuts both ways. It could mean friction, or it could mean the owner is receptive to
structuring the booking correctly if you explain it. Cite the WAC by number. Get the answer in
writing on the reservation confirmation, not just verbally.

## When you call

**Call 425-883-4900 and ask for the owner or the manager — not a desk clerk.** This is the one
property in the category where the decision-maker is likely to be the person on the phone. Lead
with the value: 60 unbroken nights, one guest, no turnover, paid up front if that helps.

1. **What is your best flat all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?** (Ignore the 2- and
   3-night discount ladder — that is not what I want.)
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front? **I want that written on the confirmation.**
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. **What is the pet policy and fee?** (Not published.)
   How often is housekeeping, and is it included at a monthly rate?
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit, or one with grab bars?
5. **Is there any room with a stove or a kitchenette, or is every room microwave-and-mini-fridge
   only?** If the answer is no stove anywhere, this is probably the wrong property for a 60-day
   stay.
