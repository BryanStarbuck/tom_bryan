## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

At the number you can see, this is absurd: **$7,470/month**, more than double the ceiling. If
that were the real price this would be a completeness-only page like
[Seattle Marriott Redmond](./Seattle_Marriott_Redmond.mdx).

It is not a completeness-only page, for three reasons. **It is a true extended-stay brand** —
Hyatt House exists to sell long stays, and its sales departments quote 30+ night rates at a
fraction of rack. **It is in downtown Redmond and walkable** — the top location preference, zero
drive, and a senior can get around on foot. And it is one of the few properties in this whole
search rated **senior-friendly: Yes**, with a **full kitchen**.

So the product is close to ideal and only the price is wrong — which is the opposite of
[Motel 6](./Motel_6_North_Kirkland.mdx), where the price is right and everything else is wrong.
A price that is wrong by 126% is a lot to negotiate away, and honesty requires saying this
probably does not close. But the call is cheap and the upside is the best-located property Tom
could get.

**One warning that is specific to this property and appears nowhere else:** the CSV records that
**31+ nights triggers a fee at the hotel's discretion.** That is unusual and it is a red flag —
it reads like a policy designed to claw back what the tax exemption gives away. Get the terms of
that fee in writing before anything else. It may quietly undo the entire reason to book 30+
nights here.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate**, and this one is a single number, not a range: **$249/night × 30 =
$7,470/month**. That is a rack rate scraped from Kayak
(https://www.kayak.com/Redmond-Hotels-Hyatt-House-Redmond.300105.ksp) — the worst-case rate a
one-night tourist pays in a Microsoft-driven market at peak. **It is not a quote, and the real
extended-stay number should be dramatically lower.**

* **30 nights at rack:** $249 × 30 = **$7,470** — 126% over the $3,300 ceiling.
* **60 nights at rack:** $249 × 60 = **$14,940 total**, or the same $7,470/month.
* **What has to happen for this to work:** the sales rate has to come in at **$110/night or
  below** — a **56% cut off rack**. Extended-stay sales rates at 50–60% off rack do exist, but
  they are typically for corporate volume accounts, not a single retiree booking 60 nights. Set
  expectations accordingly.
* **Then the tax exemption:** 12–15% off, worth $400–500/month at this price point, which is
  what would make a $110/night number actually land under budget.
* **Then subtract nothing for the "31+ night fee"** — because nobody knows what it is. Until
  that is quantified, no math here is reliable.

Downtown Redmond has high Microsoft corporate demand propping up rates, which is precisely why
the rack rate is this high — and also why the hotel has less reason to discount. That is the
argument against optimism.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month** at typical rates, and more at this one.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption.

**And here, ask the sharper question:** the property's own listed policy is that **31+ nights
triggers a discretionary fee.** Ask directly whether that fee is designed to offset the lodging
tax exemption. If the answer is anything other than a clear no with a number attached, this
property is not serious about long stays regardless of what the brand says.

## When you call

**Call 425-497-2000 and ask for the Director of Sales.** The CSV is explicit — the front desk
cannot quote an extended-stay rate and will just repeat the $249 you already saw.

1. **I want your extended-stay rate for a single guest, 30 unbroken nights and 60 unbroken
   nights, all-in.** Not the rate on hyatt.com.
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front? **And what exactly is the fee your policy triggers at 31+
   nights? Put it in writing.**
3. What does covered parking cost — is it included? What is the pet fee? How often is
   housekeeping on an extended-stay rate?
4. This property is rated senior-friendly — confirm specifics. Elevator? Ground-floor unit
   available? Grab bars?
5. The suite lists a full kitchen — confirm it has a **stove**, and confirm dishes and cookware
   are included.
