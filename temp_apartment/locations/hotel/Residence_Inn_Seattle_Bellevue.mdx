## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Eight minutes to Redmond.** That is the shortest drive of any hotel here — shorter than
[ESA Seattle-Redmond](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) at 9, and less than half of
[Larkspur](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) at 20. For a stay whose entire purpose is
house-hunting in Redmond, that is a compounding advantage over 60 days.

The product is a genuine step above the ESA tier: full-kitchen suites, **free parking**, **free
hot breakfast**, and a **free Monday–Thursday evening reception with light dinner**. That last
one is not a gimmick over a 60-day stay — it is four meals a week Tom does not buy or cook, plus
somewhere to be around other people, which for a senior living alone in a hotel for two months
has value the spreadsheet does not capture.

**The price is the problem, and it is a solvable one.** Rack estimates at **$3,960–$4,170/month**
are $660–870 over the ceiling. But Marriott's 30+ night rates routinely discount **20–30%**,
which puts a realistic quote at roughly **$2,900–$3,200/month** — under budget, with the tax
exemption still on top of that. **This is the most winnable gap on the list:** the ask is a
standard discount that the brand already offers as a product, not a heroic favor.

Compare honestly to ESA Overlake: ESA estimates at $2,010–2,520 and needs no negotiation at all.
If the goal is simply the cheapest full-kitchen room near Redmond, ESA wins. If a better product
with breakfast, evening dinner, and more frequent housekeeping is worth ~$500–900/month, this is
where that money goes. Both calls are worth making; they answer different questions.

Senior-friendliness is "Partial" — nothing documented on elevators or grab bars.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** These are nightly rates multiplied by 30 — the worst-case rate a
one-night tourist pays, taken from Marriott's own overview page, not from a long-stay quote.
**The real monthly quote should come in materially lower.**

* **30 nights at rack, worst case:** ~$132–139/night × 30 = **$3,960 – $4,170**. Over budget by
  $660–870.
* **60 nights at rack, worst case:** **$7,920 – $8,340 total.**
* **With Marriott's standard 20–30% long-stay discount:**
  * 20% off $4,170 = **$3,336/month** — $36 over. Essentially at the line.
  * 30% off $3,960 = **$2,772/month** — $528 under.
  * So the realistic landing zone is **~$2,900–$3,200/month**, which is the number the CSV
    itself projects.
* **60 nights at the discounted rate:** roughly **$5,800 – $6,400 total.**
* **Then the tax exemption** takes another 12–15% off, worth ~$400/month at this price point —
  which turns a marginal quote into a comfortable one.

Free parking matters here in a way it does not at every property: it is what keeps
[ESA Bellevue Downtown](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Downtown.mdx) from being comparable,
since downtown parking there could add $450–750/month.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**. At this rate it is at the top of that range, ~$400–500.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. The listed minimum here is 1
night, so the booking engine will sell you 29 without a word. Do not use the booking engine.

## When you call

**Call 425-882-1222 and ask for the extended-stay sales manager or Director of Sales.**
Marriott.com and the central 800 line cannot quote a 30+ night rate — they will show you the
$139/night you already saw. The discount lives at the property.

1. **What is your 30+ night rate, all-in, for a single guest — for 30 nights and for 60
   nights?** Frame it as unbroken occupancy, one reservation.
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. What is the pet fee? **How often is housekeeping on a
   long-stay rate — does it get reduced?**
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit, or one with grab bars?
5. Confirm the suite has a **full kitchen with a stove**, and that dishes and cookware are in the
   room.

Also confirm: **is the free evening reception Monday–Thursday still running, and is breakfast
included at the long-stay rate?** Those are part of the value case for paying more than ESA.
