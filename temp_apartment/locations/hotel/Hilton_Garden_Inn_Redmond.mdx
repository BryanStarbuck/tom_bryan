## Why this isn't a fit

Downtown Redmond is the right place. Everything else is wrong.

**Not an extended-stay brand.** Hilton Garden Inn is select-service, built for two-night business
trips. There is no long-stay product line to ask for, and **no kitchen** — microwave and
mini-fridge only, which over 60 days means restaurant meals every day.

**At an estimated $8,340/month it is 153% over** the $3,300 ceiling. Even a generous 30% long-stay
discount lands at $5,838 — still 77% over, still with no stove.

Hilton's actual extended-stay brands (Homewood Suites, Home2 Suites) are the products that would
fit; neither turned up in this search's Redmond geography. On the Hilton side, the closest thing
found is [Hampton Inn & Suites Woodinville](./Hampton_Inn_Suites_Woodinville.mdx), which has a
kitchenette in select rooms — and is 19 minutes out.

Skip. If Redmond proper is required, call [WoodSpring](./WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx).

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

For completeness: under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166),
a guest becomes "nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth
roughly 12–15% on the Eastside, or $300–450/month at typical rates. The clock requires **unbroken
occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward;
others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front —
a ~$350 difference, so ask which. **Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

Don't. If you do, call 425-448-7660 direct (not the Hilton 800 line) and ask for the Director of
Sales:

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. Parking cost? Pet fee? (Neither published.) Housekeeping frequency?
4. Elevator, ground-floor room, grab bars?
5. Is there a stove or kitchenette in any room? (Expect: no — microwave and mini-fridge only.)
