## Why this isn't a fit

This page exists for completeness and for one genuinely interesting fact: **Archer is the only
hotel in Redmond with a published long-stay page.** That is worth knowing, because it means the
concept of a 30+ night stay is not foreign to them.

Everything else is wrong. At an estimated **$9,930/month** it is roughly **three times the
$3,300 ceiling** — a gap no discount closes. And even if the price collapsed, the kitchen would
kill it: the CSV is explicit that it is a **wet bar** — mini-fridge and microwave, **no stove**.
For a 60-day stay that is the same failure as [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx),
except at four times the price. Parking is also a paid garage on top.

It is a luxury boutique hotel in Redmond Town Center. It is very nice. It is not what this search
is for. **Skip unless the budget changes fundamentally.**

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** $331/night × 30 = **$9,930/month** — a rack rate from Archer's own
long-stay page, i.e. the worst-case tourist number. The real 30+ night quote would come in lower,
but the gap is 201%: reaching $3,300 requires a **67% cut**. That does not happen.

* **30 nights at rack:** **$9,930** — $6,630 over budget.
* **60 nights at rack:** **$19,860 total.**

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly 12–15% on
the Eastside, or $300–450/month at normal rates (far more here). The clock requires **unbroken
occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward;
others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front —
a ~$350 difference, so ask which. **Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.
Note Archer's published long-stay tier starts at **7 nights**, which is well short of the
exemption — do not let that tier anchor the conversation.

## When you call

Don't, unless the budget changes. If you do, call 425-658-3000 direct (the CSV notes 30+ nights
requires calling direct) and ask for the Director of Sales.

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. What does garage parking cost per month? Pet fee? Housekeeping frequency?
4. Elevator, ground-floor room, grab bars?
5. **Is there any unit with a stove, or is every room a wet bar?** (Expect: wet bar.)
