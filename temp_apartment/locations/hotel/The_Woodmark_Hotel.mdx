## Why this isn't a fit

The location is perfect. Lakefront Carillon Point is exactly the South Kirkland zone, ~14
minutes to Redmond, walkable, beautiful, and the kind of place a senior would genuinely enjoy
spending two months. **It is the best location match in the whole search.**

And it is **$9,000–$15,000/month** — between **173% and 355% over** the $3,300 ceiling. Nothing
bridges that. No sales manager, no tax exemption, no shoulder season.

This page is here so that nobody spends an afternoon rediscovering the Woodmark, getting excited
about Carillon Point, and calling. The answer is already known. If South Kirkland is the
priority, the real options are [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx) — the only
hotel physically in South Kirkland at budget — or the apartment side, where
[Kirkland Crossing](../apartment/Kirkland_Crossing.mdx) and
[The Carillon Apartments](../apartment/The_Carillon_Apartments.mdx) sit in the same geography at
a payable rent.

Kitchen and parking are both unpublished, which is academic at this price.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate**, sourced from an Allstays directory listing
(https://www.allstays.com/us-washington-hotels/kirkland-hotels.htm) — nightly rack rates
multiplied by 30, the worst-case tourist number.

* **30 nights at rack:** ~$300–500/night × 30 = **$9,000 – $15,000** — $5,700 to $11,700 over.
* **60 nights at rack:** **$18,000 – $30,000 total.**
* **What clearing budget would require:** a **63–78% cut** off rack. Luxury lakefront resorts do
  not discount that far for one guest.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

For completeness, since it applies here as everywhere: under
[WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly 12–15%,
or $300–450/month at typical Eastside rates. The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one
reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax from day 30 forward; others refund it
**retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ up front — a ~$350 difference.
**Never book 29 nights.** At this property a 15% exemption is worth over $1,000/month and still
leaves the rate roughly triple budget, which is the clearest possible illustration that the
exemption cannot rescue a bad starting price.

## When you call

Don't. If the budget ever changes fundamentally, call 425-822-3700 and ask for the Director of
Sales.

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. Parking cost? Pet fee? Housekeeping frequency?
4. Elevator, ground-floor room, grab bars?
5. Is there a full kitchen with a stove in any unit, or only a mini-fridge and microwave?
