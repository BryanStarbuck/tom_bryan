## Why this isn't a fit

**This is the farthest ESA in the search at ~28 minutes to Redmond** — eight minutes past the
20-minute rule, and nearly triple the drive from
[ESA Seattle-Redmond](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) at 9. West Bothell is not in
or near any of the three target areas.

The product itself is standard ESA and perfectly fine: furnished, full kitchen, utilities
included, free parking, pets, no lease, estimated $2,430–$3,600/month.

But it loses to its own siblings on every axis at once. [ESA Factoria](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Factoria.mdx)
is estimated **$1,710–$2,400 and 10 minutes closer**. Even
[ESA Bothell Canyon Park](./Extended_Stay_America_Bothell_Canyon_Park.mdx), which is itself
outside the rule, is 3 minutes closer at the same price. And if the 20-minute rule is relaxed to
open up Bothell, the property to call is [Residence Inn Bothell](./Residence_Inn_Bothell.mdx) at
19 minutes with a far better kitchen.

**There is no scenario where this is the right call.** It is documented so the map is complete
and nobody re-derives it.

One more practical note: the only phone number on record is an **800 number**, not a local front
desk. Central reservations cannot quote a monthly rate — which means even calling this property
requires first finding a direct line that the research did not turn up.

* **60 nights, worst case:** **$4,860 – $7,200 total**, or the same $2,430–$3,600/month.
* **The comparison that ends it:** ESA Factoria at **$1,710–$2,400** and **18 minutes** — up to

Ignore the 7-night discount tier — wrong product, and it does not reach the tax exemption.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly 12–15%, or
$300–450/month. This is **Bothell**, a different jurisdiction from Redmond or Bellevue — ask what
the local rate actually is. The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no
checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively**
across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front — a ~$350 difference, so ask which.
**Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

Don't. Call [ESA Factoria](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Factoria.mdx) or
[Residence Inn Bothell](./Residence_Inn_Bothell.mdx) instead.

If you somehow need to: **the 800-804-3724 number on file is central reservations and cannot quote
a monthly rate.** Find the property's direct front desk line first, then ask for the GM:

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days? What is
   Bothell's lodging tax rate?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. Pet fee? How often is housekeeping?
4. Elevator, ground-floor unit, grab bars?
5. Confirm the full kitchen has a **stove**, and ask for a dish and cookware kit at check-in.
