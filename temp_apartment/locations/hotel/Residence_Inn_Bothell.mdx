## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

This is the Residence Inn that can actually work. Same brand and same product as the
[Redmond](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_East_Redmond.mdx) and
[Bellevue/Downtown](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_Bellevue_Downtown.mdx) properties, but at
**$2,700–$4,200/month estimated** instead of $4,410–$8,850 — and the bottom of that range is
already under budget **before any negotiation at all.**

The reason is simple and worth stating plainly: **Bothell prices below Redmond because Bothell
does not have Microsoft.** The building is not worse. The market is cheaper. That is the entire
arbitrage.

The product is the best in the category on the one dimension that matters most for a 60-day
stay. **This is the most complete kitchen of any option in the entire search** — dishwasher,
oven, *and* stovetop. Not a two-burner hot plate like
[ESA](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx), not a "kitchen suite, no oven" like
[Hyatt House Bellevue](./Hyatt_House_Seattle_Bellevue.mdx), and certainly not the microwave and
mini-fridge at [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx). A real kitchen. Over two
months, Tom cooks normally.

It is also **newly renovated**, carries an **8.6 rating — among the highest of anything found**,
includes **free hot breakfast**, and is rated **senior-friendly: Yes**.

**The cost is distance: ~19 minutes to Redmond.** That is inside the stated 20-minute rule, but
only just — and it is functionally the same drive as
[Larkspur](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) (20 min) while being further outside the target
geography. Bothell is not Redmond, East Bellevue, or South Kirkland. The overview flags this
directly as an open question: *how much does the 20-minute Redmond drive really matter?* If the
answer is "not much," this property gets a lot more attractive, and so do the
[Bothell ESAs](./Extended_Stay_America_Bothell_Canyon_Park.mdx). If the answer is "a lot," then
ESA Overlake at 9 minutes wins on geography even though its kitchen is weaker.

Parking cost is not published — confirm.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** These are nightly rates multiplied by 30 — worst-case tourist
rates from a Yelp listing
(https://www.yelp.com/biz/residence-inn-seattle-northeast-bothell-bothell), not a quote. **The
real long-stay quote should come in lower.**

* **30 nights at rack, worst case:** ~$90–140/night × 30 = **$2,700 – $4,200**. The bottom
  clears $3,300 by $600; the top misses by $900.
* **60 nights at rack, worst case:** **$5,400 – $8,400 total**, or the same $2,700–$4,200/month.
* **With Marriott's standard 20–30% long-stay discount:**
  * 20% off $4,200 = **$3,360/month** — $60 over. Basically at the line.
  * 30% off $4,200 = **$2,940/month** — under.
  * 20–30% off $2,700 = **$1,890–$2,160/month** — far under.
  * So the realistic landing zone is roughly **$1,900–$3,000/month.** **This clears budget under
    almost every combination**, which is not true of any other Residence Inn here.
* **Then the tax exemption** takes another 12–15% off — call it $300–450/month.

The unstated cost is fuel and time on a 19-minute drive, twice a day, for 60 days. That is a
real number too, and it partially offsets the savings versus a Redmond property.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**,
or **$300–450/month**. Note this property is in **Bothell**, not Bellevue or Redmond — the local
lodging tax rate differs by jurisdiction, so **ask what the actual tax percentage is here** rather
than assuming the Eastside 12–15% figure.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

**Call 425-485-3030 and ask for the extended-stay sales manager.** The Marriott 800 line cannot
quote a monthly rate.

1. **What is your 30+ night rate, all-in, for a single guest — 30 nights and 60 nights?**
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front? **What is the lodging tax rate in Bothell?**
3. **What does parking cost?** (Not published.) What is the pet fee? How often is housekeeping on
   a long-stay rate?
4. This property is rated senior-friendly — confirm. Elevator? Ground-floor suite? Grab bars?
5. Confirm the suite kitchen has the **oven, stovetop, and dishwasher** the listing describes,
   and that dishes and cookware are in the room.
