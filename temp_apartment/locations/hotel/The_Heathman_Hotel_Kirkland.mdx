## Why this isn't a fit

Two independent dealbreakers, either of which is sufficient.

**Price.** At an estimated **$6,000–$9,000/month**, it is **82% to 173% over** the $3,300
ceiling. Boutique luxury hotels do not discount 45–63% for a single long-stay guest.

**No full kitchen.** Even at a hypothetical acceptable price, a 60-day stay with no stove is the
same problem flagged at [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx) and
[Redmond Inn](./Redmond_Inn.mdx) — except here Tom would be paying luxury rates *and* eating out
every day.

Downtown Kirkland (Moss Bay) is also not South Kirkland, and at ~15 minutes to Redmond it is
worse on distance than [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx) at 11.

This page exists so the Kirkland map is complete and nobody has to look it up twice.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate**, taken from the hotel's own site (https://heathmankirkland.com/) —
nightly rack rates multiplied by 30, the worst-case tourist number. The real long-stay quote
would be lower; the gap is far too large for that to matter.

* **30 nights at rack:** ~$200–300/night × 30 = **$6,000 – $9,000** — $2,700 to $5,700 over.
* **60 nights at rack:** **$12,000 – $18,000 total.**
* **What clearing budget would require:** a **45–63% cut**, plus a kitchen that does not exist.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

For completeness: under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166),
a guest becomes "nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth
roughly 12–15% on the Eastside, or $300–450/month at typical rates. The clock requires **unbroken
occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward;
others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front —
a ~$350 difference, so ask which. **Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

Don't. If the budget changes, call 425-284-5800 and ask for the Director of Sales.

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. Parking cost? Pet fee? (Neither is published.) Housekeeping frequency?
4. Elevator, ground-floor room, grab bars?
5. Is there a stove in any unit, or only a mini-fridge and microwave?
