## Why this isn't a fit

This is the newer, nicer half of the Marriott Residence Inn pair — full-kitchen suites and an
indoor pool. It is also the half that does not work.

**Price:** **$4,050–$6,930/month** estimated, 23% to 110% over the ceiling. Apply Marriott's
standard 20–30% long-stay discount to the bottom and you get $2,835–$3,240 — technically under.
Apply it to anything above the bottom and you are over. **It needs the best case on every
variable at once.**

**Then add parking.** Downtown Bellevue routinely charges $15–25/night — **$450–750/month** — and
the cost here is unconfirmed. That is the same landmine flagged at
[ESA Bellevue Downtown](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Downtown.mdx), and it is enough to erase
the entire margin in the best case.

**And the location is wrong twice:** ~20 minutes to Redmond, the longest drive of any Bellevue
contender, and downtown Bellevue is not East Bellevue.

The sister property settles it. [Residence Inn Seattle Bellevue](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_Bellevue.mdx)
is the **same brand, same full kitchen, 8 minutes to Redmond, free parking**, and starts at
$3,960 instead of $6,930. It is better on every axis that matters. **Make that call. Skip this
one.**

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** Nightly rates × 30 from a Kayak listing
(https://www.kayak.com/Bellevue-Hotels-Residence-Inn-by-Marriott-Seattle-Bellevue-Downtown.189810.ksp)
— worst-case tourist rates, not a quote. The real long-stay quote comes in lower; the question is
whether it comes in low enough, and the answer is probably not.

* **30 nights at rack, worst case:** ~$135–231/night × 30 = **$4,050 – $6,930.**
* **60 nights at rack, worst case:** **$8,100 – $13,860 total.**
* **With a 20–30% Marriott long-stay discount:** **$2,835 – $5,544/month** — only the very bottom
  of that clears $3,300.
* **Add downtown parking at $450–750/month:** even the best case ($2,835) becomes **$3,285–$3,585**
  — at or over the line.
* **The tax exemption** then removes 12–15%, which is the only thing that could rescue it. That is
  a lot of things that all have to go right.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly 12–15% on
the Eastside, or $300–450/month. The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no
checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively**
across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front — a ~$350 difference, so ask which.
**Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption. The listed minimum is 1 night, so
the booking engine will sell you 29 at full tax without a word.

## When you call

Only after the sister property. If you do, call 425-637-8500 and ask for the extended-stay sales
manager — not the 800 line.

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. **What does parking cost per month?** (This is the question here.) Pet fee? Housekeeping
   frequency on a long-stay rate?
4. Elevator, ground-floor suite, grab bars?
5. Confirm the suite has a full kitchen with a **stove**, and that dishes and cookware are in the
   room.
