## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

Choice Hotels lists this property as **extended stay**, which means the monthly conversation is
one they expect to have. And it is **one of only two Kirkland properties in this search with any
real kitchen** — the other being nothing at all, since
[La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx),
[Baymont](./Baymont_Seattle_Kirkland.mdx), and [Motel 6](./Motel_6_North_Kirkland.mdx) are all
microwave-and-mini-fridge. For a 60-day stay, that is the difference between cooking and eating
out for two months.

The walkability is a genuine senior benefit. It sits **across from The Village at Totem Lake** —
groceries and a pharmacy on foot, no car needed for errands. Over two months that matters more
than it sounds like it does.

Budget is tight but workable: **$2,580–$3,300/month estimated**, which at the top *exactly hits*
the ceiling with zero margin. The estimate is a worst-case tourist number, so the real quote
should land lower — but this is not a property with the $900 cushion that
[ESA Factoria](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Factoria.mdx) has.

Two weaknesses. **Totem Lake is north Kirkland, not South Kirkland** — it is outside the target
sub-area and ~16 minutes to Redmond, versus La Quinta's 11. And the kitchen is only in the
**two-room suites**. If you call and price a standard room, you get a microwave and a mini-fridge
and you have thrown away the reason this page exists. **Ask for the suite rate.**

The pet policy is not published. Senior-friendliness is "Partial" — no elevator or grab-bar
detail is documented either way.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** These are nightly rates multiplied by 30 — the worst-case rate a
one-night tourist pays, sourced from TripAdvisor
(https://www.tripadvisor.com/Hotel_Review-g58541-d100451-Reviews-Comfort_Inn_Kirkland-Kirkland_Washington.html),
not a quote. **The real monthly number will likely come in lower.** The CSV notes weekly and
extended rates exist as a category but records no published figure.

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$86–110/night × 30 = **$2,580 – $3,300**. At the top, that is
  exactly the ceiling — $0 of margin.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$5,160 – $6,600 total**, or the same $2,580–$3,300/month.
* **Realistic expectation:** lower. A Choice-branded property that markets itself as extended
  stay has a monthly tier; it simply is not online.

**Price the suite, not the room.** The kitchenette is the point. A cheaper standard-room quote
is not a better deal — it is a different, worse product that puts this page in the same bucket
as La Quinta but 5 minutes further from Redmond.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**. At a property whose worst case sits exactly on the
$3,300 ceiling, **this exemption is what creates the margin.**

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. No minimum term is published
here, so nothing will prompt you toward 30 — you have to ask.

## When you call

**Call the property at 425-380-8495.** Choice properties are franchises; ask for the General
Manager or owner, who is the person who can approve an extended-stay rate.

1. **Ask specifically for the two-room suite with the kitchenette.** What is the all-in rate for
   30 nights, and for 60 nights? Choice lists this property as extended stay — what is that
   product?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. **What is the pet policy and fee?** (Not published.)
   How often is housekeeping?
4. Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit, or one with grab bars?
5. **Does the suite kitchenette have a stove, or only a microwave and a mini-fridge?** This is
   the question that decides whether the property is worth booking.
