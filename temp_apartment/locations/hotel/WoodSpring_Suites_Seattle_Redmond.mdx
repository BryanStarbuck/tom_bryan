## Why this is a fit

Two things make this page matter. First, it is **in Redmond** — not Redmond-adjacent, not
"named Redmond but actually Bellevue" like [ESA Seattle-Redmond](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx).
Zero drive. Second, it has the **best-documented accessibility features found anywhere in this
entire search**: roll-in showers, 32-inch doorways, and a braille elevator. If Tom's mobility
is a real factor now or could become one during the stay, this is the property that has already
answered the question in writing.

The product is right otherwise too. Studio suite, full kitchen **with a dishwasher** (better
than the ESA two-burner setup), utilities included, free parking, no lease.

Privacy is complete — it is a private studio, no roommates, no shared anything.

The problems are two. **No price is published anywhere**, so this page cannot tell you whether
it clears $3,300 — only a phone call can. WoodSpring is positioned as a budget extended-stay
brand, which is a reason for optimism, but that is a prior, not a fact. And the monthly rate is
a real product but it **requires 28 nights paid at check-in** — a single up-front payment, which
is a cash-flow consideration and, as noted below, a tax trap if you take it literally.

One more: **dogs only.** If Tom has a cat, this property is out. [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx)
takes pets free with no such restriction.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule — read this carefully here

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**. The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one
reservation, no checkout.

**This property is the single most important place to get that right, because its advertised
minimum is 28 nights.** Twenty-eight nights is *below* the exemption threshold. If you book
their standard 28-night package and stop, Tom pays lodging tax on the entire stay and gets
nothing back.

**Book 30 nights, not 28 and never 29.** A 29-night stay is the worst possible length: all the
cost, none of the exemption. Two extra nights buys a $300–450 tax exemption — the cheapest
money in this whole search.

Then ask the second question: does the tax drop only from day 30 forward, or is it refunded
**retroactively** across the first 29 days when you contract 30+ nights up front? That
difference is about **$350**.

## When you call

**Call the property at 425-598-7010.** Ask for the General Manager — WoodSpring properties run
lean and the GM is the person who sets and approves monthly rates.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights? (Not the 28-night package — 30.)
2. **Your minimum is listed as 28 nights. I want 30+ specifically for the WAC 458-20-166
   exemption.** Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first
   29 days if I contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. **Pets are listed as dogs only — confirm the policy and
   the fee.** How often is housekeeping?
4. **This is the priority question here:** I need the accessible unit. Confirm the roll-in
   shower, the 32" doorway, and elevator access. Can you hold one of those units for the dates?
   Are grab bars in the bathroom?
5. The suite lists a full kitchen with a dishwasher — confirm it has a **stove**, and confirm
   whether dishes and cookware are provided or requested at the desk.

Also: the 28 nights are **paid at check-in**. Confirm what that means for a 60-night stay — one
payment or two — and what the cancellation and refund terms are if Tom finds a long-term place
in week three.
