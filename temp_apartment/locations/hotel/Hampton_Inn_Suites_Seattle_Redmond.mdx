## Why this isn't a fit

The CSV's note on this property is blunt: **"Listed for completeness — recommend against."**

It is in Redmond, which is right, and it has free hot breakfast, which is nice. But it is **not
an extended-stay product** — Hampton is Hilton's select-service brand for short business trips —
and it has **no kitchen**, only a microwave and mini-fridge. Over 60 days that means eating out
every day.

At an estimated **$7,020/month** it is **113% over** the $3,300 ceiling. Even a 30% long-stay
discount lands at $4,914 — still 49% over, still with no stove.

The direct comparison ends it: [WoodSpring Suites](./WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) is
also in Redmond, has a full kitchen with a dishwasher, and has the best documented accessibility
in the search. Same city, right product. Call that instead.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

For completeness: under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166),
a guest becomes "nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth
roughly 12–15% on the Eastside, or $300–450/month at typical rates. The clock requires **unbroken
occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward;
others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front —
a ~$350 difference, so ask which. **Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

Don't. If you do, call 425-553-1200 direct — not the Hilton 800 line — and ask for the General
Manager:

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. Parking cost? Pet fee? (Neither published.) Housekeeping frequency?
4. Elevator, ground-floor room, grab bars?
5. Is there a stove or kitchenette in any suite? (Expect: no — microwave and mini-fridge only.)
