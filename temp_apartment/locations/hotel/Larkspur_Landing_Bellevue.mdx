## Why this is a fit

Everything Tom needs is already bundled. It is furnished, it has a full kitchen, utilities are
included, parking is free, and there is free coin-less laundry on site. There is no lease, no
income test, no application fee, and no furniture to rent — he arrives with suitcases and lives
there. That is the whole argument for the extended-stay category, and Larkspur executes it
better than anyone else on this list because it is the only one that will tell you the price
before you pick up the phone.

On budget it is not close. The published 30-night rate lands at **$2,550/month against a
$3,300 ceiling** — roughly $750/month of headroom. Even the top of the published range
($3,030) still clears. Nothing else in this search does that on a published number.

The term is a clean fit. Thirty nights is the stated minimum, which is exactly the shape of
Tom's stay, and the property explicitly states a tax exemption over 30 nights — meaning they
already know how to do this and you are not asking them to invent a product.

The one real flaw is distance. Eastgate is **~20 minutes to Redmond**, which is the outer edge
of the target radius and the worst drive of any contender. If Redmond proximity turns out to
matter more than price, [ESA Seattle-Redmond](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) at
9 minutes and [WoodSpring Suites](./WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) actually in Redmond
are the trade. Senior-friendliness is only rated "Partial" here — the CSV records no elevator
or grab-bar detail either way, so that is a question for the call, not an assumption.

**One caveat that matters:** the rate card behind that $2,550 number expired **2026-05-02**.
The price is real but it is stale. Reconfirm it before treating it as the plan.

## The price math

The price confidence here is **published**, and that is what makes this page different from
almost every other hotel page in this search. The source is Larkspur's own extended-stay rate
page (https://www.larkspurhotels.com/bellevue/offers/extended-stay-rates), which cites
**$85/night** at 30+ nights.

* **30 nights:** $85 × 30 = **$2,550** — $750 under the $3,300 ceiling.
* **60 nights:** $85 × 60 = **$5,100**, or **$2,550/month** — same monthly rate, $1,500 of
  total headroom across the stay.
* **Top of the published range:** $3,030/month (about $101/night) — presumably a larger suite.
  Still under budget. The CSV does not say which unit type maps to which number; ask.

Add on top of that: a pet, if Tom has one, is a real cost here — the overview notes Larkspur
at **$25/night, which is $750/month** and would eat the entire budget cushion. Confirm the
pet fee structure explicitly. [La Quinta](./La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx) takes
pets free, which is a genuine swing factor if a pet is in play.

Again: **that rate card expired 2026-05-02.** Treat $2,550 as a strong prior, not a quote.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest
becomes "nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying. On the
Eastside that is worth roughly **12–15%**, or **$300–450/month**.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy** — one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the
tax only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if
you contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350**, so ask which one they do.
Larkspur already states a tax exemption over 30 nights, so the exemption itself is not in
doubt here — only the retroactive question is.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

**Call the property directly at 425-373-1212** — not a central reservations line. Ask for the
General Manager or whoever handles extended stays.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights? **The published $85/night rate
   card expired 2026-05-02 — is that rate still live?**
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if
   I contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. What is the pet fee (nightly or per stay)? How often
   is housekeeping?
4. Is there an elevator? Can you guarantee a ground-floor unit, or a unit with grab bars?
5. The suites list a full kitchen — confirm it has a **stove**, not just a microwave, and
   confirm dishes and cookware are provided.

Also worth asking: which unit type corresponds to $2,550 and which to $3,030, and whether the
free laundry is on every floor.
