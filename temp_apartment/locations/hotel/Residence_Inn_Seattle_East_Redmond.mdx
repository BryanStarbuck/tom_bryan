## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

On product, this is the best thing in Redmond. Full-kitchen suites, **Redmond Town Center** —
walkable to shopping, restaurants, and errands without driving — and it is one of the few
properties rated **senior-friendly: Yes**. Zero drive to Redmond because it *is* Redmond. If
money were not a constraint, this page would be ranked first.

Money is a constraint, and the gap is large: **$4,410–$8,850/month estimated**, which is 34% to
168% over the ceiling. The top of that range is not a real number for a long stay, but even the
bottom needs a ~25% cut to reach $3,300.

**Here is the specific reason to be pessimistic, and it is worth understanding:** Redmond Town
Center is the Microsoft corporate hotel market. Marriott's extended-stay inventory here is
filled year-round by companies paying corporate relocation rates with no price sensitivity. A
hotel with reliable corporate demand has **no reason** to discount to a single retiree. Compare
[Residence Inn Bothell](./Residence_Inn_Bothell.mdx) — same brand, similar product, estimated
$2,700–4,200, because Bothell does not have Microsoft propping up its rates. **That price
difference is not about the buildings. It is about who else wants the rooms.**

So: make the call, because the property is genuinely the right shape for Tom and a 20–30%
Marriott long-stay discount is a real thing. But if
[Residence Inn Seattle Bellevue](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_Bellevue.mdx) is 8 minutes away and
starts $500–4,700/month cheaper, that is where the same negotiation actually lands under budget.

**One tactic from the CSV worth taking seriously: retry in shoulder season.** If Tom's dates
have any flexibility away from summer peak, this property's answer could change materially.

Parking cost is not published — at a Town Center property, ask before assuming it is free.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** These are nightly rates multiplied by 30 — worst-case tourist
rates from a Yelp listing
(https://www.yelp.com/biz/residence-inn-seattle-east-redmond-redmond-2), not a quote. **The real
long-stay quote will come in lower** — the question is whether it comes in low enough.

* **30 nights at rack, worst case:** ~$147–295/night × 30 = **$4,410 – $8,850**.
* **60 nights at rack, worst case:** **$8,820 – $17,700 total.**
* **With Marriott's standard 20–30% long-stay discount applied to the bottom of the range:**
  * 20% off $4,410 = **$3,528/month** — still $228 over.
  * 30% off $4,410 = **$3,087/month** — $213 under. That is the only combination that works.
* **What that means:** this property needs the **best case on demand and the best case on the
  discount, simultaneously**, to clear budget. It is possible. It is not likely in July.
* **The tax exemption** then takes 12–15% off, worth ~$400–500/month here — which is the thing
  that could rescue a $3,528 quote into range. Do not forget to ask for it.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**, and more at this property's rates.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. No minimum term is published here,
so the booking engine will happily sell you 29 nights at full tax.

## When you call

**Call 425-497-9226 and ask for the extended-stay sales manager.** Marriott.com and the 800
number cannot quote 30+ nights. Do not bother with them.

1. **What is your 30+ night rate, all-in, for a single guest — 30 nights and 60 nights?** State
   it as unbroken occupancy on one reservation.
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. **What does parking cost?** (Not published.) What is the pet fee? How often is housekeeping on
   a long-stay rate?
4. This property is rated senior-friendly — confirm. Elevator? Ground-floor suite available?
   Grab bars?
5. Confirm the suite has a **full kitchen with a stove**, and that dishes and cookware are in the
   room.

**And the one that might actually matter:** *"If those dates don't work at that price, what
months do?"* Shoulder season is this property's realistic path to budget.
