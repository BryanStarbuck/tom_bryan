## Why this isn't a fit

The location is right — Redmond Town Center, zero drive — and nothing else is.

**It is not an extended-stay product.** This is a full-service convention and business hotel.
Marriott runs three separate extended-stay brands in this market —
[Residence Inn](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_East_Redmond.mdx) and
[Element](./Element_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) — precisely because this one is not designed for
long stays. **There are no kitchens in any room.** Two months of restaurant meals is not the
plan.

And at an estimated **$9,270/month** it is **181% over** the ceiling. The 20–30% Marriott
long-stay discount that makes [Residence Inn Seattle Bellevue](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_Bellevue.mdx)
genuinely interesting would bring this to $6,489–$7,416 — still roughly double budget, in a room
with no stove.

If the goal is a Marriott extended-stay rate near Redmond, call Residence Inn Seattle Bellevue.
That is the same negotiation with a product that fits and a price that can land.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

For completeness: under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166),
a guest becomes "nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth
roughly 12–15% on the Eastside, or $300–450/month at typical rates. The clock requires **unbroken
occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward;
others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front —
a ~$350 difference, so ask which. **Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

Don't. Call [Residence Inn Seattle Bellevue](./Residence_Inn_Seattle_Bellevue.mdx) instead — same
brand family, real kitchen, 8 minutes to Redmond, a discount that actually reaches budget.

If you do call 425-498-4000, ask for the Director of Sales and ask the standard five:

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. Parking cost? Pet fee? (Neither published.) Housekeeping frequency?
4. Elevator, ground-floor room, grab bars?
5. Is there a stove or kitchenette in any room? (Expect: no.)
