## Why this isn't a fit

Aloft is a style-forward select-service hotel aimed at short business trips. **It has no kitchen
of any kind** — not a kitchenette, not a wet bar — and no long-stay product. At an estimated
**$8,670/month** it is **163% over** the $3,300 ceiling.

The one useful fact on this page: **Aloft shares a building with
[Element Seattle Redmond](./Element_Seattle_Redmond.mdx).** Same address, same complex, steps
from the Overlake Village light rail. Element is Marriott's *dedicated extended-stay* brand with
full kitchens and unpublished long-stay rates. **If you are looking at this building, call
Element, not Aloft.** Same location, right product.

Nothing else here. Skip.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

For completeness: under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166),
a guest becomes "nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth
roughly 12–15% on the Eastside, or $300–450/month at typical rates. The clock requires **unbroken
occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward;
others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you contract 30+ nights up front —
a ~$350 difference, so ask which. **Never book 29 nights**: all the cost, none of the exemption.

## When you call

Don't — call [Element](./Element_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) at 425-636-9942 instead, same building.

If you do call 425-636-9922, ask for the sales manager and run the standard five:

1. All-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days?
3. Parking cost? Pet fee? Housekeeping frequency?
4. Elevator, ground-floor room, grab bars?
5. Is there a kitchen or even a kitchenette in any room? (Expect: no.)
