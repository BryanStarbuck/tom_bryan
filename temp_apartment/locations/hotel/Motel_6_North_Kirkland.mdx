## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

Let's be direct about what this page is for. On price, nothing beats it: **~$1,800–2,400/month**,
roughly **$1,000 under the $3,300 ceiling**, with free parking, pets allowed, utilities
included, and no lease. Budget is not the constraint in this search — term length and furniture
are — and this property proves the point by being cheap and still not the answer.

Because the rest of it is bad. Reviews are **mixed to poor**. There is **no kitchen**, and the
microwave and fridge are not even consistent from room to room. Furnishing is listed as only
"Partial." Senior-friendliness is rated **No** — the only hotel in this entire search with that
rating. It sits in **Totem Lake**, ~17 minutes from Redmond and nowhere near the South Kirkland
target zone.

Here is the honest math that is not in dollars: Tom would be living here for 60 days. Not
crashing for a night between flights — living. Every day for two months. Against
[ESA Factoria](./Extended_Stay_America_Bellevue_Factoria.mdx) at an estimated $1,710–2,400 —
**the same money, possibly less** — he would have a full kitchen, weekly housekeeping,
breakfast, and a real extended-stay product. **Motel 6 is not even reliably the cheapest option
once you compare it to the right competitor.** That is the finding that should end this page.

Where it earns its place: if the budget tightens materially, or if everything else is booked for
Tom's dates, this is a private room with a door that he can afford. That is worth knowing exists.
It is a floor, not a plan.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** These are nightly rates multiplied by 30 — worst-case tourist
rates, sourced from an Allstays directory listing
(https://www.allstays.com/us-washington-hotels/kirkland-hotels.htm), not a quote.

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$60–80/night × 30 = **$1,800 – $2,400**. Clears $3,300 by
  $900–1,500.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$3,600 – $4,800 total**, or the same $1,800–$2,400/month.
* **Realistic expectation:** lower. Motel 6 runs weekly rates as a normal part of its business,
  and a 30+ night rate almost certainly exists — it is simply not published.

**The comparison that matters:** ESA Factoria estimates at $1,710–$2,400/month for a furnished
studio with a full kitchen. That is the **same range**. When the cheap option and the good option
cost the same, the cheap option has no argument left.

**Add the food cost** of 60 days with no kitchen — and note that unlike La Quinta or Redmond Inn,
you may not even reliably get a microwave and a fridge to fall back on.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the tax
only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That gap is about **$350** — ask which.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. Note that at this price point the
exemption is worth proportionally less in dollars than it is at a $3,000/month property — but it
is still free money for asking.

## When you call

**Call the property directly at 425-821-5618**, not the Motel 6 central line. Ask for the
manager.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights? Do you have a weekly or monthly
   rate?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as free — confirm. What is the pet fee? **How often is housekeeping, and is
   it included at a weekly/monthly rate?** (Budget properties often reduce or drop housekeeping
   on long stays — ask directly.)
4. **Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit?** This property is rated poorly for
   senior-friendliness and nothing about accessibility is documented. Any grab bars anywhere?
5. **Does the room have a microwave and a fridge?** The CSV says this **varies by room** — do not
   assume. There is no stove in any case.

Ask one more thing before booking: **can you see the specific room first?** Given the review
profile, sight-unseen is a bad idea for a 60-day commitment.
