## Why this is a fit

This is the **cheapest East Bellevue option found in the search**, and it is a complete product:
furnished studio, full kitchen, utilities included, free surface parking, pets allowed, no
lease, no income test. Grab-and-go breakfast is included. For a 60-day stay where the goal is
to spend as little as possible while Tom hunts for a real lease, this is the value play.

The budget case is the strongest of any hotel here. Even at the *worst-case* estimate of
$2,400/month, it clears the $3,300 ceiling by $900 — and the real quote should come in lower
still. At the bottom of the range it is nearly half the budget.

The cost of that price is distance and service. **Factoria is ~18 minutes to Redmond** — better
than [Larkspur's](./Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) 20 but nowhere near
[ESA Seattle-Redmond's](./Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) 9. And **housekeeping is
weekly only**, which is the standard ESA tier. Over 60 days that is eight or nine cleanings —
better than the every-other-week schedule at the Redmond ESA, but well short of a hotel.

Senior-friendliness is listed as **Unknown** — the research turned up nothing either way on
elevators, grab bars, or accessible units. That is a gap, not a negative, but it means the
accessibility question has to be settled on the call. The CSV notes to **ask for a
ground-floor unit**, which is the practical workaround if there is no elevator.

Privacy is complete: a private studio, no roommates.

## The price math

**Price confidence: estimate.** This is a nightly rate multiplied by 30 — the worst-case rate a
one-night tourist pays. It is not a quote. **The real monthly number will very likely come in
lower**, because ESA does not publish its 30+ night rate and the nightly rate is not
representative of it.

* **30 nights, worst case:** ~$57–80/night × 30 = **$1,710 – $2,400**. Clears $3,300 by
  $900–1,590.
* **60 nights, worst case:** **$3,420 – $4,800 total**, or the same $1,710–$2,400/month.
* **Realistic expectation:** below the bottom of that range once the 30+ night tier is applied.

There is no furniture to rent, no utility account to open, no deposit, and no application fee —
so the monthly figure is close to the true all-in figure. That is the hidden advantage over the
cheap unfurnished apartments, which look cheaper on paper and are not once you add
[CORT furniture](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) and two moves.

## The Washington 30-day tax rule

Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166), a guest becomes
"nontransient" at **30 continuous days** and lodging tax stops applying — worth roughly **12–15%**
on the Eastside, or **$300–450/month**.

The clock requires **unbroken occupancy**: one reservation, no checkout. Some hotels drop the
tax only from day 30 forward; others refund it **retroactively** across the first 29 days if you
contract 30+ nights up front. That difference is about **$350** — ask which one they do.

**Never book 29 nights.** All the cost, none of the exemption. ESA will try to sell you the
7-night discount tier; ignore it. Seven nights is the wrong product and does not reach the
exemption.

## When you call

**Call the front desk directly at 425-865-8680 — not the ESA 800 number.** Central reservations
cannot quote monthly rates. Ask for the General Manager.

1. What is the all-in rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30, and is it refunded retroactively on the first 29 days if I
   contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Parking is listed as a free surface lot — confirm. What is the pet fee? **Housekeeping is
   listed as weekly — confirm, and ask what it costs to add visits.**
4. **Is there an elevator? Can you hold a ground-floor unit?** Nothing is documented about
   accessibility here — this is the open question. Any units with grab bars?
5. Confirm the full kitchen has a **stove**, and confirm whether dishes and cookware come with
   the room or are requested at the desk.
