## Why this isn't a fit

**The price is real. That's what makes it dangerous.** $1,795/month flat, furnished, utilities
included, 30-day minimum, an entire private apartment. That is roughly **$1,500 under budget** and
cheaper than anything else in this search. If you only read the price, this looks like the answer.

**It isn't, because you don't get to choose where you live.** Standby is Landing's inventory-filling
product: you accept whatever unit Landing has sitting empty, and **Landing picks both the apartment
and the CITY.** The entire charter for this search is three specific places — Redmond, East Bellevue,
South Kirkland — chosen so Tom is near family and inside a 20-minute drive. Standby cannot honor
that. There is no version of this product where you say "Redmond" and it says yes.

**And then the part that actually disqualifies it: the 3-day bump.** Landing can **relocate you to a
different unit with three days' notice.** Think about what that means concretely for a 78-year-old on
a 60-day stay: pack, move, and re-learn a new building and a new neighborhood on 72 hours' notice,
possibly more than once, possibly in a different city. For a corporate traveler with a suitcase and a
laptop, that's a bad weekend. For a senior, it is the exact failure mode this whole search exists to
avoid — the same category of harm as the [Sonder](./Sonder.mdx) lockouts, just slower and legal.

**The other restrictions, any one of which is its own problem:**

* **No pets.** Hard stop if Tom has one.
* **Parking not guaranteed.** Not "extra fee" — *not guaranteed*.
* **640+ credit score** required.
* **$150 switch fee** if *you* want to move.

**The honest verdict:** this is a product for a young, flexible, mobile, pet-less renter who does not
care which city they wake up in and can move on a Tuesday. That is the precise opposite of Tom. The
$1,795 is not a discount — it's the price Landing pays you to accept the risk, and this is a renter
who cannot afford to accept it.

## The price math

The arithmetic is included only to show that **cheap is not the same as suitable.**

| | 30 days | 60 days |
|---|---|---|
| Standby ($1,795/mo flat) | $1,795 | $3,590 |
| Plus $199/year membership | ~$1,994 | ~$3,789 |

Against **$3,300/month**: clears by roughly **$1,500/month**. A full 60-day stay costs about **$3,789
— barely more than one month** of most alternatives.

**And it should still be declined.** Note what the number does not include: the cost of being
relocated mid-stay on three days' notice — a move Tom would have to execute himself, potentially
twice, potentially to another city. That cost isn't in dollars, which is exactly why it's easy to
miss when comparing a spreadsheet column.

**Compare instead:** [Landing — Skyline Redmond](./Landing_Skyline_Redmond.mdx) is **$1,767–2,451**
for a *named unit* in *Redmond* with *covered parking* — the low end is **cheaper than Standby** with
none of the risk. That comparison is the whole argument. Standby isn't even the best price Landing
offers in Redmond.
