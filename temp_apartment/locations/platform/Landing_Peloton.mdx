## Why this is a fit

**Location: exactly where Tom wants to be.** Downtown Redmond — the top of the charter's preference
list. Zero drive time, because he'd already be there.

**Budget: it clears, at both ends of the range.** $2,204–3,223/month, published. The ceiling is $77
under the $3,300 budget and the floor is nearly $1,100 under it. That is a rare thing in this search:
almost every furnished option either has no price at all ([Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx))
or prices well over the line. Note the caveat below about which rate this actually is.

**Term and privacy: correct.** Landing's 30-day minimum is a real product. Tom gets an entire
private 1BR — no roommates, no cohabitation. Utilities, WiFi, and cleaning are bundled. No security
deposit, no application fee; the only extra is Landing's **$199/year** membership.

**The senior case is the best part.** This is a 3-story, 150-unit building sitting **directly on the
Sammamish River Trail**. For an older resident who walks — and who may prefer not to drive for
exercise or errands — a flat, paved, scenic trail out the front door is a genuine daily-life
upgrade, not a marketing bullet. This row is one of the few in the entire CSV flagged senior
friendly: **Yes**.

**The one real caveat on price.** The $2,204–3,223 range is the **building-level** rate. **Landing's
furnished rate may differ** — furnished units through a platform typically price above the building's
own unfurnished rate. Do not treat $3,223 as a confirmed ceiling until Landing quotes you directly.
This is the first question to ask on the call.

**The Landing-wide risk applies here too.** Every specific Landing unit URL 404'd during research,
and Landing has a documented BBB/Trustpilot pattern of reservation bumping, cancellations two weeks
out, and lockbox failures. See [Landing (platform)](./Landing.mdx) for the full picture. It doesn't
disqualify this building — it dictates that you book it by phone, on a named unit, in writing.

## How to book

**Call 415-231-1701.** Do not try to book this on the website.

1. **Do not search "Redmond" on hellolanding.com** — it 404s. Redmond is filed under the **Seattle
   metro**. Give the address to the agent instead: **7435 159th Pl NE**.
2. **Ask what Landing's furnished rate is for this building** — specifically whether it differs from
   the $2,204–3,223 building rate. This is the number that decides everything.
3. **Ask them to HOLD a specific unit** for Tom's dates. 9 units showed at building level, but
   Landing inventory churns in days.
4. **Get it in writing**: unit number, exact dates, all-in monthly total.
5. **Ask what happens if Landing cancels or reassigns.** Their record on bumping is poor. Get the
   policy before paying.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* "Is the $199 membership annual and charged once?"
* "Confirm: no security deposit, no application fee?"
* "Is parking included at Peloton, or is it an add-on?"
* "Is there an elevator? Can Tom get a ground-floor or low-floor unit?" *(3-story building — worth
  asking.)*
* "If the lockbox fails on arrival, what number reaches a human after hours?"
