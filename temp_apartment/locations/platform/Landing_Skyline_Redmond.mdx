## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**On price, nothing else comes close.** $1,767–2,451/month for a furnished, utilities-included,
30-day-minimum private apartment **in Redmond**. The ceiling is $849 under budget; the floor is
$1,533 under it. For comparison, [Blueground's](../blueground/Blueground.mdx) Redmond estimate starts
at $3,500. This is the cheapest verified Redmond furnished number found anywhere in the search, and
it is published, not estimated.

**Term, privacy, and structure: all correct.** 30-day minimum — a real product, not a penalty tier.
An entire private apartment, no roommates. Utilities, WiFi, and cleaning bundled. **Covered parking**
included, which is a small but real quality-of-life item for a senior in a Pacific Northwest winter —
no scraping, no walking through rain to the car. No security deposit, no application fee; just
Landing's **$199/year** membership. The building has a pool, clubhouse, and fitness center.

**And then the problem: it showed ZERO availability.** At the time of research, no units. That is the
whole story. A price this good with no inventory behind it is not an option — it's a lottery ticket.

**Why call anyway.** Landing's inventory turns over in days, in both directions. Units 404 within a
week, but they also *appear* within a week. A building showing zero today can have something next
week, and Landing's phone agents see inventory the website doesn't reliably surface. Given the price
gap — this is $1,000+/month cheaper than the alternatives — one phone call is obviously worth it.
Just don't wait on it.

**The Landing-wide risk applies.** Reservation bumping, cancellations two weeks before move-in, and
lockbox failures are documented in BBB/Trustpilot complaints. See [Landing (platform)](./Landing.mdx).
For a building already showing zero availability, the bumping risk is *higher*, not lower — there's
no slack in the inventory to reassign you within the same building.

## How to book

**Call 415-231-1701 and ask about Olde Redmond Place, 7001 Old Redmond Rd.**

1. **Do not search "Redmond" on hellolanding.com** — it 404s. Redmond is filed under the **Seattle
   metro**. Give the agent the address.
2. **Ask the real question: "When does something open at this building?"** The website said zero. Ask
   what's coming, and what the notice window looks like.
3. **Ask to be notified** the moment a unit opens for Tom's dates, and get a name and a direct
   callback number — not a web form.
4. **Ask what else Landing has in Redmond at a similar price** while you have them on the phone. See
   [Peloton](./Landing_Peloton.mdx) and [Chelsea Square](./Landing_Chelsea_Square.mdx).
5. **Have a backup ready.** Do not let this building's price talk you into waiting. If it opens, take
   it; if it doesn't, Peloton is the realistic Redmond answer.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* "Is the $199 membership annual and charged once?"
* "Confirm: no security deposit, no application fee?"
* "Is the covered parking included in the rate?"
* "Is there an elevator? Can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"
* "If Landing cancels or reassigns my unit, how much notice do I get?"
