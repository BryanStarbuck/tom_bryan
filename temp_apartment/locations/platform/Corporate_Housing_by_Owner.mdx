## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The model is the appeal: private owners, direct negotiation, no markup.** CHBO is a marketplace
where individual property owners list furnished units themselves. There's no corporate operator in
the middle taking a spread. That matters here for two reasons. First, price — you're paying the
owner's number, not the owner's number plus an operator's margin (which is essentially what
[Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx) and
[CorporateHousing.com](./CorporateHousing_com.mdx) charge for). Second, **flexibility** — a private
owner can say yes to a 45-day stay on the phone. A corporate pricing engine cannot. For a 1–2 month
term, the person who can just decide is worth finding.

**Term and privacy: both correct.** **1-month minimum** — among the shortest in the entire search,
and it's the platform's actual product. All whole units — studios, condos, townhouses, houses. No
roommates, no cohabitation, no shared anything.

**The $1,450 floor is the lowest verified number found anywhere in this search.** That's a real data
point, not an estimate.

## The inventory dispute — read this honestly

**Two research agents looked at this platform and reached opposite conclusions. Neither has been
resolved, so both are reported here.**

**Reading A — there's real inventory:**
Found **42+ Redmond-area rentals** spanning **$1,450–$17,000**, including a specific, plausible
candidate: **a Kirkland 3BR townhouse at $3,500/month, 1-month minimum, available Aug 15.** On this
reading CHBO is a legitimate channel worth working.

**Reading B — the inventory is essentially fictional for our purposes:**
Found **essentially no true Redmond inventory** — just one **$7,999 executive home** — and judged the
"42+ rentals" headline to be **padded with Mercer Island, Kenmore, SeaTac, Snoqualmie, Lynnwood and
Seattle listings**. On this reading, CHBO's Redmond search is a geography-stretching SEO artifact and
there's nothing here.

**Bryan: search it yourself and judge.** These two readings cannot both be right, and the difference
decides whether this is a 20-minute detour or a real lead. The tell will be obvious within a few
minutes of looking at the actual result list: **check the city on every listing.** If the 42 results
are mostly Snoqualmie and SeaTac, Reading B is correct and you can close the tab. If there are ten
genuine Redmond/Bellevue/Kirkland units, Reading A is correct and this is worth working.

**One note that supports Reading B's skepticism:** the price range is **$1,450 to $17,000**. A 12×
spread is what you see when a search is scooping in an entire region rather than a city. A real
Redmond furnished search should not contain a $17,000/month listing.

Against **$3,300/month**:

* **The $1,450 floor clears by $1,850/month** — a 60-day stay at $2,900 costs less than one month of
  budget. But no specific unit at that price was documented in the target cities.
* **The Kirkland townhouse at $3,500 is $200 over budget per month** — close, and a private owner
  might move on that for a clean 2-month booking. It's also a 3BR, so Tom pays for two bedrooms he
  won't use.
* **Everything above that is not in the conversation.**

**Utilities are "Partial"** and vary by owner — confirm per listing. An unbundled unit can add
$150–250/month.

## How to book

**Search first — the search result is what settles the dispute above. Phone: 877-333-2426.**

1. **Go to
   https://www.corporatehousingbyowner.com/search/United-States--Washington--Redmond/** and look at
   the result list.
2. **Check the CITY on every single listing.** This is the decisive step. Discard anything in Mercer
   Island, Kenmore, SeaTac, Snoqualmie, Lynnwood, or Seattle — none of them meet the 20-minute rule.
   **Count what's left.** That number tells you whether this platform is real for us.
3. **If a handful of genuine Redmond / East Bellevue / South Kirkland units survive**, contact those
   owners directly. That's the whole value here — a person who can negotiate.
4. **Ask the Kirkland 3BR townhouse owner** ($3,500/mo, available Aug 15) whether they'd take $3,300
   for a clean 60-day booking. A private owner facing an empty August can say yes on the spot. A
   corporate operator never will.
5. **See the unit in person before paying.** CHBO is a listings marketplace — verify the unit exists.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* "What's the all-in monthly total — rent, utilities, WiFi, parking, cleaning — for 30 days and for
  60 days?"
* "Are utilities included or billed separately?"
* **"Will anyone else be living in the unit?"** — should be no on CHBO, but confirm.
* "What's the shortest term you'll write, and will you do 30 days with an option to extend to 60?"
* **"Would you take $3,300 for a firm 60-day booking?"** — the reason to use a private-owner
  marketplace is that this question has an answer.
* "Is there an elevator, or is it single-level / ground floor?" *(Tom is a senior.)*
* "Can I see it before I pay?" **A "no" is a red flag.**

### Safety

Payment is owner-to-tenant. **There is no escrow.** Never wire, Zelle, Venmo, or gift-card a deposit
for a unit nobody has seen — the overview documents two confirmed live scams in this exact geography.
