## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Location and term: both right.** Downtown Redmond — top of the charter's preference list, zero
drive time. Landing's 30-day minimum is a genuine product, and Tom gets an entire private apartment
with utilities, WiFi, and cleaning included. No roommates, no security deposit, no application fee —
just the **$199/year** Landing membership.

**Budget: it clears.** $1,839–3,106/month published. Even the ceiling is $194 under budget. The floor
is nearly $1,500 under. Chelsea Square is an **Equity Residential** property that Landing stocks
units in, so the underlying building is professionally managed at scale — a point in its favor over a
small independent landlord. This row is flagged senior friendly: **Yes**.

**Parking is the one structural negative.** Unlike [Skyline Redmond](./Landing_Skyline_Redmond.mdx)
(covered parking included) or [Peloton](./Landing_Peloton.mdx) (parking yes), Chelsea Square's
parking is **partial — an extra fee**. Get the monthly dollar amount before comparing this to the
others, because it changes the ranking. A $150–200/month parking charge erases most of Chelsea
Square's price advantage over Peloton.

**The availability problem.** **The Landing unit URL went dead during the research session.** Landing
had a 2BR here at 1,100 sqft; that listing is gone. This is the same pattern seen across every
Landing building — inventory churns in days and the website is not a reliable picture of what
exists. It does not mean Chelsea Square has nothing; it means the website can't tell you, and only a
phone call can.

**The Landing-wide risk applies.** Documented BBB/Trustpilot pattern of reservation bumping,
cancellations two weeks before move-in, and lockbox/key failures — a January 2026 guest was stranded
outside for an hour. See [Landing (platform)](./Landing.mdx). Book by phone, on a named unit, in
writing.

**Where it ranks:** third among Landing's Redmond buildings. [Peloton](./Landing_Peloton.mdx) has
confirmed availability (9 units) and the Sammamish River Trail.
[Skyline](./Landing_Skyline_Redmond.mdx) is cheaper with covered parking included. Chelsea Square is
the fallback — but it's a good one, and one call covers all three.

| Scenario | 30 days all-in | 60 days all-in |
|---|---|---|
| Low end | ~$2,038 | ~$3,877 |
| High end | ~$3,305 | ~$6,411 |

Against **$3,300/month**:

* **Low end clears by ~$1,460/month.**
* **High end**, amortized over 60 days, is about **$3,206/month — under budget by ~$94.** On a single
  30-day stay it's $3,305, essentially exactly at the line.

**Parking is NOT in these numbers** — it's an extra fee and the amount is not published. **You must
get that figure**, because at the high end it is the difference between clearing budget and missing
it. No security deposit and no application fee, so move-in cash is rent + $199 + parking.

## How to book

**Call 415-231-1701 (Landing).** The property's own number is 425-284-9132, but Equity leases
long-term — Landing is the 30-day channel.

1. **Do not search "Redmond" on hellolanding.com** — it 404s. Redmond is filed under the **Seattle
   metro**. Give the agent the address: **16340 NE 83rd St**.
2. **Ask what Landing actually has at Chelsea Square right now.** The unit URL is dead; the website
   is not the source of truth.
3. **Get the parking fee in dollars per month.** This is the deciding number here.
4. **Ask them to HOLD a specific unit** and confirm in writing: unit number, dates, all-in total
   including parking.
5. **Ask about Peloton and Skyline on the same call** — one conversation should cover all three
   Redmond buildings.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* "What is the monthly parking charge at Chelsea Square?"
* "Is the $199 membership annual and charged once?"
* "Confirm: no security deposit, no application fee?"
* "Is there an elevator? Can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"
* "If Landing cancels or reassigns my unit, how much notice do I get?"
