## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The walkability is the reason this page exists.** **Walk Score 96** — the highest in the entire
search. For a 65-year-old that's a nice-to-have; for a senior spending two months in an unfamiliar
place, it's the difference between independence and driving for every carton of milk. Downtown
Kirkland's Moss Bay means groceries, a pharmacy, restaurants, and the waterfront are all on foot.
This row is flagged senior friendly: **Yes**.

**The unit itself is well matched.** A **576 sqft studio with A/C, in-unit laundry, and covered
parking**. A studio is the right size for one person on a two-month stay — Tom isn't paying for rooms
he won't use, and it's the cheapest tier in the building. In-unit laundry means no hauling baskets to
a shared room. Covered parking matters in a Northwest winter.

**Term and privacy: correct.** 30-day minimum, an entire private apartment, no roommates. Utilities,
WiFi, and cleaning bundled. No security deposit, no application fee — just the **$199/year**
membership.

**Location: acceptable, not preferred.** South Kirkland is third on the charter's list, behind
Redmond and East Bellevue. 15 minutes to Redmond is comfortably inside the 20-minute rule. The
walkability arguably compensates for the ranking.

**Price is unknown, and that's the gap.** Landing publishes no rate for this specific unit. What we
know: Landing's Eastside range runs **$1,767–3,223/month**, and the whole range fits under $3,300. A
576 sqft studio should sit toward the lower half of that. But this is downtown Kirkland waterfront —
one of the pricier addresses on the Eastside — so don't assume the floor. **Get a quote.**

**This is the same building Blueground uses — and that's the actionable point.**
[Blueground — Kirkland](../blueground/Blueground_Kirkland.mdx) offers a furnished studio here
(#4564A, 566 sqft) with an estimated price of **$3,000–4,790**, and a 2BR in this building was quoted
$4,790 as the *lowest 12-month* price. Landing's entire published Eastside ceiling is $3,223. **Same
apartment, same address — Landing is very likely the cheaper door.** Price both before booking
either. [The 101 direct](../apartment/The_101_Kirkland.mdx) is the third door, but it leases 12–15
months only and **fails** the term requirement outright.

**The Landing-wide risk applies.** The unit URL 404s — inventory churns in days. BBB/Trustpilot show
reservation bumping, cancellations two weeks before move-in, and lockbox failures (a January 2026
guest was stranded outside in the cold for an hour). See [Landing (platform)](./Landing.mdx).

## How to book

**Call 415-231-1701 and ask about The 101, 11 Main St S, Kirkland.**

1. **Ask for the studio** — 576 sqft, A/C, in-unit laundry. It's the right size and the cheapest tier.
2. **Get a real quote for Tom's exact dates.** Nothing is published for this unit; the range above is
   inferred.
3. **Ask them to HOLD it and confirm in writing** — unit number, dates, all-in total.
4. **Then call [Blueground](../blueground/Blueground_Kirkland.mdx) at 1-917-259-1222** and price the
   same building. Blueground has a studio here available Jul 25. Compare the two all-in totals
   directly. This takes ten minutes and could save $1,000+/month.
5. **Confirm covered parking is included** in the rate, not an add-on.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* "What's the all-in monthly rate for the studio at The 101 for my dates?"
* "Is the $199 membership annual and charged once?"
* "Confirm: no security deposit, no application fee?"
* "Is covered parking included in the rate?"
* "Is there an elevator, and is the bathroom entry step-free?" *(The 101's direct listings mention
  accessible units — confirm for the specific unit.)*
* "If Landing cancels or reassigns my unit, how much notice do I get?"
