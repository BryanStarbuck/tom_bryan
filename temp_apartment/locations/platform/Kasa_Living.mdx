## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Kasa is a serious operator, and that's the good news.** It **acquired Mint House on 2026-01-14** and
is now the largest apartment-hotel manager in the United States. This is not a startup that might
evaporate mid-stay — the [Sonder](./Sonder.mdx) failure mode isn't the concern here. The product is
real: a furnished, private, entire apartment with utilities included and no lease.

**And then it has exactly one unit on the entire Eastside.** The Bellevue Seattle at 900 108th Ave NE.
That's it. There's no inventory to choose from, no fallback if the dates don't work, no cheaper floor
plan. One address, one shot.

**It's a 2BR only — so Tom pays for space he doesn't need.** One person on a two-month stay needs a
studio or a 1BR. Every furnished operator in this search prices by bedroom count, so a 2BR is
structurally the wrong tier: it's the most expensive unit type in a building where Tom would use half
of it. That single fact is most of why the price doesn't work.

**Location: acceptable but not preferred.** Downtown Bellevue, not **East** Bellevue, which is what
the charter names. 15 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule.

**The no-phone problem is the real dealbreaker for this specific renter.** Kasa has **no phone
number** — support is **text/chat only via help.kasa.com.** For a corporate traveler that's fine,
maybe even preferable. For a 78-year-old alone in an unfamiliar apartment who needs to reach someone
about a lock, a heater, or a billing error, "open a chat widget on a help site" is a bad answer. Every
other serious option in this search has a human on a phone line —
[Landing](./Landing.mdx) at 415-231-1701, [Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx) at
1-917-259-1222, [Merrill Gardens](../senior_living/Merrill_Gardens_at_Kirkland.mdx) at 425-828-2570.
That difference matters more than a hundred dollars a month.

**The honest verdict:** right product, wrong unit type, wrong price, wrong support model for a senior.
Skip unless everything else falls through.

## The price math

**These are ESTIMATES built from Kasa's nightly range and its advertised discount — not quotes.**

Kasa's nightly rate at this property runs **$135–247**, and it advertises **up to 25% off for 30+
nights**. Applying that discount:

| Scenario | Nightly | After 25% | 30 days | 60 days |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Low case** | $135 | ~$101/night | **~$3,038** | ~$6,075 |
| **Average case** | $247 | ~$185/night | **~$5,554** | ~$11,108 |

Against **$3,300/month**:

* **Only the bottom of the range clears budget** — the low case lands ~$262 under.
* **The average case is ~$2,254/month over.** A 60-day stay would run ~$11,108 against a $6,600
  two-month budget.

**Two things make the low case unlikely:**

1. **July is peak season.** Tom's stay starts in the summer, which is precisely when a downtown
   Bellevue nightly rate sits near the top of its range, not the bottom.
2. **The discount is "up to 25%"** — an advertised maximum, not a guaranteed rate. It may be less.

**Not included above:** extra parking at **$15/night** (one compact space is included — a second is
~$450/month) and pets at **$8–30/night** (up to ~$900/month for a 30-day stay). A pet alone could add
more than the entire budget cushion.

**Realistic read: over budget.** The one scenario where this works — bottom-of-range nightly, full
25% discount, in July — requires all three to break your way at once.

## How to book

**There is no one to call. Booking and support both run through https://help.kasa.com (text/chat) and
the property page.**

1. **Go to https://kasa.com/properties/kasa-bellevue-seattle** and enter Tom's exact dates. Kasa
   prices dynamically — the number only exists once dates are in.
2. **Enter 30 nights minimum** to trigger the extended-stay discount. Check what the site actually
   applies — "up to 25%" is not a promise.
3. **Open a chat at help.kasa.com** and ask for the all-in total in writing before booking.
4. **If the 30-night quote comes in over ~$3,300, stop.** There is no second unit here and no cheaper
   floor plan to fall back on. Go to [Landing](./Landing.mdx) instead.

### When you ask (via chat, since there's no phone)

* **"What is the all-in total for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?"** — including taxes, fees, cleaning,
  and parking. Get it in writing in the chat transcript; save it.
* **"What discount actually applies at 30+ nights for my dates?"**
* "Is the one included parking space compact-only? Will a full-size car fit?"
* "What is the total pet fee for a 30-night stay?" *(At $8–30/night this compounds badly.)*
* **"If something breaks at 9pm, how do I reach a person?"** — the answer to this question is the one
  that should decide it.
* "Is there an elevator? Which floor is the unit on?"
