## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The privacy question is settled, and that's worth something.** Every unit on CorporateHousing.com is
a private corporate apartment. There are **no private-room or shared-house listings** — unlike
[Furnished Finder](./Furnished_Finder.mdx), where you must filter carefully and interrogate every
landlord about cohabitation. Here the whole inventory is entire units by definition. **Zero roommate
risk**, no filtering required. For a charter that names "no roommates" as a hard requirement, that's
a genuine structural advantage.

**Term is right.** 30-day minimum, furnished, utilities included. The product shape matches what Tom
needs.

**It's a CoStar property** — the same company that owns Apartments.com. That means a real database
behind it and real corporate operators, not a scam surface. Payment and process are professional.

**The inventory numbers are not what they look like.** Redmond shows **73 furnished** and Bellevue
**661** — impressive until you know that **the counts are inflated by unit-level duplicates within
single buildings.** One building with 20 available floor plans can appear as 20 listings. The real
number of distinct *buildings* is a small fraction of 661. Don't read those counts as market depth.

**The real problem is price, and you can't even see it.** **No prices are published.** Every property
sits behind a per-property inquiry form — you submit contact details and wait for someone to email
you a quote. That's a deliberate design: it's built for corporate relocation accounts where a company
is paying, not for an individual comparing options. The estimate for an Eastside 1BR at 1–2 months is
**$3,000–6,000/month**, and **budget fit is doubtful**. The bottom of that range clears $3,300 by
$300; the middle and top don't come close.

**Expect the upsell.** These operators want 3+ month terms, because that's where their margin lives.
A 30-day inquiry will very likely come back with either a 3-month quote or a 30-day quote priced to
push you toward 3 months. Have the answer ready: **30 days, possibly 60, nothing longer.**

**The honest verdict:** this is a legitimate channel with the right product shape and the wrong price
point, sold through a process designed for someone else's budget. Submit one inquiry — it costs
nothing but an email address — but put [Landing](./Landing.mdx) and
[Furnished Finder](./Furnished_Finder.mdx) ahead of it.

## The price math

**Every number here is an ESTIMATE. CorporateHousing.com publishes no prices at all.**

| Scenario | 30 days | 60 days |
|---|---|---|
| Optimistic (low end of estimate) | ~$3,000 | ~$6,000 |
| Mid estimate | ~$4,500 | ~$9,000 |
| Pessimistic (high end) | ~$6,000 | ~$12,000 |

Against **$3,300/month**:

* **Only the very bottom of the range clears budget** — by about $300/month.
* **The mid case is ~$1,200/month over.** The high case is ~$2,700/month over.
* A 60-day stay at the mid estimate runs **~$9,000 — roughly $2,400 over a $6,600 two-month budget.**

Utilities are included, which is real value — no accounts to open, no separate bills. But it doesn't
close a $1,200/month gap.

**For comparison:** [Landing](./Landing.mdx) publishes **$1,767–3,223** for real Redmond units, and
the *entire* range fits under budget. That comparison is why this page is not a priority.

## How to book

**There's no booking — there's an inquiry. Phone: 888-539-1150.**

1. **Go to https://www.corporatehousing.com/wa/redmond** and browse Redmond, then Bellevue.
2. **Ignore the listing counts.** Look at distinct *addresses*, not listings — the 73/661 figures are
   padded with unit-level duplicates from the same buildings.
3. **Call 888-539-1150 rather than filling in the form**, if you want an answer this week. The form is
   built for corporate accounts and routes accordingly.
4. **Lead with the constraint, not the question:** *"I need 30 days, possibly 60, for one person,
   under $3,300 all-in, in Redmond or East Bellevue. Do you have anything, yes or no?"* This
   short-circuits the upsell and gets you a real answer fast.
5. **Refuse the 3-month pitch.** It will come. A 3-month lease defeats the entire purpose — Tom is
   looking for a long-term place and does not want to be committed anywhere.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* **"What is the all-in monthly total for 30 nights and for 60 nights?"** — not the nightly rate, not
  "starting from."
* "Is parking included, or an add-on?" *(Unknown on this platform — get the number.)*
* "Is there a booking fee, service fee, or cleaning fee on top of rent?"
* "What's your actual minimum term — is 30 days available at this property, or is 3 months the real
  floor?"
* "Is there an elevator? Is a ground-floor unit available?" *(Tom is a senior.)*
* "Is a security deposit required, and how much?"
