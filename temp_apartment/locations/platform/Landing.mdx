## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Budget: it fits, and almost nothing else furnished does.** Landing's published Eastside range is
$1,767–3,223/month. The top of that range is $77 *under* budget and the bottom is roughly half of it.
Compare that to [Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx), whose Redmond estimate starts at $3,500
and runs to $5,000. Landing is the only national furnished platform in this entire search that both
serves Redmond and prices under the ceiling. That combination is why it ranks where it does.

**Term, privacy, and cost structure: all correct.** 30-day minimum. An entire private apartment — no
roommates. The model is unusual and favorable: a **$199/YEAR membership** (a year, not a month —
this is frequently misread), **no security deposit**, **no application fee**, and utilities, WiFi,
and cleaning included in rent. Membership is transferable between cities. For a 1–2 month stay,
skipping a deposit and an application fee is worth several hundred dollars on its own.

**The structural quirk you must understand before searching.** `hellolanding.com/s/redmond-wa`
**404s.** Redmond is not a Landing market. The buildings are filed under the **Seattle metro** — the
unit URLs literally read `apartment-in-seattle-olde-redmond-place`. So if you search "Redmond" on
Landing's site, you will conclude they have nothing in Redmond. They do. **You must search
Seattle/Bellevue and filter by map**, not search Redmond. This one fact is why Landing gets
overlooked.

**The red flag — and it is serious for a senior.** **Every specific unit URL 404'd within the
research session.** Inventory churns in days, and nothing on the site is reliably bookable ahead.
Worse, BBB and Trustpilot show a recurring pattern of:

* **Reservation bumping** — you're booked, then you aren't.
* **Cancellations two weeks before move-in**, with pricier substitutes offered as the fix.
* **Lockbox and key failures** — a January 2026 guest was **stranded outside in the cold for an
  hour**.

For a business traveler, that's an annoyance. For Tom, arriving with suitcases and nowhere to go, or
locked out on a cold evening, it's a genuine problem. It does not disqualify Landing — the price and
location advantage is too large — but it dictates *how* you book: **by phone, with a human, on a
named unit, confirmed in writing.**

## The price math

Using Landing's **published** range:

|---|---|---|
| Low end ($1,767/mo) | $1,767 | $3,534 |
| High end ($3,223/mo) | $3,223 | $6,446 |

Plus the **$199/year membership**, paid once:

| Scenario | 30 days all-in | 60 days all-in |
|---|---|---|
| Low end | ~$1,966 | ~$3,733 |
| High end | ~$3,422 | ~$6,645 |

Against **$3,300/month**: the low end clears by roughly **$1,500/month**. Even the high end, once
the one-time $199 is amortized across two months, lands at about **$3,322/month** — within $22 of
budget. **No security deposit and no application fee** means the move-in cash outlay is just rent
plus the membership.

This is the only furnished option in the search where the arithmetic isn't a stretch.

## How to book

**Call 415-231-1701. Do not rely on the website.**

1. **Do not search "Redmond" on the site** — it 404s. Search Seattle or Bellevue and filter by map,
   or just describe the location to the person on the phone.
2. **Ask a human to HOLD a specific unit** for your dates. This is the whole point of calling. The
   website's inventory is gone within days.
3. **Get the confirmation in writing** — the specific unit address and number, the exact dates, the
   all-in monthly total.
4. **Ask directly about the bumping policy:** *"If Landing cancels or reassigns my unit, what
   happens, and how much notice do I get?"* Their record here is poor; get the answer before paying.
5. **Confirm the key/entry method** and what the backup is if the lockbox fails on arrival. Ask for
   a phone number that reaches a human after hours.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* "Which specific units do you have in **Redmond** for my dates?" (They're filed under Seattle —
  the agent will know.)
* "Is the $199 membership annual, and is it charged once?"
* "Confirm: no security deposit and no application fee?"
* "Is parking included at this specific building, or extra?"
* "Is there an elevator? Can I get a ground-floor unit?"
