## Why this is a fit

**The term is the whole point — this is the rare option where 30 days isn't a negotiation.**
Furnished Finder is the travel-nurse marketplace. Its entire customer base is people on 13-week
contracts who need a furnished place for a month or three and then leave. Every landlord on the
platform is there *because* they want short-term tenants. Contrast that with nearly every
conventional building in this search, where a 1–2 month stay is an exception you have to beg for and
pay a premium for. Here it's the default. That structural alignment is worth more than any single
price on the page.

**Budget: comfortable, with real headroom.** $1,450–3,500 published — most of the range sits under
$3,300. One documented example: **a Redmond 1BR/2BA at $1,500/month with utilities, WiFi, and laundry
included.** That is less than half budget, in the preferred city, furnished, on a 30-day term. Even
if that specific unit is gone, it establishes that the bottom of this market is real and not a
teaser.

**Inventory is genuinely deep across all three target cities:** Redmond 72 listings / 21 available,
Bellevue 103 / 44, Kirkland 156 / 50. That's 115 available units across the target geography — more
than any other single channel found.

**It's free for renters.** Landlords pay $199/year; Tom pays nothing to search or contact. Compare
[Sublet.com's](../sublet/Sublet_com.mdx) pay-to-contact model.

## The two caveats — read these before contacting anyone

**1. It MIXES entire units with private rooms in occupied homes.** This is the important one. The
charter is explicit: **no roommates, no cohabitation.** Furnished Finder's listings include private
bedrooms inside homes where the owner still lives. The listing photos and titles do not always make
this obvious. **You must filter to entire place, and then confirm with every single landlord,
directly, that nobody else lives in the unit.** Ask it as a plain question: *"Will anyone else be
living in the home during the stay?"* Do not infer it from the listing.

**2. Payment is off-platform — landlord to tenant — so there is NO escrow protection.** Furnished
Finder is a listings board, not a transaction platform. It does not hold funds, does not mediate
disputes, and does not guarantee the unit exists. That is a meaningfully different risk profile from
Airbnb or Blueground. The mitigation is simple and non-negotiable: **verify the unit exists in person
before sending money.** Walk it, or have someone walk it. Never wire or send funds to a landlord for
a place nobody has seen.

Neither caveat is disqualifying. Both are the price of getting a private Redmond 1BR at $1,500 with a
30-day term. But they turn this from a click-to-book into a channel that requires legwork — and for a
senior, that legwork should probably be Bryan's, not Tom's.

## How to book

**Search first, then call the landlord directly. 877-982-4959 is Furnished Finder's own line.**

1. **Go to https://www.furnishedfinder.com/housing/us--wa--redmond** and filter to **entire place**.
   Then repeat for Bellevue and Kirkland.
2. **Filter out every private-room listing.** If a listing is ambiguous, treat it as a room until the
   landlord says otherwise in writing.
3. **Contact landlords directly** — the platform hands you contact info; it is not an intermediary.
4. **Ask the cohabitation question explicitly** of every landlord (script below).
5. **See the unit in person before paying anything.** There is no escrow. This step is not optional.
6. **Get the all-in monthly total in writing**: rent + utilities + WiFi + parking + any cleaning or
   pet fee, for 30 days and for 60 days.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* **"Will anyone else be living in the home during my stay?"** — the disqualifying question. Ask it
  every time.
* **"Is this the entire unit, with a private entrance, private kitchen, and private bathroom?"**
* "Are utilities, WiFi, and laundry included, or billed separately?"
* "Is parking included?"
* "What's the shortest term you'll write? Can I do 30 days, and can I extend to 60?"
* "Is there an elevator, or is the unit on the ground floor?" *(Tom is a senior — ask.)*
* "Can I see the unit before I pay?" **A "no" here is a red flag. Walk away.**

### Safety

**Never wire, Zelle, Venmo, or gift-card a deposit for a unit nobody has seen.** The overview flags
two confirmed live Craigslist scams in this exact geography, and the rule of thumb applies here too:
**treat any Eastside furnished 1BR under ~$1,800/month as presumptively fraudulent until seen in
person.** The documented $1,500 Redmond unit is below that line — which doesn't mean it's fake, it
means **go look at it**.
