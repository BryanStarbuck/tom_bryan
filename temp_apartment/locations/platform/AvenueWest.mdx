## Why this isn't a fit

**On paper this should have been near the top of the list.** AvenueWest is a franchise network of
furnished corporate condos: **30-day minimum, all utilities included, entire private units, no
roommates.** That is precisely the shape of what Tom needs — the same structure that makes
[Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx) and [Landing](./Landing.mdx) work, from an operator that's
been doing it a long time.

**It fails on one thing, and it's fatal: there is no active Washington franchise.** AvenueWest runs on
independently owned local franchises, and **the Eastside doesn't have one.** Without a franchisee,
there is no inventory, no local agent, and no phone number to call.

**"Bellevue" is an SEO location page, not a market.** AvenueWest's site surfaces a Bellevue page in
search results. **There is nothing behind it** — no active franchise, no units. This is the same trap
as [Anyplace's](./Anyplace.mdx) Seattle waitlist page: a URL that looks like a market and isn't. If a
future search turns up "AvenueWest Bellevue," that page is the reason it looks promising and the
reason it's worthless.

**What little exists is in the wrong place.** Roughly **one Seattle property**, ~30 minutes to
Redmond — outside the 20-minute rule even if it were available.

**The lesson worth keeping:** the month-to-month + all-utilities structure is the right thing to look
for. Two operators actually deliver it on the Eastside — [Landing](./Landing.mdx) (published prices,
fits budget) and [Blueground](../blueground/Blueground.mdx) (deep Redmond inventory, no published
price). Those are where this energy should go.
