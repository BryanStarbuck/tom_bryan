## Why this isn't a fit

**Anyplace does not serve Seattle — verified live.** The company operates in exactly four markets:
**New York City, San Francisco, Los Angeles, and Tokyo.** Washington state is not one of them.

**The Seattle URL is the trap here, and it's worth being precise about it.** There *is* a page at
`anyplace.com/corporate-housing/united-states/seattle`. It looks like a market page. It is **a
waitlist signup form** — Anyplace collects your email against a city it might enter someday. There is
no inventory behind it, no units, no prices, and nothing to book. A future search that finds that URL
will look like it found a Seattle option. It didn't.

Even a real waitlist wouldn't help: Tom needs somewhere to live for one to two months **starting this
summer**, not a notification if a company expands.
