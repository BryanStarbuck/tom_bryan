## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Inventory is the problem — this is basically one building.** Blueground's Kirkland presence is
much thinner than Redmond (23+ units) or Bellevue (176). It is mostly **The 101 in Moss Bay**. JADE
Residences is unavailable until January 2027, which is irrelevant to a stay starting this summer. So
in practice, "Blueground Kirkland" means "The 101, if it has something open."

**But The 101 is a genuinely good building for Tom.** It's a 66-unit boutique property in downtown
Kirkland with accessible units, and it is one of the most walkable addresses in the whole search —
which matters a great deal for a senior who may prefer not to drive for every errand. South Kirkland
is third on the charter's preference list, and 15 minutes to Redmond is comfortably inside the
20-minute rule.

**This building is reachable three different ways — that's the useful insight.** The same address
appears three times in this search under three different channels:

* **Blueground** (this page) — furnished, 30-night minimum.
* **[Landing — The 101](../platform/Landing_The_101.mdx)** — furnished, 30-day minimum, and Landing's
  pricing is dramatically lower than Blueground's. Walk Score 96.
* **[The 101 (direct lease)](../apartment/The_101_Kirkland.mdx)** — 12–15 months only, which **fails**
  the term requirement outright, but tells you what the unfurnished market rate is ($2,428–5,798).

If Tom likes this building, **check Landing first** — same apartment, likely much cheaper.

**Budget: the studio is the only realistic shot.** The estimate is $3,000–4,790/month. The high end
is not a guess: **a 2BR here was quoted $4,790/month as the LOWEST price on a 12-month stay** — the
most heavily discounted tier Blueground offers. A 1–2 month stay prices *above* that curve, because
their policy is "the longer you commit, the more you save." So the 1BRs (Jul 30, Aug 8) are almost
certainly out of reach. **The studio available Jul 25 — #4564A, 566 sqft — is the budget shot**, and
even it is not likely to land under $3,300.

Against **$3,300/month**: only the **optimistic studio case clears budget**, and it clears it by
about $300. Everything else is over — the 2BR by roughly $1,500/month.

Note carefully what the $4,790 figure is: it is the **lowest** price for that unit, on the **longest**
commitment. A 30-day booking of that same 2BR would price higher. Do not read $4,790 as a ceiling.

* **Security deposit** — **waived if you pay via Plaid instant bank transfer.**
* **Credit card surcharge** — avoid.

## How to book

**Call 1-917-259-1222.**

1. **Mention the prior stay and ask for a returning-guest rate.** The family has used Blueground
   before — use it.
2. **Ask specifically about the studio at The 101 (#4564A, 566 sqft, available Jul 25).** Skip
   straight to it. The 1BRs are out of budget range.
3. **Before you book, price the same building through
   [Landing](../platform/Landing_The_101.mdx).** Same address, same product, materially cheaper.
   This is the cheapest possible piece of due diligence.
4. **Get the all-in total in writing** for 30 and 60 nights — rent + booking fee + parking.
5. **Pay via Plaid** to waive the deposit.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* **"What is the exact cancellation policy for my dates?"** 15+ days out = **half a month's rent**;
  under 15 days = a **full month's rent**. A late cancellation on a 1-month stay costs the whole stay.
* **"Is there an elevator, and is the bathroom entry step-free?"** Blueground has no senior program;
  only Avalon Bellevue is confirmed accessible. The 101's direct listings mention accessible units —
  confirm the *specific* unit.
* **"What is the move-out deep-cleaning charge?"**

### Red flags

Trustpilot shows a documented pattern of **surprise deposit demands after booking**, **arbitrary
move-out deep-cleaning charges** with thin photo justification, and **unresponsive support during
disputes**. Pay via **Plaid** so there's no deposit to argue over, and **photograph every room at
move-in AND move-out**.
