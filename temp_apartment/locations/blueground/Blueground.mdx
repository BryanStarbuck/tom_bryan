exactly the 1–2 month stay Tom needs. Call, but call with the estimate in mind, not the hope.**

## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Term and privacy: perfect.** The 30-night minimum is Blueground's actual product, not a penalty
tier bolted onto a 12-month lease. That distinction matters enormously here. Nearly every
conventional Redmond building in this search treats a short stay as an exception it will grudgingly
price at a premium; Blueground treats it as the normal case. Tom gets an entire private apartment —
no roommates, no cohabitation, no shared anything. Utilities, WiFi, furnishings, and move-out
cleaning are bundled into the rent, which means no utility accounts to open and close, no furniture
to rent, and no second move. For a 1–2 month stay, that bundling is worth real money against a
"cheaper" unfurnished unit.

**Location: the best in the search.** 23+ Redmond units across Everlight, Milehouse, Allez, Bell
River Trail, and Avalon ParcSquare in and around downtown, plus Avalon Esterra Park and Eaves
Redmond Campus in Overlake, The Piper in SE Redmond, and Cru at Willows. No other furnished
operator has this depth in Redmond proper. Drive time to Redmond is zero because he'd already be
there.

**Budget: this is where it likely falls apart.** Blueground publishes zero prices. Every listing
says "add dates to see prices," and the rate comes from a dynamic-pricing engine. Their own pricing
policy states plainly that *"the longer you commit, the more you save"* — which means a 30–60 day
stay sits at the **worst end** of that curve, paying the highest per-month rate Blueground charges.
The only hard data points found anywhere in this research: a Kirkland 2BR at $4,790/month as the
**lowest** price on a **12-month** stay, and a third party quoting a Bellevue 1BR at roughly
$4,734–4,787. Both of those are the *discounted* long-stay end. A 1–2 month Redmond 1BR realistically
lands **$3,500–5,000/month**. The odds of clearing $3,300 are low.

**The honest read:** everything about Blueground fits except the number, and the number is the
constraint. Call anyway — dynamic pricing cuts both ways, and a returning-guest rate on a
low-demand unit is the one scenario where this clears budget.

**Costs on top of rent, which the estimate above does not include:**

* **Booking / service fee** — a percentage of the booking, capped at one month's rent. On a 1-month

* **Credit card surcharge** — paying by card adds a fee. Don't.
* **Parking** — extra fee, not included in rent.

## How to book

**Call 1-917-259-1222. Do not book online.** The website will only give you the algorithm's price.

1. **Mention the prior stay.** The family has booked with Blueground before. Say so, up front, and
   **ask for a returning-guest rate.** This is the single highest-leverage thing on this page.
2. **Give exact dates.** Nothing can be priced without them. Dynamic pricing means a July 16 start
   and an August 1 start can differ by hundreds.
3. **Ask for the all-in total in writing** — rent + booking/service fee + parking + any pet fee,
   for 30 nights and for 60 nights. Not the nightly rate. Not "starting from."
4. **Pay via Plaid instant bank transfer.** This waives the security deposit. A credit card adds a
   surcharge.
5. **Ask about the smallest units first** — Avalon Esterra Park #507 (525 sqft) and The Piper #832
   (575 sqft). Studios and small 1BRs are the only realistic path under $3,300.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* **"What is the exact cancellation policy for my dates?"** This is harsh and you need it in writing.
  15+ days before start = **half a month's rent**. Under 15 days = a **full month's rent**. On a
  1-month booking, canceling late means paying for the entire stay and getting nothing. If Tom's
  plans are not firm, this is a real risk.
* **"Is there an elevator?"** Blueground has no senior program of any kind. Across all three cities,
  only Avalon Bellevue (11000 NE 10th St) is confirmed to have elevator, concierge, and secured
  garage. For Redmond buildings you must ask, per building.
* **"Is the bathroom entry step-free?"** Ask before booking, not after.
* **"What is the move-out cleaning charge and when is it assessed?"**

### Red flags to protect against

Trustpilot shows a recurring pattern that matters more for a senior than for a corporate traveler:

* **Surprise deposit demands after booking** — get the all-in total in writing first.
* **Arbitrary move-out "deep cleaning" charges** with thin photo justification.
* **Unresponsive support during disputes.**

**Mitigations, all cheap:** pay via Plaid (no deposit to argue about), and **photograph the entire
unit at move-in AND at move-out** — timestamped, every room, every surface. That photo set is the
only thing that reliably defeats a fabricated cleaning charge.
