## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Term and privacy: perfect, same as everywhere in Blueground's network.** 30 nights is the real
minimum, not a penalty tier. Tom gets an entire private apartment with utilities, WiFi, furnishings,
and move-out cleaning bundled into one rent number. No roommates, no shared space, no furniture
rental, no utility accounts.

**Inventory: by far the best.** 176 units in Bellevue versus 23+ in Redmond and a handful in
Kirkland. If Tom's dates are awkward or he needs something specific, Bellevue is where Blueground
can actually produce options. That said, these buildings sit in West Bellevue, Northwest Bellevue,
and Wilburton — **downtown-adjacent, not East Bellevue**, which is the sub-area the charter targets.
It's a location compromise, though a modest one at 13 minutes to Redmond.

**The senior angle — this page's one real advantage.** Blueground has no senior program anywhere.
But **Avalon Bellevue (11000 NE 10th St) is the ONLY building across all three cities confirmed to
have an elevator, a concierge, and a secured garage.** For an older resident staying somewhere
unfamiliar for two months, a concierge is not a luxury amenity — it's a person at a desk who signs
for packages, lets in a locked-out resident, and notices if something is wrong. That is worth
paying something for. If Blueground ends up being the answer, **this is the building to push for.**

**Budget: worse here than in Redmond.** The estimate is $3,500–5,500/month, and it is an estimate —
Blueground publishes nothing. The one real-world data point found: **a third party quoted a Bellevue
1BR at roughly $4,734–4,787/month.** That is roughly **$1,400–1,500 over budget**, and it likely
reflects a longer commitment than 1–2 months, since Blueground's own policy is "the longer you
commit, the more you save." A 30–60 day stay sits at the worst end of that curve. Realistically,
Bellevue does not clear $3,300.

The cheapest path here is a studio, not a 1BR. The ~$4,760 figure is a 1BR; nothing indicates what a
Bellevue studio costs, and that gap is worth one phone call.

## How to book

**Call 1-917-259-1222.** Do not rely on the website — it only shows the algorithm's price and gives
you no room to negotiate.

1. **Mention the prior stay and ask for a returning-guest rate.** The family has booked Blueground
   before. This is the highest-leverage move available.
2. **Ask for Avalon Bellevue (11000 NE 10th St) by name.** It is the only confirmed
   elevator + concierge + secured garage building.
3. **Give exact dates**, then ask for the **all-in total in writing** for 30 nights and for 60
   nights — rent + booking/service fee + parking + pet fee, not the nightly rate.
4. **Ask what a studio costs**, not just a 1BR. The only quote we have is for a 1BR.
5. **Pay via Plaid instant bank transfer** to waive the deposit.

### When you call — ask these specifically

* **"What is the exact cancellation policy for my dates?"** 15+ days before start = **half a month's
  rent**; under 15 days = a **full month's rent**. Canceling a 1-month stay late means paying for
  all of it.
* **"Is the bathroom entry step-free?"** Confirm before booking.
* **"Is the concierge staffed 24/7 or business hours only?"** This is the reason to pick this
  building — find out what you're actually getting.
* **"What is the move-out deep-cleaning charge?"**

### Red flags

Trustpilot shows a recurring, documented pattern: **surprise deposit demands after booking**,
**arbitrary move-out "deep cleaning" charges** backed by thin photo evidence, and **unresponsive
support during disputes**. For a senior handling this alone, those are worse than they sound.

**Do two things:** pay via **Plaid** (there's then no deposit to fight over), and **photograph every
room at move-in AND move-out**. Timestamped photos are the only reliable defense against a
fabricated cleaning charge.
