## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and Redmond Place passes.** 2–12 month terms. Along with [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx), it's one of only two buildings in Redmond proper that will write a lease as short as Tom's actual need. Call both. Compare what they quote.

**Budget works on paper.** A 1BR at $2,250 leaves $1,050/month of headroom under $3,300. But note there are **no studios** — the cheapest door here is a 1BR, so the floor is $200 higher than The Luke's.

**The short-term fee is unpublished, and it's the deciding number.** The listing says short leases carry extra fees but gives no amount. That is the pattern across every building in this search. Treat $300–600/month as an **estimate**, not a quote. Nothing on this page is real until a leasing agent says a dollar figure.

**Availability is the specific problem here.** Units skew to **August–September 2026**. If Tom needs to move in mid-July or early August, this building may simply not have a door open. That makes the availability question more urgent than the price question — ask it first, and don't spend time negotiating a unit that doesn't exist on his date.

**Privacy and location are clean.** Private apartment, downtown Redmond, zero drive.

## The real cost for 1-2 months

Working a 1BR at $2,250, for a 2-month stay:

| Line item | Monthly | 2 months |
|---|---|---|
| Base rent (1BR) | $2,250 | $4,500 |
| Short-term premium — **ESTIMATE, not a quote** | $300–600 | $600–1,200 |
| Utilities (not published — assume not included) | ~$150–250 | $300–500 |
| Furniture rental — [CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) | $300+ | $600+ |
| **Estimated all-in** | **~$3,000–3,400/mo** | **~$6,000–6,800** |

**That top end crosses $3,300.** Redmond Place clears budget only if the short-term premium comes in at the low end. It is not a comfortable margin.

**Unfurnished is a trap for 60 days.** [CORT's Redmond showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) (2118 148th Ave NE, 425-242-5435) quotes $240–295/month for a 1BR package, but **that is the 12-month rate** — a 1–2 month rental prices higher, plus delivery and pickup. Then two moves and two utility account setups for a stay measured in weeks.

That whole stack is why extended-stay hotels frequently beat an apartment at this duration: [Extended Stay America Seattle-Redmond](../hotel/Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) and [WoodSpring Suites Redmond](../hotel/WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) come furnished, with a kitchen, no lease, and no utility accounts.

## When you call

Call **718-612-7681**. Note the area code is a 718 (New York) number — likely a call center or listing-service line, so push to reach the on-site leasing office.

1. **What do you actually have available on Tom's move-in date?** Ask this first — the listing shows Aug–Sep. If nothing is open when he needs it, stop here.
2. **What is the exact extra fee on a 2-month lease?** Get a dollar amount, not "it varies."
3. Confirm 2 months is genuinely writable, not a best-case on the listing site.
4. Are utilities included, billed via RUBS, or set up by the resident?
5. **What is the income requirement, and is asset-based qualification available for a retiree on a fixed income?**
6. Elevator? Ground-floor unit available?
7. Deposit, application fee, admin fee, parking fee.
