## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and here it is genuinely winnable.** The published term is 12
months, which taken at face value is a hard fail — **12 × $2,325 = $27,900 for a 2-month
need**. But the site says "flexible," and the surrounding facts matter more than the
published default:

This is a **brand-new lease-up giving away up to 8 weeks free** with many units sitting
empty. That combination is the single strongest negotiating position a renter can walk
into. A stabilized building with 3 vacancies has no reason to write Tom a 3-month lease. A
lease-up burning money on empty units, whose leasing team is measured on occupancy this
quarter, has every reason. **This is the place to make the hard ask.** Not because they
advertise it — they do not — but because their incentives are aligned with saying yes in a
way that no other building's are.

**The distance is a real, hard advantage.** ~9 minutes to Redmond makes Modera the closest
Kirkland building in this entire set — closer than most of *Bellevue*. If the point of the
stay is being near Redmond while Tom hunts for a permanent place, this beats every other
Kirkland address on the list, including the ones with better published terms.

**Budget is workable but not roomy.** $2,325 at the studio/1BR end leaves ~$975/month under
the $3,300 ceiling. Parking is **not listed as included**, which on a new building often
means $75–200/month for a garage stall — enough to matter. And a lease-up's headline rents
are usually *net effective* numbers that assume the 8-week concession, which is exactly the
concession Tom cannot use on a short term.

**That last point deserves emphasis, because it is a trap.** "Up to 8 weeks free" almost
certainly requires a 12-month lease. If Tom takes a 3-month term, the concession likely
evaporates and the *gross* rent applies — which may be meaningfully higher than the
advertised $2,325. Ask what the rent is **without** the concession before celebrating.

**Geography is acceptable.** Bridle Trails is South Kirkland — the third-choice area behind
Redmond and East Bellevue, but in the target zone. Private apartments, no roommates.

## The real cost for 1–2 months

**All of this is conditional on winning a short term. If they hold at 12 months, this page
is over — a 12-month-only building is not a fit for a 2-month stay.**

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Base rent (studio/1BR) | $2,325 *(likely a net-effective number assuming the 8-week concession — ask for the gross rent)* |
| **Short-term premium** | **NOT published.** Estimate **$300–600/mo** — an **ESTIMATE**, not a quote |
| Lost concession on a short term | Potentially significant — **ask what the rent is without it** |
| Utilities | Not published — assume **$100–200/mo. Estimate.** |
| Parking | Not listed as included — **ask.** Assume **$75–200/mo** on a new building. **Estimate.** |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; a 1–2 month rental prices **higher**, plus delivery, pickup, and two moves |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$3,000–3,600** |

Note that this crosses $3,300 at the pessimistic end — driven by the unbundled parking and
the concession that probably does not survive a short lease. Modera is the newest and
nicest building on this list, and it prices like it.

**Short-term premiums are never published.** Every listing in this market says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Modera does not
publish even that. The estimate above is exactly that.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility setup. The [CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)
is in Redmond at 2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435), but their $240–295 quote is the
12-month rate; 1–2 months prices higher.

## When you call

Call **425-655-0095**. **Ask for the leasing manager or the community manager — not a
leasing consultant.** A consultant reads the 12-month policy off a screen. A manager has
authority to make an exception, and on a lease-up they are motivated to.

Make the ask directly and give them the reason to say yes:

1. **"You have a lot of units and you're giving 8 weeks free. I'd like a 3-month lease at
   full rent, no concession, move-in [Tom's date]. Can you do that?"** Full rent on a short
   term is a good trade for them — they collect real money now on a unit sitting empty. Lead
   with that framing, not with "do you do short-term?"
2. **"If not 3 months, what is the shortest you'll write, and at what premium?"**
3. **"What is the *gross* rent — the rent without the 8-week concession?"** This is the
   number that will actually apply. The advertised $2,325 probably is not.
4. **"Is parking included? What does a stall cost?"** Not listed as included.
5. **"Which utilities are included, and which are billed separately?"**
6. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published, though a
   new build makes an elevator likely. Confirm it.
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published. New
   luxury lease-ups commonly want 3× rent, which is a real barrier for a retiree — ask about
   asset-based qualification before an application fee is spent.
8. If they say no: ask again in two weeks. Lease-up concessions deepen as quarter-end
   approaches, and so does flexibility.
