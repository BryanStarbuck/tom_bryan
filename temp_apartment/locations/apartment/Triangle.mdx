## Why this isn't a fit

**The term is the crux, and 6 months is triple the need.** Tom wants 1–2 months. Triangle's floor is 6. That is **6 × $2,482 = $14,892 for a 2-month stay** — about **$9,928 of rent for four months Tom never sets foot in**. No amenity, location, or concession closes a gap that size.

Compare: [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx) is in the same downtown Redmond neighborhood, will write **2 months**, and starts at a **$2,050 studio** — $4,100 of base rent. Triangle costs roughly **3.6× as much** for the same 2 months of actual living, in the same part of town.

**The base rent is also high for the set.** $2,482 is the most expensive floor of any Redmond building here except the furnished ones, and there are no studios, so a 1BR is the cheapest door.

**Everything else is moot,** but for the record: unfurnished, so add [CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) furniture, delivery, two moves, and utility accounts. Short-term premium unpublished as always — an **estimated** $300–600/month, but irrelevant when the term is wrong by four months. Privacy and distance are fine.

**The one mild positive:** the 2.5× income test is easier than the 3× at [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx). That's not enough to overcome the term.
