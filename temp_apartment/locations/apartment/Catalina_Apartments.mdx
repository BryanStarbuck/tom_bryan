## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and Catalina is one of only two buildings in this whole search
that solves it.** Nearly every conventional apartment building on the Eastside writes a
5-, 6-, or 12-month minimum, which means Tom pays for months he does not need. Catalina
publishes month-to-month — and states terms as short as 3 nights. That is not a
negotiation you have to win. It is the advertised product. That single fact outweighs
almost everything else on this page.

**Budget is not close.** At $1,495–1,895 against a $3,300 ceiling, Catalina leaves
$1,400–1,800/month of headroom. That headroom is what pays for furniture rental and any
short-term premium, and there is still room left over.

**The bundled utilities are worth real money.** Most buildings quote base rent and then
add electric, gas, water, sewer, trash, internet, and often parking on top — easily
$150–300/month, plus the hassle of opening and closing utility accounts for a 60-day
stay. Catalina's number is close to an all-in number. For a 2-month stay, avoiding four
account setups and four account closures is worth more than it sounds.

**It is in the preferred area.** Wilburton / Eastgate is East Bellevue, which is the
second-choice geography after Redmond itself, and ~18 minutes to Redmond keeps it inside
the 20-minute rule. Privacy is not a question — these are conventional private apartments,
no roommates.

**The honest caveats:** it is a 1970s property, so expect dated finishes. The CSV marks
it only "Partial" on senior-friendliness and does not confirm an elevator — for a senior
that is a real question, not a detail. And "Partial" furnished means most units are
unfurnished; the furnished corporate suites are a small subset and their price is not
published.

Even taking the pessimistic end, Path B lands roughly $1,000/month under budget. That is
the widest margin of any apartment in this search.

**One caveat that applies to every apartment page:** short-term premiums are essentially
never published. Listings say only that "leases shorter than 12 months often include
additional fees," with no amount. Estimate **$300–600/month** if one applies here —
marked clearly as an **estimate**. Catalina is the one property where it plausibly does
not apply at all, since month-to-month *is* the policy. Get that in writing.

## When you call

Call the leasing office at **425-746-5400**. Ask for the leasing manager, not whoever
picks up — you want someone who can quote a non-standard term.

Ask, in this order:

1. **"You publish month-to-month. Is that available right now, for a 60-day stay starting
   [Tom's date]?"** Get a yes or no before anything else.
2. **"Is there any premium, fee, or rate difference for month-to-month versus a
   12-month lease?"** This is the number nobody publishes. Make them say it.
3. **"Confirm what the rent includes."** Read the list back: all utilities, cable, HBO,
   phone, internet, parking. Confirm nothing is billed separately.
4. **"Do you have any furnished corporate suites available, and what do they rent for?"**
   If yes and it is under ~$2,800, stop researching and take it — that is a turnkey,
   no-furniture, no-utility-setup, month-to-month private apartment in the preferred area.
5. **"Is there an elevator? Can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** 1970s garden-style
   properties frequently have no elevator. This matters.
6. **"What is the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Many buildings want
   3× rent in income, which trips up a retiree. At $1,495 the bar is lower than anywhere
   else, but ask about asset-based qualification anyway.
7. Confirm: application fee $45, deposit $400–600, pet policy (1 pet, 60 lb max).
