## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and normally a 5-month floor is where a page ends.** **5 × $1,950 =
$9,750 for a 2-month need** — three months, roughly $5,850, paid for nothing. That is the
standard verdict for every 5- and 6-month building on this list.

**The concession is what makes this one different.** Up to **10 weeks free** is about 2.3
months of rent given back. On a 5-month lease at $1,950, ten weeks free is roughly **$4,500
off**, which brings the effective cost of the whole 5-month term down to around **$5,250** —
or about **$1,050/month effective across five months.** Look at what that does to the waste
calculation: the three unwanted months now cost roughly $3,150 effective instead of $5,850.
The concession does not eliminate the problem, but it is the only building in this search
where the giveaway meaningfully offsets the unwanted term.

Be careful with that math on the call. Concessions are usually structured as free weeks
*applied to a 12-month lease*, and a 5-month term may qualify for a smaller giveaway or none
at all. **Ask what the concession is specifically on a 5-month lease.** If the answer is
"the 10 weeks requires 12 months," the whole argument for this page collapses and it becomes
an ordinary 5-month building you should skip.

**Budget is the best in Kirkland.** $1,950 is the cheapest Kirkland 1BR found. Against a
$3,300 ceiling that is $1,350/month of headroom before any concession.

**The income requirement is a real risk: 3× rent.** On $1,950 that is **$5,850/month of
income** — a wall for a retiree. Ask about asset-based qualification before spending an
application fee. [Vue at Kirkland](./Vue_at_Kirkland.mdx) at 2× and
[Westwater](./Westwater.mdx) at 2.5× are the more forgiving options if income is what binds.

**Geography is the weak spot.** Forbes Creek is central Kirkland — not South Kirkland, not
the preferred zone. ~16 minutes to Redmond stays inside the 20-minute rule, but it is
noticeably farther than [Modera](./Modera_Bridle_Trails.mdx) (~9) or
[The Pine](./The_Pine.mdx) (~10). **40 units with immediate move-ins** is a genuine plus:
deep inventory means flexibility on dates and leverage on terms.

**The number that decides it — total commitment for a 2-month need:**

| Scenario | Math | Total |
|---|---|---|
| 5 months, **no** concession | 5 × ~$2,550–3,050 | **~$12,750–15,250** |
| 5 months, **10 weeks free applies** | ~$4,500 off the rent | **~$8,250–10,750** |
| For comparison — [Larkspur Landing](../hotel/Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx), 2 months, no lease | 2 × $2,550 | **~$5,100** |

Even in the best concession case, this costs roughly **$3,000–5,600 more** than simply
booking a furnished extended-stay suite for the two months Tom actually needs. The
concession makes the 5-month term *tolerable*, not *good*. That is the honest read.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Every estimate above
carries that unknown.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a short stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility setup. The [CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) is
in Redmond at 2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435); their $240–295 quote is the 12-month rate
and 1–2 months prices higher.

## When you call

Call **425-332-7512**. Ask for the leasing manager — the concession question needs someone
with authority.

1. **"What concession applies to a 5-month lease?"** This is the entire page. If 10 weeks
   free requires 12 months, say thank you and hang up.
2. **"You have 40 units and immediate move-ins. Will you write 2 or 3 months?"** Deep
   vacancy is leverage. Make the ask the way you would at
   [Modera](./Modera_Bridle_Trails.mdx): full rent, short term, money now.
3. **"What is the short-term premium in dollars per month?"**
4. **"Income is 3× rent. Tom is retired — do you qualify on assets?"** Ask early, before an
   application fee.
5. **"If Tom leaves at month 2, what does early termination cost, and is any concession
   clawed back?"** Free-rent concessions almost always come with a clawback clause. Read it.
6. **"Which utilities are included? Is the carport included?"**
7. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
