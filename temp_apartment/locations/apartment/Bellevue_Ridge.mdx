## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term works, and that is why this ranks so high.** Nearly every other apartment in
this search demands 5, 6, or 12 months. Bellevue Ridge publishes an *MTM flat fee* — a
specific line item for month-to-month tenancy. Properties do not publish a fee for a thing
they do not sell. That fee is the proof. It is also the number you must extract by phone,
because the amount itself is not published.

**Budget is comfortable, even with the unknown fee.** Base rent runs $1,495–2,000 against
a $3,300 ceiling. Even if the MTM flat fee is $500/month, a studio lands around $2,000–2,500
all-in before furniture. There is room here.

**The size is the real problem, and it deserves honesty.** 157–270 sqft is a micro-studio.
For reference, a standard extended-stay hotel studio suite runs roughly 300 sqft with a
full kitchen. At the bottom of that range Tom would be living in a space smaller than most
hotel rooms, unfurnished, for 60 days. That is livable for a young person storing most of
their life elsewhere. For a 60-day stay by a senior, it is a genuine quality-of-life
question, and it is why the [hotel tier](../hotel/Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) competes
hard against this page. Ask which specific units are at 270 sqft and see one in person
before signing.

**On senior fit, this is one of the better buildings in the set.** It has an **elevator** —
which a surprising number of Eastside garden properties do not — and a dedicated **ADA
studio at $2,000**. If Tom has any mobility concern, that ADA unit at $2,000 is arguably a
better buy than a $1,495 walk-up anywhere else. There is **no in-unit laundry**, which over
60 days means trips to a shared laundry room. Ask where it is and whether it is on Tom's
floor.

**Geography is right.** Eastgate / Lake Hills is East Bellevue — the preferred area — and
~18 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule. Private studio, no roommates.

## The real cost for 1–2 months

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Base rent (studio) | $1,495–2,000 (ADA studio $2,000) |
| **MTM flat fee** | **Published as existing, amount NOT published — ask when you call.** Estimate **$300–600/mo** — this is an **ESTIMATE**, not a quote |
| Utilities | "Partial" included — assume **$75–150/mo** for whatever is not. **Estimate.** |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; a 1–2 month rental prices **higher**, plus delivery and pickup |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,100–3,050** |

Even at the pessimistic end it clears $3,300 — but note how much of that estimate rests on
a fee nobody will publish. **Short-term premiums are never published anywhere in this
market.** Listings say only "leases shorter than 12 months often include additional fees."
Every under-budget number on this page is contingent on that unknown. Get the MTM flat fee
in dollars on the phone before you get attached to this option.

There is also a **two months free promo** advertised. Ask directly whether it applies to a
month-to-month tenancy or only to a 12-month lease — concessions on a lease-up almost always
require the long term, and a promo Tom cannot use is worth $0.

**Note the furniture trap.** These are unfurnished. For a 60-day stay you are renting
furniture, paying delivery, moving twice, and setting up utility accounts — for a 200 sqft
room. Price that against a Larkspur Landing suite that needs none of it before deciding.

## When you call

Call **425-864-4997**. Ask for the leasing manager.

1. **"What is the MTM flat fee, in dollars per month?"** This is the whole call. Do not
   let them answer "it depends."
2. **"Can Tom start month-to-month from day one, or only convert after a lease term?"**
   Some properties only offer MTM on *renewal* — which would make this useless for a
   first-time move-in. Nail this down. (This is exactly what disqualifies
   [Aspen Creek](./Aspen_Creek_Apartments.mdx).)
3. **"What is the square footage of the specific unit you'd give Tom?"** 157 and 270 are
   very different apartments. Get the number.
4. **"Is the ADA studio at $2,000 available, and what does ADA mean here?"** Grab bars?
   Roll-in shower? Wider doors? Ground floor?
5. **"Does the two-months-free promo apply to month-to-month, or 12-month only?"**
6. **"Which utilities are included and which are billed separately?"**
7. **"Is parking included, and what does it cost if not?"**
8. **"Where is the laundry, and is it on the same floor as the unit?"**
9. **"What's the income requirement — and do you qualify on assets?"** A retiree may not
   show 3× rent in income. Ask about asset-based qualification.
