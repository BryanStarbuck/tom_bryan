## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The price and the location are the reasons to call.** $1,640 is among the lowest rents in
Crossroads, in the **preferred East Bellevue area**, ~12 minutes to Redmond. Against a
$3,300 ceiling, that base leaves roughly $1,660/month of headroom — enough to absorb a
short-term premium and furniture rental and still finish well under budget. Studios and 1BRs
only, which means Tom is not paying for a bedroom he will not use.

**Being across from Crossroads Mall matters more than it sounds.** For a senior on a 60-day
stay, groceries, a pharmacy, restaurants, and a place to sit indoors on a rainy Tuesday — all
within a short walk — is a genuine quality-of-life feature. Very few options on this list can
say it. [Kirkland Crossing](./Kirkland_Crossing.mdx) has the Park & Ride; Colonial Square has
the errands.

**The term is the crux and it is a complete unknown.** Nothing is published. What is known is
about the *landlord*, not the property: **Weidner routinely writes 3–11 month terms at a
premium but publishes nothing about it.** That is a genuinely useful pattern — it means the
answer is likely "yes, at a price" rather than "no" — but it is a tendency, not a policy, and
3 months is still one more than Tom needs. If the answer comes back 6 months,
**6 × $1,640 = $9,840 for a 2-month need**, and cheap rent does not fix it.

Weidner also manages [Uptown at Kirkland Urban](./Uptown_at_Kirkland_Urban.mdx), so whatever
you learn on this call likely applies there too. Ask once, use it twice.

Comfortably under budget at both ends — one of the better margins in the preferred area,
behind [Catalina](./Catalina_Apartments.mdx) and roughly level with
[The Ridge](./The_Ridge_at_Bellevue.mdx).

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Colonial Square does
not even say that much. Every number above is contingent on that unknown *and* on a short
term existing at all.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility accounts opened and closed inside 60 days. The
[CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) is in Redmond at
2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435); their $240–295 quote is the 12-month rate and 1–2 months
prices higher.

## When you call

Call **425-256-4046**. Everything about this property requires it — nothing is published.

1. **"What is the shortest lease term you will write, and what is the premium in dollars per
   month?"** Weidner does write short terms. Make them name the length and the number.
2. **"Will you do 2 months? 3?"** Ask both explicitly.
3. **"What's available around [Tom's date], and what does the studio rent for?"** Availability
   is not published at all.
4. **"Which utilities are billed separately, and roughly what do they run?"** Not included.
5. **"Is parking included, and what does it cost?"** Not published.
6. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published. At
   $1,640 the bar is low, but many buildings want 3× rent — ask about asset-based
   qualification for a retiree.
