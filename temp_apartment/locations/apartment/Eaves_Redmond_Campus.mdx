## Why this isn't a fit

**The term is the crux, and Eaves fails it on the published number.** 12 months is the standard term. For a 2-month need that is **12 × $2,260 = $27,120** on the unfurnished 1BR, or **12 × $3,510 = $42,120** on Furnished+. Neither is a real option. The only thing keeping this page open is that Eaves runs AvalonBay's **Furnished+** program, and AvalonBay states Furnished+ supports 30-day terms at *select* communities. Whether Eaves is one is unknown.

**Even if the 30-day term exists, the price loses to its own sibling.** [Avalon Esterra Park](./Avalon_Esterra_Park.mdx) — same operator, same Overlake neighborhood, same Furnished+ program — runs **$3,435–3,485** and has studios. Eaves is **$3,510 and has no studios**, so the cheapest door here is a 1BR. Eaves is more expensive and offers less. There is no scenario where Eaves wins and Esterra Park doesn't.

**No studios closes the cheapest path.** In every other Redmond building the studio is what makes budget work. Here the floor is a 1BR at $2,260 unfurnished.

**What's fine:** privacy is clean (private apartment, no roommates) and location is Redmond/Overlake at zero drive.

**Over budget at the floor, before any premium.** And if the answer is 12 months only, the number is $42,120 and the conversation is over.

The unfurnished path ($2,260 1BR) is worse: it's still a 12-month lease, and it adds the full furniture trap — [CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) at $240–295/month is the *12-month* rate, so 1–2 months prices higher, plus delivery, pickup, two moves, and utility accounts.

## When you call

Only call **425-414-7548** *after* [Esterra Park](./Avalon_Esterra_Park.mdx) has said no. This is a backstop.

1. **Does Furnished+ at Eaves support a 30-day term?** If no, stop — 12 months ends it.
2. If yes: exact rate for 30 and 60 nights, and the short-term premium in dollars.
3. What do "partial" utilities mean — what's actually included on Furnished+?
4. Anything cheaper than the $3,510 1BR? (Expect no — there are no studios.)
5. Income requirement, and asset-based qualification for a retiree on fixed income?
