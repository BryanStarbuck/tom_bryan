## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The reason to call is structural, not advertised.** Monterey is a **60-unit property built
in 1968** — small, old, and almost certainly not run by a national management company with a
leasing policy handed down from a corporate office. That is precisely the profile of a
landlord who will write a two-month lease on a handshake because the alternative is an empty
unit. Every large building in this search says "6 months" because a system says so. A
60-unit owner says yes or no because he decides. **That is the entire argument for this
page, and it is a real one** — but it is an argument about odds, not about a published fact.
Nothing about the term is published. This is a phone call, and only a phone call.

**The senior fit is the worst on this list, and it needs to be said plainly.** Three stories,
built in 1968. Buildings of that vintage and size in this area typically have **no
elevator**. The CSV marks senior-friendliness as **"No."** If Tom would be assigned a
second- or third-floor walk-up, that is not a minor inconvenience over 60 days — it is a
disqualifier. **The first question on the call is whether a ground-floor unit is available.**
If the answer is no, hang up politely; the rest does not matter.

**The price is unconfirmed.** $2,495–3,195 is marked *conflicting* — sources disagree. Do
not plan on $2,495. At $3,195, this property is at the ceiling before a single dollar of
furniture or utilities, and it fails.

**Geography is genuinely good.** Lakeview / Houghton is South Kirkland — squarely in the
target zone — and ~14 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule. Private apartments,
no roommates. **Pets are not published**, which matters if Tom has one.

**This likely breaks $3,300**, and at the high end it breaks it badly. The upside case
requires two things to go right at once: the rent lands at $2,495 rather than $3,195, *and*
the owner writes a short term with little or no premium. Both are plausible with a small
independent landlord. Neither is published.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees." Monterey publishes no term at all, so
there is not even that.

## When you call

Call **425-827-1337**. With a 60-unit property you are likely speaking to someone with real
authority on the first try — which is the advantage here. Use it.

1. **"Is there an elevator? If not, do you have a ground-floor unit available?"** **Ask this
   first.** A 1968 three-story walk-up ends the conversation for a senior.
2. **"Would you write a two-month lease?"** Ask it directly and plainly. This is the whole
   reason for the call, and a small owner can answer it in one sentence.
3. **"What does a 1BR actually rent for?"** Published sources conflict between $2,495 and
   $3,195. Get the real number from the person who sets it.
4. **"Is there a premium for a short term?"**
5. **"Do you allow pets?"** Not published anywhere.
6. **"Which utilities are included? Is parking included?"**
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** A small landlord is
   often far more flexible here than a corporate 3× rule — worth asking.
