## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**Be clear about where the short-term claim comes from: residents, not management.** Reviews
describe the building as having "corporate rentals and a lot of short term leases." That is
a description of what other tenants appear to be doing — it is not a policy, not a published
term, and not something anyone at Surrey Downs has committed to. Resident reviews are also
frequently out of date; a building that did corporate rentals two years ago under different
management may not now. **Treat this as a rumor that is cheap to check, and do not build a
plan around it.**

That said, the rumor is a reasonable one. Corporate rentals in a building usually mean the
owner is comfortable with turnover and non-standard terms, and Wilburton is exactly where
corporate tenants land. It is worth the call.

**The term, if it exists, is the crux — as everywhere.** If Surrey Downs turns out to write
6-month leases, **6 × $2,370 = $14,220 for a 2-month need** and the page is over. If it
genuinely writes 2–3 months, it becomes a legitimate option in the preferred area at a fair
price.

**Budget is workable.** $2,370 against a $3,300 ceiling leaves ~$930/month for a premium and
furniture. That is enough, but not generous — utilities are **not included** here, which
eats into it.

**Geography is right.** Surrey Downs / Wilburton is East Bellevue — the preferred area —
and ~18 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule, though at the far end of it.
Private apartments, no roommates. The **full-size washer/dryer in unit** is a genuine
quality-of-life win over a shared laundry room for a 60-day stay.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only that
"leases shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Here even the
existence of a short term rests on hearsay. Two unknowns stacked on each other is why this
page is short.

## When you call

Call **425-644-9878**. This is a verification call — five minutes, one question.

1. **"What is the shortest lease term you will actually write?"** Do not mention the
   reviews. Just ask. Their answer is the entire page.
2. **"Do you do corporate rentals here?"** If yes, ask who arranges them — that channel may
   be the real door, the way
   [CorporateHousing.com is the door into CityScape](./CityScape_Apartments.mdx).
3. **"What is the premium for a short term, in dollars per month?"**
4. **"What's available around [Tom's date], and what does the 1BR rent for?"** Availability
   is not published.
5. **"Which utilities are billed separately?"** They are not included.
6. **"Is parking included, and what does it cost?"** Not published.
7. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
8. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published — a real
   risk for a retiree.
