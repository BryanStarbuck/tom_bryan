## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The direct lease fails, and it is not close.** 12–15 months minimum. **12 × $2,428 =
$29,136 for a 2-month need.** There is no version of this where signing a direct lease at
The 101 makes sense. If you call 206-488-0094, that is what you will be offered, and you
should decline it.

**But the building itself is exactly right, and there are two doors into it that do not
require a lease at all.** Both [Blueground](../blueground/Blueground_Kirkland.mdx) and
[Landing](../platform/Landing_The_101.mdx) operate furnished units inside The 101 on
**30-night minimums**. Same building, same accessible units, same included parking — with
a term that actually matches Tom's need, furniture already in place, utilities included, and
no 12-month signature. That is the whole point of this page: **the address is a good idea;
the lease is not.**

**The building is unusually well-suited to a senior.** A 66-unit boutique property with
**accessible units** — one of only a handful in this search that explicitly lists them,
alongside [Westwater](./Westwater.mdx), [Bell Totem Lake](./Bell_Totem_Lake.mdx), and
[Bellevue Ridge](./Bellevue_Ridge.mdx). Landing's listing for this building notes a
**Walk Score of 96** — the most walkable option found anywhere in the search, which matters
a great deal if Tom would rather not drive to get groceries.

**Geography is the caveat.** Moss Bay is downtown Kirkland, not South Kirkland, so it is
outside the stated target sub-areas. ~15 minutes to Redmond keeps it inside the 20-minute
rule. Privacy is not an issue either way — these are private apartments.

## The real cost for 1–2 months

**Ignore the direct-lease math; the term disqualifies it before price matters.** The
relevant cost is the furnished-operator cost:

| Path | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct lease** | $2,428–5,798/mo × **12 months minimum** = **~$29,000+** | Disqualified on term |
| **[Blueground](../blueground/Blueground_Kirkland.mdx)** | Est. **$3,000–4,790/mo** — an **ESTIMATE**, no published price | 30-night min, furnished, utilities included, parking extra fee. The **studio is the budget shot**; a 2BR was quoted $4,790 |
| **[Landing](../platform/Landing_The_101.mdx)** | **Not published — ask when you call** | 30-day min, furnished, utilities included, covered parking |

Neither operator publishes a firm price for this building, so the honest answer is that the
number has to come from a phone call. Blueground's Kirkland estimate straddles the $3,300
ceiling — the studio may clear it, the 1BR likely does not. Blueground's pricing also
**rewards long stays**, which makes 1–2 months their worst tier.

The advantage over every apartment page in this set: **no furniture rental, no utility
setup, no short-term premium, no 12-month signature.** All three of those costs — the ones
that quietly add $500–900/month to a cheap unfurnished apartment — are already inside the
operator's number.

## When you call

**Do not start with the building.** Start with the operators.

1. **[Landing — 415-231-1701](../platform/Landing_The_101.mdx).** Ask specifically for The
   101 in Kirkland, 11 Main St S. Landing's unit URLs go dead within days, so ask a human to
   check inventory and hold something. Ask for the all-in monthly rate for 30 and 60 nights,
   and whether the $199/year membership applies.
2. **[Blueground — 1-917-259-1222](../blueground/Blueground_Kirkland.mdx).** Studio showed
   available Jul 25; 1BR Jul 30 and Aug 8. Ask for the studio rate first — it is the only
   unit type with a real shot at budget. Ask what parking costs; it is an extra fee. **Ask
   about the cancellation policy** — Blueground's is harsh, which matters for a senior whose
   dates may move.
3. **Ask both:** "Is the unit you're offering one of the accessible units?" The building has
   them. Get the specific unit.
4. **Only then, if you want:** call the building at **206-488-0094** and ask whether they
   will write anything under 12 months. Expect no. If they surprise you, it changes the
   math.
