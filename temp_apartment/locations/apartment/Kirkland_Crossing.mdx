## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and here it is unresolved rather than closed.** The published
default is 12 months. A 12-month-only building is not a fit — **12 × $1,890 = $22,680 for a
2-month need**, and no amount of cheap rent fixes that. But 12 months is what the website
says, not necessarily what the leasing office will write. Unlike a property that states a
hard 6-month floor, Kirkland Crossing simply publishes its standard term and says nothing
about flexibility. That is a question, not a refusal. This page exists because the question
is worth asking, and because the answer decides everything.

**Budget is genuinely good if the term lands.** $1,890–2,395 is the cheapest rent in South
Kirkland, and the garage is included rather than a $75–150/month add-on. Against a $3,300
ceiling that is $900–1,400 of headroom to absorb a short-term premium and furniture. On
money alone this building is comfortable.

**The transit is the real, differentiated argument.** Kirkland Crossing sits *on* the South
Kirkland Park & Ride. Nothing else in this search is on a transit hub. That matters
specifically because of who Tom is: if he stops driving — during this stay or after it —
this is the one address where that does not strand him. For a 60-day stay that is a nice
bonus. If the temporary stay ever turned into something longer, it would be the whole
argument.

**Geography is acceptable and the drive is good.** South Kirkland / Lakeview is squarely
in the target zone, not a Totem Lake compromise. ~13 minutes to Redmond is comfortably
inside the 20-minute rule. Private apartments, no roommates.

**What is not known is significant.** Senior-friendliness is not published — no elevator
confirmation, no accessibility notes. Utilities are not published. The availability dates
(studio Aug 4, 1BR Aug 8) are firm-ish, which means this only works if Tom's move-in is
early August or later.

**This math is conditional on getting a short term at all. If the answer is 12 months only,
stop — the page ends there.**

| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; a 1–2 month rental prices **higher**, plus delivery, pickup, and two moves |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,500–3,200** |

Clears $3,300 in every scenario where the term works. The money was never the problem here.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing that mentions
them says only "leases shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no
amount. At Kirkland Crossing there is not even that much — the short term is not advertised
at all, so the premium is pure unknown. Treat the estimate above as exactly that.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery,
two moves, utility accounts opened and closed inside 60 days. The
[CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) is in Redmond at
2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435), but their $240–295 quote is the 12-month rate; 1–2 months
prices higher. Weigh the whole package against a furnished no-lease
[hotel suite](../hotel/La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx).

## When you call

Call **425-675-4731**. Ask for the leasing manager — a leasing agent reading off a screen
will just repeat "12 months."

1. **"What is the shortest lease term you will actually write?"** Phrase it exactly that
   way. Not "do you do short-term" — that gets a scripted no. You want the real floor.
2. **"If the answer is 12 months, will you write a 2- or 3-month lease at a premium?"**
   Make them quote the premium in dollars. A yes at $500/month is still a win here.
3. **"Are the Aug 4 studio and Aug 8 1BR still available, and what do they rent for
   specifically?"** Get unit-level numbers, not the range.
4. **"Which utilities are included?"** Confirm the garage really is in the rent.
5. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
6. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published here.
   Many Eastside buildings want 3× rent — a real barrier for a retiree. Ask about
   asset-based qualification before an application fee is spent.
7. **"How close is the Park & Ride entrance to the unit's door?"** If transit is part of
   why you are here, the walk matters.
