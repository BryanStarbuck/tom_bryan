## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and The Luke passes it.** A 2-month minimum is the shortest conventional apartment lease found anywhere in Redmond, Bellevue, or Kirkland. Tom needs 1–2 months. This is one of only four buildings in the entire search where the published minimum actually matches the need — the others being [Redmond Place](./Redmond_Place.mdx), [Avalon at Bear Creek](./Avalon_at_Bear_Creek.mdx), and Villaggio on Yarrow Bay in Kirkland. Everything else asks Tom to pay for months he does not need.

**On budget, the base rent works.** A studio at $2,050 sits $1,250 under the $3,300 ceiling. Even mid-range units clear it. That headroom is not decoration — it is exactly what gets consumed by the short-term premium, utilities, and furniture below.

**But the short-term premium is unpublished, and that is the whole question.** The listing does not state what a 2-month lease costs versus a 12-month one. It never does — no building in this search publishes that number. Until someone at the leasing office says an amount out loud, every estimate on this page is contingent. Assume $300–600/month as a working **estimate only**, not a quote.

**Privacy and distance are both clean.** It's a private apartment, no roommates, in downtown Redmond — zero drive. That's the best location outcome available.

**The income requirement is the sleeper risk.** 3× rent means a $2,050 studio requires roughly $6,150/month of documented income. A retiree on a fixed income may not clear that on income alone. Ask about **asset-based qualification** on the first call — do not wait until after the application fee.

## The real cost for 1-2 months

Working the math on a studio at $2,050, for a 2-month stay:

| Line item | Monthly | 2 months |
|---|---|---|
| Base rent (studio) | $2,050 | $4,100 |
| Short-term premium — **ESTIMATE, not a quote** | $300–600 | $600–1,200 |
| Utilities (not included) | ~$150–250 | $300–500 |
| Furniture rental — [CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) | $300+ *(see below)* | $600+ |
| **Estimated all-in** | **~$2,800–3,200/mo** | **~$5,600–6,400** |

**Unfurnished is the trap here.** The Luke is empty. [CORT's Redmond showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) (2118 148th Ave NE, 425-242-5435) quotes $240–295/month for a 1BR package — but **that is the 12-month rate**. A 1–2 month rental prices higher, plus delivery, plus pickup. Then add two moves, and setting up and closing electric and internet accounts for a 60-day stay.

That stack of friction is why the extended-stay hotels often win: [Extended Stay America Seattle-Redmond](../hotel/Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) and [WoodSpring Suites Redmond](../hotel/WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) have no lease, no furniture, no utility accounts. Tom walks in with suitcases.

**The Luke only beats a hotel if the short-term premium comes in low.** If the leasing agent quotes $300/month, this is competitive. If they quote $600, it isn't.

## When you call

Call the leasing office at **425-584-2426**. Ask for a leasing consultant — not the call center.

1. **What is the exact short-term premium on a 2-month lease?** Get a dollar amount. This is the single question that decides the page.
2. Confirm a 2-month term is actually writable right now, or whether that's a "sometimes" thing.
3. Which units are available for Tom's move-in date, and at what rent? Availability runs out Aug 7, 2026.
4. **What is the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets for a retiree on fixed income?** Ask before paying any application fee.
5. What are the utility costs — is there a flat RUBS charge, or does Tom set up accounts himself?
6. Is there an elevator? Can Tom get a ground-floor unit?
7. Application fee, deposit, and any admin or amenity fees.
