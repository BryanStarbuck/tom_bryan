## Why this isn't a fit

**The term is the crux, and 5 months fails it outright.** Tom needs 1–2 months. The shortest lease Evans Creek writes is 5. That is **5 × $1,997 = $9,985 for a 2-month need** — about **$5,990 spent on three months of an empty apartment**. There is no version of this where that's a good trade.

For comparison: [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx) will write 2 months at $2,050/month, for $4,100 of base rent. Evans Creek's "cheaper" rent costs **more than twice as much in total** because of the three months Tom doesn't want.

**The concession doesn't save it.** There's up to 1 month free advertised. Even if it applied to a short term (concessions are usually written for 12-month leases — ask), that's $1,997 off a $9,985 commitment. Still roughly $8,000 for a 2-month stay, and still far worse than a 2-month building.

**Everything else is beside the point,** but for completeness: it's unfurnished, so add [CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) furniture, delivery, two moves, and utility accounts. Storage is $30/month. Short-term premium, as everywhere, is unpublished — assume $300–600/month as an **estimate**. No studios, so the cheapest door is a 1BR. Privacy and location are fine — private apartment, zero drive to Redmond.
