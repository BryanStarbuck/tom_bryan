## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The price is the surprise.** A **$1,695 studio in downtown Bellevue** is genuinely cheap
for that address — most downtown Bellevue product starts well north of $2,200, which is why
downtown is the secondary geography in this search rather than a target. At $1,695, budget is
not the obstacle; there is ~$1,600/month of headroom under the $3,300 ceiling.

**The term is on a list, not in a policy.** Meydenbauer appears on RentCafe's short-term
list, which is a Yardi flag the property sets itself. That is meaningful — someone there
affirmatively said they do short terms — but the flag carries no length and no price. Note
also that RentCafe's short-term inventory has a **floor around $2,449**, meaning most of that
site sits at or above our ceiling once a premium applies; Meydenbauer's $1,695 studio is an
outlier on it, which is exactly why it is worth a look.

If the short term turns out to be 6 months, **6 × $1,695 = $10,170 for a 2-month need**, and
the page ends.

**The operational friction is the real problem, and it is specific.** The leasing office
hours are **extremely limited** — one listing shows Monday 10–5 only — and **management is
based in Lynnwood**, roughly 25 miles away. That combination predicts exactly what you would
expect: slow callbacks, hard-to-schedule tours, and nobody on site to solve a problem. For a
60-day stay by a senior, an absentee manager is a real risk, not a nitpick. It is also going
to make simply *getting a quote* take longer than it should. The **888 number** confirms the
pattern — that is a call center, not a leasing desk.

**Geography is secondary.** Meydenbauer Bay is downtown Bellevue — not the preferred East
Bellevue zone (Crossroads, Lake Hills, Eastgate, Factoria, Wilburton, Bel-Red). ~15 minutes
to Redmond keeps it inside the 20-minute rule. Water views are pleasant and will not change
Tom's life in 60 days.

Under budget at both ends. **Short-term premiums are never published in this market** — every
listing says only "leases shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no
amount — so that conclusion, as everywhere, rests on an unknown. Pay attention to the garage:
downtown parking is where a $1,695 studio quietly becomes a $1,850 studio.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility setup. The [CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) is
in Redmond at 2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435); their $240–295 quote is the 12-month rate and
1–2 months prices higher.

## When you call

Call **888-298-6544** and expect to work for it. **Check the office hours before calling —
one listing shows Monday 10–5 only.** Ask immediately to be connected to someone who can
quote a lease, not a call-center agent taking a message.

1. **"You're listed as offering short-term leases. What lengths do you write, and at what
   premium?"**
2. **"Is the $1,695 studio real, and is it available around [Tom's date]?"** Availability is
   not published at all.
3. **"Is garage parking included in the rent, or extra?"** Downtown — assume extra until told
   otherwise.
4. **"Who manages the property day to day, and is anyone on site?"** Management is in
   Lynnwood. For a senior, knowing who answers a maintenance call at 7pm matters.
5. **"Which utilities are included?"**
6. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published.
