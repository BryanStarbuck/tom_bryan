## Why this isn't a fit

**The term is the crux, and 12 months ends the conversation.** Tom needs 1–2 months. A 12-month lease at the $2,188 floor is **12 × $2,188 = $26,256** for a 2-month need — roughly **$21,900 of rent for ten months of an empty apartment**. There is no premium, concession, or amenity that makes that arithmetic work. Nothing published suggests Windsor writes anything shorter here.

That's the page. Everything below is why it's still worth two minutes of your attention.

**The reason it's listed: the CORT partnership.** Reflections has a standing relationship with [CORT Furniture Rental](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) offering **48-hour delivery**. That's a data point about CORT's Redmond operation — the showroom is at 2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435), physically in Redmond — and it confirms furniture can land fast on the Eastside. That matters when furnishing [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx), [Redmond Place](./Redmond_Place.mdx), or [Bell Marymoor](./Bell_Marymoor_Park.mdx), all of which are unfurnished and *will* write a short lease.

Remember CORT's published $240–295/month is the **12-month** rate. A 1–2 month rental prices higher, plus delivery and pickup. Fast delivery doesn't make it cheap.

**The other detail worth stealing:** the deposit here is only **$300**. That's low for Redmond, and a useful benchmark when other buildings quote Tom a deposit — it establishes that a small deposit is a thing Redmond landlords do.

## When you call

Don't call about a lease. If you call **425-217-5185** at all, ask one question:

1. **Do you ever write terms shorter than 12 months, and at what premium?** If no — and expect no — move on to [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx) or [Redmond Place](./Redmond_Place.mdx).

The genuinely useful call is to **CORT at 425-242-5435** — but make that call *last*, after a unit is actually secured. See [CORT Furniture Rental](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx).
