## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and Westwater fails it on arithmetic.** The floor is 6 months.
**6 × $2,450 = $14,700 for a 2-month need** — roughly **$9,800 paid for months Tom does not
use.** And the listing is explicit that anything under 12 months **incurs extra fees**, so
the real per-month number is higher than $2,450. There is no reading of this where a
6-month commitment is a sensible way to buy 60 days of housing.

So why is this page not two paragraphs long? Because Westwater is strong on the two things
that are hardest to find anywhere else, and if Tom's situation shifts they become decisive.

**It explicitly lists accessible units.** Only a handful of properties in this entire search
do: Westwater, [Bell Totem Lake](./Bell_Totem_Lake.mdx), [The 101](./The_101_Kirkland.mdx),
and [Bellevue Ridge](./Bellevue_Ridge.mdx)'s ADA studio. For a senior, an accessible unit is
not an amenity — it is the requirement that quietly eliminates most of the list. If
accessibility turns out to be a hard need, Westwater re-enters the conversation despite the
term.

**The income requirement is 2.5×, not 3×.** Most Eastside buildings want 3× rent in income,
which is the wall a retiree hits. At 2.5× Westwater is among the more forgiving found —
behind [Vue at Kirkland](./Vue_at_Kirkland.mdx) at 2× but ahead of most. Still, 2.5× on
$2,450 is **$6,125/month of income**. Ask about asset-based qualification.

**Walkability is real value for a senior.** Downtown Kirkland means groceries, restaurants,
and the waterfront on foot rather than by car. That matters more over 60 days than most
amenity lists do.

**Geography is the weak part.** Moss Bay is *downtown* Kirkland, not South Kirkland — it is
outside the stated target sub-areas, though ~15 minutes to Redmond does keep it inside the
20-minute rule. And there are **only 2 units**, so even if everything else worked, the
inventory may not be there.

| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$3,100–3,550** |

**Per month, this is at or over the $3,300 ceiling before you even get to the term problem.**

**The number that actually decides it:** 6-month minimum × ~$3,100–3,550 =
**~$18,600–21,300 committed** to house Tom for 60 days. Against
[Larkspur Landing at ~$2,550/month with no lease](../hotel/Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) —
$5,100 for the same two months — this is not a close call.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Westwater at least admits the
fees exist, which is more than most. It still will not say how much. Every estimate above
depends on that unknown.

## When you call

Call **425-287-5623** — but only if accessibility is a hard requirement, or if Tom's
timeline has stretched toward 6 months. Otherwise skip it; the arithmetic is not close.

If you do call:

1. **"Will you write anything shorter than 6 months?"** Ask once. With only 2 units they
   have little pressure to say yes, but it costs nothing.
2. **"What are the extra fees for a lease under 12 months, in dollars per month?"** They
   admit these exist. Make them quantify.
3. **"What does 'accessible unit' mean here specifically?"** Grab bars? Roll-in shower?
   Wider doors? Elevator? Which of the 2 available units is accessible? This is the reason
   to make the call.
4. **"Income is 2.5× — Tom is retired. Do you qualify on assets?"** Ask before an
   application fee.
5. **"Which utilities are included? Is the garage included?"**
6. **"Are both units still available, and on what dates?"** Studio now, 1BR Aug 8 — with 2
   units, this changes weekly.
