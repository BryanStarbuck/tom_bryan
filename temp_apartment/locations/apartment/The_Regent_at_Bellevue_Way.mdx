## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The direct term fails.** 6-month floor: **6 × $2,325 = $13,950 for a 2-month need** —
roughly **$9,300 for months Tom does not use.** That is the standard verdict for every
6-month building here.

**But two things make The Regent worth one call rather than zero.** First, the listing
mentions **6–14 month leases plus "short-term options"** as a named product — which is more
specific than the vague "flexible" language at [Palisades](./Palisades.mdx). Somebody there
sells something shorter than six months. Ask what it is. Second, **ABODA and
CorporateHousing.com stock 8 furnished units in this building** — an independent channel with
a 30-day minimum that does not care what the leasing office's floor is. That is the same
building/operator split that makes [The 101](./The_101_Kirkland.mdx) and
[CityScape](./CityScape_Apartments.mdx) interesting.

The catch on the furnished channel is price. Corporate housing on the Eastside generally
starts around $3,000 and climbs; **that channel is likely over budget.** But it eliminates
furniture rental, utility setup, and the short-term premium in one number, so get a quote
before dismissing it.

**Geography is a demerit.** North Bellevue is not East Bellevue — not Crossroads, Lake Hills,
Eastgate, Factoria, Wilburton, or Bel-Red. It is outside the preferred sub-areas, though
~14 minutes to Redmond keeps it well inside the 20-minute rule.

**The building is from 1973** and senior-friendliness is only "Partial" — no elevator
confirmed. The pool, spa, and sauna are pleasant and will not matter in 60 days. **Utilities
are not included.**

| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; 1–2 months prices **higher** |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$3,000–3,400** — at or over the ceiling at the high end |
| **Est. total for the 6-month minimum** | **~$18,000–20,400** |

**Path B — the furnished channel (ABODA / CorporateHousing.com):** **Not published — price is
behind an inquiry form.** Expect **$3,000–6,000/mo** — an **ESTIMATE**. Likely over the
$3,300 ceiling, but it is a genuine 30-day product with everything included.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount.

## When you call

Note the **833 number is a call center**, not the leasing desk — ask for the on-site office
or a direct line.

Call **833-291-1276**:

1. **"Your listing mentions short-term options. What are they, specifically — what length,
   what price?"** Named options are more promising than vague flexibility. Make them define
   it.
2. **"Will you write 2 or 3 months, and at what premium?"**
3. **"Who runs the furnished units here — ABODA? What do they rent for?"** Get the phone
   number. That is the second door, and it does not go through this office.
4. **"Which utilities are billed separately?"** Not included.
5. **"Is parking included?"** Surface plus EV — confirm cost.
6. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** 1973 construction — ask.
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published — a real
   risk for a retiree.

Also call **[CorporateHousing.com — 888-539-1150](../platform/CorporateHousing_com.mdx)** and
ask about The Regent, 10600 NE 29th St, directly. Skip the middle step.
