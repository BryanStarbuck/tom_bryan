## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and Madison is close but not exact.** A published 3-month floor is
genuinely rare — most of East Bellevue is 6 or 12 months — and it is the clearest
short-lease window in Crossroads. But Tom needs 1–2 months. **3 × $2,125 = $6,375 for a
2-month need**, which means roughly **$2,125 paid for a month Tom does not use.** That is
the honest cost of this option. It is not a disqualifier — $2,125 of waste is far better
than the $9,800 you burn at a 6-month building like [Westwater](./Westwater.mdx) — but it
should be stated plainly rather than buried.

Whether that wasted month matters depends on Tom's timeline. If the real need is closer to
2 months than 1, and the long-term lease search might run over, a 3-month term is a buffer
rather than pure waste. If Tom is confident about 60 days and out, that third month is
$2,125 thrown away, and [Catalina](./Catalina_Apartments.mdx) or
[Bellevue Ridge](./Bellevue_Ridge.mdx) — actual month-to-month — are simply better.

**Budget is comfortable.** $2,125–2,325 against a $3,300 ceiling leaves ~$975–1,175/month of
headroom for premium, utilities, and furniture. The fees are also unusually gentle: a **$300
deposit** (most buildings want more) and a **$42 application fee**. For a stay this short,
low up-front friction has real value.

**Geography is right.** Crossroads is East Bellevue — the preferred area — and ~12 minutes
to Redmond is comfortably inside the 20-minute rule. Crossroads Mall is nearby, which means
groceries, pharmacy, and errands without a long drive. Private apartments, no roommates.

**Utilities are explicitly not included**, which separates Madison from
[Catalina](./Catalina_Apartments.mdx), where everything down to internet is bundled. Over a
short stay that is not just money; it is opening and closing accounts for a 90-day tenancy.
Senior-friendliness is only "Partial" and no elevator is confirmed — ask.

## The real cost for 1–2 months

Two ways to read this. Both are worth having in hand on the call.

**Per-month, while Tom is actually there:**

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Base rent (1BR) | $2,125–2,325 |
| **Short-term premium** | **NOT published.** Estimate **$300–600/mo** — an **ESTIMATE**, not a quote |
| Utilities (not included) | **$100–200/mo. Estimate.** |
| Parking | Assigned garage — confirm whether included or extra |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; a 1–2 month rental prices **higher**, plus delivery and pickup |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,800–3,400** |

That is *at or slightly over* $3,300 at the pessimistic end. Madison has less margin than
its base rent suggests, because nothing is bundled.

**Total cost of the commitment (the number that actually matters):**

3-month minimum × ~$2,800–3,400 all-in = **~$8,400–10,200 committed** for a stay Tom may
only need 2 months of. Ask whether the third month can be sublet or whether an early
termination fee is cheaper than the rent — sometimes it is.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Madison publishes a
3–12 month range but no pricing for the short end. Every under-budget estimate above is
contingent on that unknown.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a short stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility accounts. The [CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)
is in Redmond at 2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435), but their $240–295 quote is the 12-month
rate; 1–2 months prices higher.

## When you call

Call **425-747-2051**. Ask for the leasing manager.

1. **"3 months is your published floor. Will you write 2?"** Always ask. A published floor
   is a policy, and policies bend on a slow unit.
2. **"What is the premium for a 3-month lease versus 12, in dollars per month?"** The range
   is published; the pricing is not. This is the number.
3. **"If Tom leaves after 2 months, what does that cost?"** Early termination fee vs. paying
   the third month — compare them. Sometimes the fee is cheaper than $2,125.
4. **"What does the 1BR rent for specifically, and what's available on [Tom's date]?"**
   Availability is not published at all.
5. **"Is the assigned garage included in the rent or billed separately?"**
6. **"Which utilities will Tom be billed for, and roughly what do they run?"** They are not
   included — get an estimate.
7. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
8. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published here.
   Many buildings want 3× rent — a real risk for a retiree. Ask about asset-based
   qualification before spending the $42 application fee.
9. Confirm: deposit $300, application fee $42, pet $200 fee + $200 deposit + $25/mo.
