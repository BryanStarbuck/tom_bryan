## Why this isn't a fit

**The term is the crux, and 4 months is double the need.** A 2-month stay on a 4-month lease means **4 × $1,846 = $7,384**, of which roughly **$3,692 is rent for months Tom does not live there**. The cheap rent is real, but you cannot buy only the part you want.

Run it against the alternatives and the "cheap" advantage evaporates. A 2-month term at [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx)'s $2,050 studio is $4,100 of base rent. Bell Overlake's "cheaper" $1,846 studio costs $7,384 because of the extra two months. **The Luke is $3,284 cheaper despite a higher monthly rent.** The term matters more than the rate.

**The short-term premium is unpublished on top of that.** Even the 4-month price isn't firm — assume $300–600/month as an **estimate**, not a quote. That would push the 4-month total toward $8,600–9,800.

**Unfurnished adds the usual trap.** Empty apartment, so [CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) furniture at $240–295/month — and **that's the 12-month rate**; short rentals price higher, plus delivery, pickup, two moves, and utility accounts for a stay Tom wants to be 60 days.

**What's fine:** privacy is clean, and Overlake is zero minutes to Redmond.

## When you call

Worth one call only because the rent is genuinely the lowest in the set. Call **206-222-8731** or email **overlake@bellpartnersinc.com**.

1. **Will you write shorter than 4 months?** That's the only question that matters. If no, skip.
2. If they'll do 2 or 3 months: what's the exact premium in dollars?
3. Is there a buyout or early-termination option on a 4-month lease, and what does it cost? (If a 2-month buyout is cheap, the math could change.)
4. Utilities — included, RUBS, or resident accounts?
5. Income requirement, and asset-based qualification for a retiree on fixed income?
