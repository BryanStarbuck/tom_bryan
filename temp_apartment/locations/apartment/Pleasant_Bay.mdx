## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**This one fails twice, which is unusual — most weak options fail on term alone.**

**First, the term.** A 6-month floor against a 1–2 month need: **6 × $2,788 = $16,728**,
with roughly **$11,150 of that paid for months Tom does not use.** That alone would end the
page.

**Second, the budget — and this is what separates Pleasant Bay from the other 6-month
buildings.** At [The Preserve at Forbes Creek](./The_Preserve_at_Forbes_Creek.mdx) a $1,950
base leaves room to absorb a premium and furniture. At Pleasant Bay the base is **$2,788**,
which is only $512 below the $3,300 ceiling **before** a short-term premium (est. $300–600),
before utilities (est. $100–200), and before [furniture rental](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)
(est. $240–295+, and higher for a short term). Any one of those breaks budget. All three
together put this well over $3,300 per month. There is no scenario where a 6-month lease at
$2,788 is a sensible way to buy 60 days.

**What is genuinely good here is beside the point.** Houghton / Yarrow Bay is South Kirkland
— the acceptable target zone — and ~15 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule. It
accepts Section 8. And the **income requirement is 2.5×, not 3×**, which is one of the more
forgiving found and a real plus for a retiree in the abstract. But 2.5× of $2,788 is still
**$6,970/month of income**. A friendlier multiplier on a higher rent is not actually
friendlier.

| **Est. total for the 6-month minimum** | **~$20,400–23,400** |

For comparison: [Larkspur Landing](../hotel/Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx), furnished, no
lease, no utility setup — **~$5,100 for the two months Tom actually needs.** Pleasant Bay
costs roughly four times that to solve the same problem worse.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market** — every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. But it does not
matter here. Even at a $0 premium, this is over budget once furniture and utilities are
added.

## When you call

**Don't.** Both failure modes are published facts, not unknowns — the 6-month term and the
$2,788 base are on the listing. There is no question whose answer rescues this.

If Tom's situation changes to a 6+ month timeline and budget rises, it becomes an ordinary
lakefront Kirkland apartment with a forgiving 2.5× income test. That is a different search.
