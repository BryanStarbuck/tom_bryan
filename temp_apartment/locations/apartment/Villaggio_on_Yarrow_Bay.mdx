## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux of every apartment page, and Villaggio is one of the few that
actually clears it.** Lease terms run 2–18 months. A 2-month minimum for a 1–2 month need
means Tom pays for what he uses and nothing more. Compare that to the rest of Kirkland:
[Pleasant Bay](./Pleasant_Bay.mdx), [Westwater](./Westwater.mdx), and
[Vue at Kirkland](./Vue_at_Kirkland.mdx) all start at 6 months. At Westwater's $2,450, a
6-month floor is **6 × $2,450 = $14,700 for a 2-month need** — roughly $9,800 of it wasted.
Villaggio's 2-month term makes that arithmetic disappear. That is worth more than any
amenity on the page.

**Budget works at the bottom of the range only.** The published span is enormous —
$2,157 to $5,397 — because it covers 1BR through 3BR. Tom needs a 1BR. The $2,157 entry
leaves roughly $1,140/month of headroom under the $3,300 ceiling, which is what has to
absorb the short-term fee, utilities, and furniture. Anything above about a $2,600 base
starts to squeeze. Ask specifically for the 1BR price, not "the range."

**Geography is acceptable, not preferred.** Yarrow Bay is South Kirkland — the third
choice behind Redmond and East Bellevue, but genuinely in the target zone, not a Totem
Lake / Juanita compromise. ~15 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule with margin.
Privacy is a non-issue: conventional private apartments, no roommates.

**The amenities are unusually good and mostly irrelevant.** A private beach and marina on
Lake Washington is a lovely thing that Tom will use approximately twice in 60 days. Do not
let it move the decision. What should move the decision is that senior-friendliness is
**not published** — no elevator confirmation, no accessibility notes. For a building on a
lakefront slope, ask about stairs and ground-floor availability before anything else.

## The real cost for 1–2 months

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Base rent (1BR, bottom of range) | $2,157 |
| **Short-term fee** | Property confirms shorter leases carry added fees; **amount NOT published**. Estimate **$300–600/mo** — this is an **ESTIMATE**, not a quote |
| Utilities | Not published — assume **$100–200/mo**. **Estimate.** |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; a 1–2 month rental prices **higher**, plus delivery, pickup, and two moves |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,800–3,250** |

That clears $3,300 — but barely, and only from the $2,157 base. Push the base to $2,600 and
this option breaks budget.

**Every under-budget number on this page is contingent on an unknown.** Short-term premiums
are never published in this market. Listings say only "leases shorter than 12 months often
include additional fees," with no amount. Villaggio's own listing says exactly that. Until
someone at that office says a dollar figure out loud, this page is an estimate, not a plan.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** These units come empty. That means renting
furniture, paying delivery, moving in, moving out 60 days later, and opening and closing
utility accounts — for two months. The
[CORT showroom is in Redmond](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) at
2118 148th Ave NE, but their $240–295 quote is the 12-month rate; short rentals price
higher. Weigh the total against a furnished, no-lease
[hotel suite](../hotel/La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx) before committing.

## When you call

Call **206-488-0135**, or **425-827-4603** if that goes nowhere. Ask for the leasing manager.

1. **"Confirm the 2-month lease term is available for a move-in on [Tom's date]."** The
   2–18 month range is published, but availability of a 2-month term on a specific unit is
   a different question.
2. **"What is the exact short-term fee for a 2-month lease, in dollars per month?"** This
   is the number that decides the page. Do not accept "there may be additional fees."
3. **"What does the 1BR actually rent for?"** The $2,157–5,397 range is not useful. Get the
   1BR number for the specific available unit.
4. **"Which utilities are included, and which are billed separately?"**
5. **"Is there an elevator? Is a ground-floor unit available?"** Not published — and this
   is a lakefront property. Ask before you visit.
6. **"What is the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published here.
   Many buildings want 3× rent, which is a real risk for a retiree. Ask about asset-based
   qualification.
7. **"Is parking included in the rent or billed separately?"**
8. Confirm the deposit and application fee — neither is published.
