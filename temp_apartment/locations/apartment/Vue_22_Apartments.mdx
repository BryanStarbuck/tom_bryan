## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**On paper this has more going for it than almost anything in Bellevue, and the only missing
piece is the one that matters most.**

**The location is the best in Bellevue for this job.** ~10 minutes to Redmond makes Vue 22
the closest Bellevue building in the search — closer than [Catalina](./Catalina_Apartments.mdx)
(~18), closer than [The Ridge](./The_Ridge_at_Bellevue.mdx) (~12), and closer than most of
Kirkland. Bel-Red is East Bellevue — the preferred area — and it sits on the Redmond side of
it, which is the right side.

**The term is completely unconfirmed, and that is the crux.** Nothing is published. The one
thread is that **the site references corporate housing**, which suggests a furnished
short-term channel exists in the building — the same building/operator split that makes
[The 101](./The_101_Kirkland.mdx) and [CityScape](./CityScape_Apartments.mdx) worth calling.
"Partial" furnished and "Partial" utilities in the data point the same way: some units in
this building are already set up as short-term product. But nobody has confirmed the length
or the price. Until someone does, this is a promising phone call and nothing more.

**Budget is comfortable.** $1,989 leaves ~$1,300/month of headroom. **4 weeks free** is
advertised — ask whether it survives a short term; concessions usually require 12 months.
Application fee is $50.

**24 units available is real leverage.** Deep vacancy is what makes a leasing office willing
to write a non-standard term — the same reason [Modera Bridle Trails](./Modera_Bridle_Trails.mdx)
is the best negotiating target in Kirkland. Make the ask.

**The amenities — concierge, rooftop deck, pet spa — are mostly noise for 60 days**, with one
exception: **a concierge is a genuine plus for a senior.** Someone at a desk who takes
packages, lets in a repair tech, and notices if something is wrong is worth more than a
rooftop deck.

| Parking | **Garage, price varies — ask.** Assume **$75–200/mo** on a newer building. **Estimate.** |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; 1–2 months prices **higher** — *unless a partially furnished unit is available* |
| Application fee | $50 |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,700–3,400** |

Crosses $3,300 at the pessimistic end, driven by the newer-building parking charge and the
premium. If a **partially furnished** unit is available, the furniture line drops out and it
clears comfortably — that is the question to ask.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Vue 22 publishes no
term at all.

## When you call

Call **425-448-8770**. Ask for the leasing manager. With 24 units empty, they have reason to
talk.

1. **"What is the shortest lease you will actually write, and what is the premium?"** The
   whole page turns on this.
2. **"Your site mentions corporate housing. Who runs those units, and what do they rent
   for?"** This is the second door. Get a name and a number.
3. **"Do you have any furnished or partially furnished units?"** The data says "Partial." If
   yes, this jumps up the list — it kills the furniture problem entirely.
4. **"You have 24 units. Will you take full rent for 3 months?"** Deep vacancy is leverage;
   full rent now on an empty unit is a good trade for them.
5. **"What does garage parking cost?"** Price varies — this is where the budget quietly goes.
6. **"Which utilities are included and which are billed separately?"** "Partial" is not an
   answer.
7. **"Does the 4 weeks free apply to a short term, or 12 months only?"**
8. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
9. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published. Newer
   amenitized buildings often want 3× — a real risk for a retiree.
