## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and The Pine has the best circumstantial evidence in Kirkland
without an actual published number.** It is the **only property inside Kirkland proper that
appears on RentCafe's short-term list**. That list is Yardi's own flag, set by the property
itself — meaning someone at The Pine affirmatively told their management software they do
short-term leases. That is better than a rumor and worse than a policy. It is also not a
length: the flag says "we do short terms," not "we will write two months." Everything hangs
on what a human says on the phone.

Be aware of how that flag actually behaves in this market. The Apartments.com short-term
flag is set at the *property* level and usually means 3–6 months, not 1–2. Assume the same
here until told otherwise. If the answer comes back "6 months," the arithmetic is brutal:
**6 × $2,099 = $12,594 for a 2-month need**, roughly $8,400 of it wasted.

**Distance is a genuine advantage.** ~10 minutes to Redmond makes The Pine the
second-closest Kirkland option, behind only
[Modera Bridle Trails](./Modera_Bridle_Trails.mdx) at ~9. That beats most of Bellevue.
South Rose Hill sits on the Redmond side of Kirkland, which is exactly the right side.

**Budget has real headroom.** A 1BR from $2,099 against a $3,300 ceiling leaves ~$1,200/month
to absorb a premium, utilities, and furniture. That margin is what makes The Pine a
contender rather than a footnote — a building with a good term and a $2,900 base would fail
where this one passes.

**The income requirement is a real risk.** The Pine wants **3× rent**. On a $2,099 1BR that
is **$6,297/month of income**, and with a short-term premium the bar rises with the rent.
For a retiree living on fixed income and assets, 3× is exactly the wall that ends
applications. Ask about asset-based qualification *before* paying an application fee. For
comparison, [Vue at Kirkland](./Vue_at_Kirkland.mdx) asks only 2× and
[Westwater](./Westwater.mdx) 2.5× — if income is the binding constraint, those are the
calls to make.

**Geography is acceptable, not preferred.** South Rose Hill is South Kirkland — third
choice behind Redmond and East Bellevue, but in the target zone and not a Totem Lake
compromise. Private apartments, no roommates. Senior-friendliness is not published: no
elevator confirmation, no accessibility notes.

| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; a 1–2 month rental prices **higher**, plus delivery, pickup, and two moves |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,700–3,200** |

Clears $3,300 at both ends — but only from the $2,099 base, and only if the term is short.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. The Pine is on a
short-term list and still publishes no lease length and no premium. Every number above is
contingent on that unknown.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility accounts opened and closed inside 60 days. The
[CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) is in Redmond at
2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435) — convenient to this address — but their $240–295 quote is
the 12-month rate; 1–2 months prices higher.

## When you call

Call **425-600-5296**. Ask for the leasing manager.

1. **"You're listed as offering short-term leases. What lease lengths do you actually
   write?"** Lead with the fact that they published the flag — it puts the burden on them.
2. **"What is the shortest term, and what is the premium in dollars per month?"** If the
   answer is 6 months, say thank you and price it against
   [Villaggio's 2-month term](./Villaggio_on_Yarrow_Bay.mdx).
3. **"What does the 1BR at $2,099 rent for on a short term, and is it one of the 7
   available units?"**
4. **"The income requirement is 3× rent. Tom is retired — do you qualify on assets
   instead?"** Ask this *early*. If the answer is a hard no, nothing else on this page
   matters and you have saved an application fee.
5. **"Which utilities are included, and which are billed separately?"**
6. **"Is garage parking included in the rent, or extra?"**
7. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
8. Confirm deposit and application fee — neither is published.
