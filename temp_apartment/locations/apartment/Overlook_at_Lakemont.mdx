## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term story is unusually strong for Bellevue, and worth understanding.** Overlook offers
two separate doors: it advertises short-term leases directly, *and* Churchill Living operates
furnished corporate units on site. Those are independent paths — if the leasing office says
6 months, the corporate operator may still do 30 days. That is the same pattern that makes
[The 101 in Kirkland](./The_101_Kirkland.mdx) and [CityScape](./CityScape_Apartments.mdx)
interesting: the building is one thing, the operator inside it is another. With **22 units
available**, there is inventory to work with too.

**The distance is what sinks it.** ~25 minutes to Redmond puts Overlook **outside the
20-minute rule** — the farthest Bellevue option in the entire search. Lakemont / Cougar
Mountain is far southeast Bellevue, well past Eastgate and Factoria, and nowhere near the
preferred East Bellevue zone (Crossroads, Lake Hills, Wilburton, Bel-Red). Over 60 days,
every trip toward Redmond costs an extra ten minutes each way.

**The terrain is a specific concern for a senior.** Cougar Mountain is hilly. A hillside
garden community typically means outdoor stairs between the parking and the unit regardless
of whether the building itself has an elevator. Senior-friendliness is marked only
"Partial," and no elevator is confirmed. If Tom has any mobility concern, this is a
walk-the-property-first situation, and that is a lot of effort for an address that is already
too far away.

**Budget is fine on the direct lease.** $1,985 leaves ~$1,300/month of headroom. The
Churchill furnished units will price far higher — furnished corporate operators typically add
a $1,500–2,500/month premium over unfurnished — and that likely breaks the ceiling.

**Path A — direct lease, unfurnished:**

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Base rent (1BR) | $1,985 |
| **Short-term premium** | **NOT published.** Estimate **$300–600/mo** — an **ESTIMATE**, not a quote |
| Utilities (not included) | **$100–200/mo. Estimate.** |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; 1–2 months prices **higher**, plus delivery, pickup, two moves |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,600–3,100** |

**Path B — Churchill Living furnished:** **Not published — ask when you call.** Expect
corporate-housing pricing, which on the Eastside generally starts around $3,000 and climbs.
Likely over budget, but it eliminates furniture rental, utility setup, and the short-term
premium in one number.

Path A clears budget comfortably. The problem was never the money — it is the 25 minutes.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Overlook says
"short-term available" without a length or a price.

## When you call

Only worth a call if the 20-minute rule turns out to be soft, or if everything closer has
failed.

Call **425-786-0362**:

1. **"What lease lengths do you actually write, and what is the premium?"** Their listing
   says short-term is available without defining it.
2. **"Tell me about the Churchill Living units on site — who do I call, and what do they
   rent for?"** This is the second door and it has its own phone number. Get it.
3. **"Is the property hilly between parking and the units? Is there an elevator? Can Tom get
   a ground-floor unit with flat access from the car?"** On Cougar Mountain, ask this in
   detail.
4. **"Which utilities are billed separately?"** Not included.
5. **"Is parking included?"** Not published.
6. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published — a real
   risk for a retiree.
