## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term fails outright.** 12 months. **12 × $2,407 = $28,884 for a 2-month need.** A
12-month-only building is not a fit for a 60-day stay, and that is the verdict regardless of
anything else on this page.

**The income requirement fails independently, and it is worth stating because it recurs.**
Uptown wants **3× rent**. On a $2,407 studio that is **$7,221/month of income** — over
**$86,000/year** to rent a studio. For a retiree living on fixed income and assets, that is
not a hurdle, it is a wall. This is the single clearest illustration of why income
qualification is a real risk in this search, not a formality. **Ask about asset-based
qualification** — many managers will count liquid reserves in place of income, and Weidner
(who manages this property) may. But do not assume it.

**What makes this frustrating is that the building is genuinely right for a senior.** An
**elevator mid-rise, walkable to Kirkland Urban and a QFC** — meaning groceries on foot, no
stairs, and somewhere to go — is exactly the profile that matters over 60 days. Very few
buildings on this list combine an elevator with real walkable groceries.
[Colonial Square](./Colonial_Square.mdx) has the errands but no confirmed elevator;
[The Ridge](./The_Ridge_at_Bellevue.mdx) has the elevator in a strip-mall setting. Uptown has
both and won't rent it to him for two months.

**Geography is decent.** Central Houghton edges into South Kirkland; the address itself is
downtown-adjacent. ~13 minutes to Redmond is comfortably inside the 20-minute rule.

**One note on the concession:** $800 off move-in-ready units is advertised. On a 12-month
lease that is $67/month. It does not change anything.

## When you call

**Don't**, unless you want to test one thing: whether Weidner will bend. Weidner **routinely
writes 3–11 month terms at a premium** but publishes nothing about it — that is the same
pattern noted at [Colonial Square](./Colonial_Square.mdx), which Weidner also manages. The
website says 12 months; the company's habits say otherwise.

If you make the call to **425-484-7847**, ask exactly two questions:

1. **"What is the shortest lease you will actually write, and what is the premium?"**
2. **"Income is 3× rent. Tom is retired — do you qualify on assets instead?"** If the answer
   is no, nothing else matters, and you have learned something that applies to
   [Colonial Square](./Colonial_Square.mdx) too.
