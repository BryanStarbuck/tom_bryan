## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term language is the best in Kirkland, and it is worth understanding why.** Most
properties publish a fixed floor — 6 months, 12 months — which is a policy you have to argue
against. The Carillon publishes *"lease terms are variable, please inquire."* That is not a
policy; it is an invitation. Properties that say this are telling you the length is a
conversation with a human, which is exactly what Tom needs. On term language alone this
would be a top call.

**Except there are zero units.** As of July 2026 the listing shows no availability at all.
An invitation to negotiate a lease on an apartment that does not exist is worth nothing. That
is the whole verdict.

**Two other things you should know before you spend time here.** First, the **base rent
excludes an amenity fee and utilities**, so the published $2,209 is not the real number —
expect it to land meaningfully higher once everything is added, and a Carillon Point
lakefront property is not where the amenity fee will be small. Second, **reviews criticize
management at 2.0/5**. For a 60-day stay by a senior, a management company with a poor
service reputation is a real risk, not a footnote — it is the difference between a
maintenance issue being fixed in a day and it being fixed after Tom has already moved out.

**Geography is good.** Carillon Point / Lakeview is South Kirkland — genuinely in the target
zone — and ~14 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule. Private apartments, no
roommates.

| Parking | Garage/carport — confirm whether included |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; 1–2 months prices **higher**, plus delivery, pickup, two moves |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,900–3,500+** |

That crosses $3,300 at the pessimistic end, and the unpublished amenity fee could push it
further. **Short-term premiums are never published in this market** — every listing says
only "leases shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. Here even
the *term* is unpublished, so both variables are unknown.

## When you call

Call **425-406-3722** — but treat it as a five-minute waitlist call, not a real prospect.

1. **"Do you have anything at all coming available around [Tom's date]?"** If no, stop.
2. **"Your listing says lease terms are variable. Will you write 2 months?"** If a unit ever
   opens, this is the question that makes The Carillon worth revisiting.
3. **"What is the amenity fee?"** The base rent excludes it. Get the number.
4. **"Which utilities are billed separately, and roughly what do they run?"**
5. **"Is parking included?"**
6. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
7. **Ask to be called if something opens.** That is really the only value of this call.
