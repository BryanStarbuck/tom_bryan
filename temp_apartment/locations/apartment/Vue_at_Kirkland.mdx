## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term fails, and that is the headline.** A 6-month floor against a 1–2 month need:
**6 × $1,662 = $9,972**, with roughly **$6,650 paid for months Tom does not use.** That
disqualifies it as a plan.

**But the income requirement is why this page exists.** Vue at Kirkland asks **2× rent** —
the most forgiving multiplier found in the entire search. Most Eastside buildings want **3×**,
which on a $2,100 apartment means proving **$6,300/month of income**. That is the wall that
quietly ends applications for retirees, and it is a bigger risk than budget for Tom.
[The Pine](./The_Pine.mdx), [The Preserve](./The_Preserve_at_Forbes_Creek.mdx), and
[Uptown at Kirkland Urban](./Uptown_at_Kirkland_Urban.mdx) all want 3×. Vue wants 2× — on a
$1,662 studio that is **$3,324/month of income**, a bar most retirees clear easily.

**So the honest use of this page is as a fallback of a specific kind:** if Tom gets turned
down on income elsewhere, or if the 3× requirement makes the better-located buildings
unreachable, Vue is the first call. Not because the term is right — it is not — but because
it is the one place likely to say yes to the applicant.

**Budget is the best in Kirkland.** $1,662 for a studio is the cheapest confirmed
short-term-capable rent in the city, leaving ~$1,640/month of headroom. There is also **4
weeks free** advertised — ask whether it survives a shorter lease; concessions usually
require 12 months, and one Tom cannot use is worth $0.

**Geography is the other strike.** Totem Lake is **North Kirkland** — not South Kirkland, not
East Bellevue, not Redmond. It is outside every target area, and ~16 minutes to Redmond is in
the wrong direction. [Kirkland Crossing](./Kirkland_Crossing.mdx) is closer *and* in the
acceptable zone for $200 more.

Per month it is cheap. As a way to buy 60 days it is not — against
[Larkspur Landing at ~$5,100 for two months with no lease](../hotel/Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx),
this costs roughly three times as much.

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only "leases
shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount.

## When you call

Call **425-961-9031** if income is the binding constraint, or if everything closer has failed.

1. **"Income is 2× rent — confirm that, and confirm Tom can qualify on retirement income or
   assets."** This is the reason for the call. Get it clearly.
2. **"Will you write anything shorter than 6 months?"** Ask once. With 14 units they have
   some vacancy pressure.
3. **"What is the short-term premium in dollars per month?"**
4. **"Does the 4 weeks free apply to a short lease, or 12 months only?"**
5. **"Is the $1,662 studio available around [Tom's date]?"** Availability runs through Aug 11.
6. **"Which utilities are included? Is the garage included?"**
7. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
