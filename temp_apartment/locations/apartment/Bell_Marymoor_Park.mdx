## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The term is the crux, and Bell Marymoor half-passes.** The floor is 3 months. Tom needs 1–2. If he takes a 3-month lease for a 2-month need, he pays for one month he does not use. On a $2,243 studio that is **3 × $2,243 = $6,729 for a 2-month need** — roughly $2,243 of pure waste versus a true 2-month deal. That is real money, and it's the honest reason [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx) and [Redmond Place](./Redmond_Place.mdx) get called first.

**But this building has a genuinely unusual property that argues for calling it anyway.** The 9-month studio prices at $2,243 — **below** the 12-month at $2,468. Short terms are priced *down* here, not up. That is the opposite of every other building in this search, and it suggests Bell Marymoor's revenue model does not punish a short lease. If that logic extends to a 3-month term, the "unpublished short-term premium" that poisons every other page may be small or absent here. **Confirm that on the phone** — the published inversion is at 9 months, not 3, so do not assume.

**The income test is the standout.** 2.25× rent is far friendlier than the 3× that [The Luke](./The_Luke.mdx), Triangle-adjacent buildings, and most of the Kirkland set demand. For a retiree on a fixed income, this is the difference between qualifying and not. On a $2,243 studio, 2.25× is about $5,047/month of required income versus $6,729 at 3×.

**Budget clears comfortably.** A studio at $2,243 leaves over $1,000/month of room. Privacy is clean — private apartment, no roommates. Zero drive; it's in Redmond, near Marymoor Park, which is a pleasant setting for someone who walks.

## The real cost for 1-2 months

Working a studio at $2,243. Note the term forces a 3-month commitment:

| Line item | Monthly | **3 months (the real commitment)** |
|---|---|---|
| Base rent (studio) | $2,243 | $6,729 |
| Short-term premium — **ESTIMATE, not a quote** *(may be $0 here — see above)* | $0–600 | $0–1,800 |
| Utilities (not included) | ~$150–250 | $450–750 |
| Furniture rental — [CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) | $300+ | $900+ |
| Look-and-lease credit | *−$500 one-time* | *−$500* |
| **Estimated all-in** | **~$2,700–3,300/mo** | **~$7,600–8,900** |

There is a **$500 look-and-lease** incentive published — ask whether it applies to a 3-month term, since concessions are usually written for 12-month leases.

**Unfurnished is a trap.** [CORT's Redmond showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) (2118 148th Ave NE, 425-242-5435) quotes $240–295/month for a 1BR package — **that is the 12-month rate**; 1–3 months prices higher, plus delivery and pickup. Add two moves and setting up utility accounts for a stay this short. This friction is why [Extended Stay America Seattle-Redmond](../hotel/Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) and [WoodSpring Suites Redmond](../hotel/WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) — furnished, kitchen, no lease, no utility accounts — often win at this duration.

## When you call

Call **425-286-8622**.

1. **Will you write shorter than 3 months?** Ask directly. The published floor is 3, but ask what they'd actually do for a 2-month stay.
2. **What does a 3-month term price at versus 12?** Your listing shows the 9-month studio *below* the 12-month — does that pricing logic hold at 3 months, or is there a premium?
3. **Confirm the income requirement is 2.25×, and ask whether assets qualify for a retiree on a fixed income.** This is the building's biggest advantage — verify it.
4. Does the $500 look-and-lease credit apply to a short term?
5. What's available on Tom's move-in date? Availability runs now through Aug 2026.
6. Elevator? Ground-floor unit? Grab bars?
7. Utility setup — RUBS or resident accounts? Deposit, app fee, parking, pet fee.
