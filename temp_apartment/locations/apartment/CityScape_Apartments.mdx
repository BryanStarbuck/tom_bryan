## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The direct lease fails on term.** 12 months only. **12 × $2,195 = $26,340 for a 2-month
need.** A 12-month-only building is not a fit for a 60-day stay, and no amount of good
location fixes that. If you call the leasing office, that is what you will be offered.

**The interesting part is the split.** Furnished short-term units inside this same building
are marketed through
[CorporateHousing.com](../platform/CorporateHousing_com.mdx) — same address, same building,
different product, and a 30-day minimum. That is the same pattern as
[The 101 in Kirkland](./The_101_Kirkland.mdx), where the direct lease fails but Blueground
and Landing sell 30-night units in the building. The difference is that at The 101 the
operators are known and the units are confirmed available. Here, the corporate-housing
channel is real but the price is hidden behind an inquiry form, and corporate-housing
pricing on the Eastside generally starts around $3,000 and climbs — likely at or over the
$3,300 ceiling.

**The location is genuinely good.** Wilburton is East Bellevue — the preferred area — and
**two blocks from Wilburton light rail.** For a senior, that is real: it is a car-free route
to downtown Bellevue and beyond. ~16 minutes to Redmond is inside the 20-minute rule.
Private apartments, no roommates.

## The real cost for 1–2 months

**Direct lease:** disqualified on term. $2,195 × 12 = $26,340. Do not price it further.

**Corporate-housing channel (the only viable path):**

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Furnished unit via CorporateHousing.com | **Not published — price is behind an inquiry form.** Eastside corporate housing generally runs **$3,000–6,000/mo** — an **ESTIMATE** |
| Utilities | Typically included in corporate housing — confirm |
| Furniture | $0 — already furnished |
| Short-term premium | Not applicable — the short term *is* the product |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$3,000+ — likely at or over the $3,300 ceiling** |

The upside of the corporate channel is that it eliminates the three costs that quietly wreck
a cheap unfurnished apartment: no [furniture rental](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx),
no utility account setup and teardown, no unpublished short-term premium. The downside is
that you pay for all of that in one bundled number, and that number tends to be high. Expect
an upsell to 3+ month terms — that is how the channel is built.

## When you call

**Do not call the leasing office first.** They sell the 12-month lease.

1. **[CorporateHousing.com — 888-539-1150](../platform/CorporateHousing_com.mdx).** Ask
   specifically about CityScape, 558 121st Pl NE, Bellevue. Ask for the all-in monthly rate
   for 30 nights and for 60 nights. If it is over $3,300, stop.
2. **Ask whether the unit is private and whole** — corporate housing is, but confirm.
3. **Ask what is included:** utilities, internet, parking, housekeeping. Parking is not
   published for this building at all.
4. **Ask about the elevator and ground-floor availability.** Not published.
5. **Only if you are curious:** call **425-453-0720** and ask whether they will write
   anything under 12 months directly. Expect no.
