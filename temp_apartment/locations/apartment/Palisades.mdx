## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The hook is a phrase: "flexible lease terms and pricing to fit your needs."** That is
better than silence and worse than a number. Properties that publish a hard 6- or 12-month
floor are telling you no. Palisades is telling you the length is negotiable — which is
exactly what Tom needs — without committing to anything. It is a genuine hint. It is not a
fact, and there is no published range anywhere to check it against. **Everything here
requires a call.**

Be realistic about what "flexible" usually means in this market. On most Eastside listings
it resolves to 6–9 months, not 1–2. Assume that until someone tells you otherwise. If the
answer is 6 months, **6 × $1,957 = $11,742 for a 2-month need**, and the page ends.

**Budget is good.** $1,957 is one of the lowest 1BR entry prices in East Bellevue, leaving
~$1,340/month of headroom under the $3,300 ceiling — enough to absorb a premium and
furniture with room left. That margin is what makes the call worth making.

**Geography is right.** Crossroads / Bel-Red is East Bellevue — the preferred area — and
~13 minutes to Redmond is comfortably inside the 20-minute rule. Bel-Red in particular sits
on the Redmond side of Bellevue, which is the right side. Private apartments, no roommates.

**The constraint is inventory: only 4 units.** A property with 4 vacancies has little
pressure to bend on term — that is the opposite of the leverage you get at a lease-up like
[Modera Bridle Trails](./Modera_Bridle_Trails.mdx) with dozens of empty units. Flexible
language plus thin inventory usually means flexible *pricing*, not flexible *length*.

an unpublished premium — **short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every
listing says only "leases shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no
amount. Palisades does not even say that; it just says "flexible."

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility setup and teardown. The

2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435); their $240–295 quote is the 12-month rate and 1–2 months
prices higher.

## When you call

Call **425-589-0607**. Ask for the leasing manager.

1. **"Your listing says flexible lease terms. What lengths do you actually write?"** Use
   their own words back at them. It is the only leverage the listing gives you.
2. **"Will you write 2 months? 3?"** Ask both explicitly, and get a premium in dollars.
3. **"Which of the 4 units is available around [Tom's date], and what does it rent for?"**
   With 4 units this changes weekly.
4. **"Which utilities are billed separately?"** Not included.
5. **"Is parking included, and what does it cost?"** Not published.
6. **"Is there an elevator, and can Tom get a ground-floor unit?"** Not published.
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published — a real
   risk for a retiree. Ask before spending an application fee.
