## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The price is the headline: $1,500 is the cheapest studio in the preferred area, full
stop.** Against a $3,300 ceiling that is $1,800/month of headroom — enough to absorb a
short-term premium, utilities, and furniture rental and still finish well under budget.
There is no cheaper way to be in East Bellevue.

**The senior fit is better than the price implies.** It has an **elevator** — which most
sub-$2,000 Eastside garden properties do not — plus **controlled access**. For a cheap
building, that is a genuinely good combination for a senior: no stairs, and a locked front
door. On those two specific points The Ridge beats several buildings charging $800 more.

**The term is the crux, and it is unresolved.** RentCafe explicitly notes that no
month-to-month is mentioned. That is a meaningful negative signal — it means nobody has
found evidence this building writes short leases. But the listing does say **"prices may
vary depending on lease length,"** which is a real hint: a property that prices by lease
length is a property that *writes* more than one lease length. That is worth a phone call.
It is not worth a plan until someone confirms it.

If the answer is 12 months only, this page ends there. **12 × $1,500 = $18,000 for a
2-month need**, and cheap rent does not fix a term that does not fit.

**Geography is right.** Crossroads is East Bellevue — the preferred area — and ~12 minutes
to Redmond is comfortably inside the 20-minute rule. Crossroads Mall is nearby for
groceries and errands. Private apartments, no roommates.

**The friction:** utilities are **not included**, which means account setup and teardown for
a short stay. The office is **closed Sunday and Monday**, so plan the call. And the phone
number is an **833 toll-free line**, which usually means a call center rather than the
on-site office — expect to work to reach someone with authority.

| Item | Amount |

| Base rent (studio) | $1,500 (1BR/2BR up to $1,950) |
| **Short-term premium** | **NOT published.** The listing says prices vary by lease length but never says how much. Estimate **$300–600/mo** — an **ESTIMATE**, not a quote |
| Utilities (not included) | **$100–200/mo. Estimate.** |
| Parking | Covered / carport — confirm whether included or extra |
| Furniture rental ([CORT](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx)) | $240–295+/mo at CORT's **12-month** rate; a 1–2 month rental prices **higher**, plus delivery, pickup, and two moves |
| Application fee | $60 (one-time) |
| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$2,150–2,650** |

That is roughly $650–1,150/month under budget even at the pessimistic end — one of the
widest margins on this list, behind only [Catalina](./Catalina_Apartments.mdx).

**Short-term premiums are never published in this market.** Every listing says only that
"leases shorter than 12 months often include additional fees," with no amount. The Ridge
says even less. Every under-budget number above is contingent on that unknown, and on the
short term existing at all.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** Empty unit, rented furniture, delivery, two
moves, utility accounts opened and closed inside 60 days. The
[CORT showroom](../corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) is in Redmond at
2118 148th Ave NE (425-242-5435), but their $240–295 quote is the 12-month rate; 1–2 months
prices higher. At $1,500 base, the furniture and premium roughly *double* the real cost —
which is the whole reason the [hotel tier](../hotel/Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) stays
competitive against a cheap studio.

## When you call

Call **833-623-6015**. **Note the office is closed Sunday and Monday.** The 833 number is
likely a call center — ask to be transferred to the on-site leasing office, or ask for a
direct number. A call center cannot approve a short term.

1. **"Your listing says prices vary by lease length. What lease lengths do you offer?"**
   Use their own words. It is the one lever you have.
2. **"What is the shortest lease you will actually write, and what does it cost per
   month?"** If the answer is 12 months, thank them and move on.
3. **"Is the $1,500 studio one of the 7 available units, and is it available on [Tom's
   date]?"** Cheap units go first — confirm the $1,500 is real inventory, not a teaser.
4. **"Confirm the elevator, and is the studio near it?"** The elevator is the reason this
   building is on the shortlist. Verify it.
5. **"Which utilities are billed separately, and roughly what do they run?"**
6. **"Is the carport included in the rent?"**
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published. At
   $1,500 the bar is low, but many buildings want 3× rent — ask about asset-based
   qualification before spending the $60 application fee.
8. Confirm the pet policy if relevant: dogs 50 lb max.
