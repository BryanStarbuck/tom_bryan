## Why this is (or isn't) a fit

**The pet policy is the first and possibly last thing to check.** No pets allowed, with no
exception language published. This is the only property in the search with a flat pet ban.
Whether Tom has a pet is an open question in the [overview](../../overview.mdx) — resolve
it before spending any more time here.

**The second problem is that it is 2BR-only.** A boutique 33-unit community with nothing but
1,007 sqft two-bedrooms means Tom pays for a bedroom he will not use. That is why the floor
is $2,449 rather than the ~$1,500–1,900 the cheaper Crossroads buildings ask for a studio.
For a 60-day stay, the extra bedroom is pure cost. It also means more furniture to rent if
you furnish the whole unit — or an empty room, which is the sane choice.

**The term is unresolved.** "Short-term available" is published with no length attached.
That is a hint, not a policy — the same phrase appears on properties that mean 6 months.
If it turns out to mean 6 months, **6 × $2,449 = $14,694 for a 2-month need**, and the page
is over.

**What is genuinely good:** it is in the preferred area (Crossroads, East Bellevue),
~12 minutes to Redmond, parking is **included**, there is an **in-unit washer/dryer** (which
[Bellevue Ridge](./Bellevue_Ridge.mdx) does not have), and the deposit is only **$500**. A
33-unit boutique property is also small enough that the owner may have real discretion on
term — small landlords are usually the flexible ones.

| **Est. all-in per month** | **~$3,100–3,700** |

**This breaks $3,300 at the pessimistic end, and it is barely under at the optimistic end.**
The 2BR-only footprint is what does it. Short-term premiums are never published in this
market — every listing says only "leases shorter than 12 months often include additional
fees" — so that estimate is the load-bearing unknown on this page, and here there is no
headroom to absorb a surprise.

## When you call

Only call **425-785-0101** if Tom has no pet.

1. **"Confirm: no pets at all, no exceptions?"** Get it plainly before anything else.
2. **"What lease lengths do you actually write?"** Their listing says short-term is
   available without saying what that means.
3. **"What is the short-term premium in dollars per month?"**
4. **"Is there anything smaller than the 2BR?"** Almost certainly no, but ask.
5. **"Which utilities are included?"** Parking is included — confirm.
6. **"Is there an elevator, and is a ground-floor unit available?"** Not published; a
   33-unit boutique property may well have none.
7. **"What's the income requirement, and do you qualify on assets?"** Not published, and a
   real risk for a retiree at a $2,449 base.
