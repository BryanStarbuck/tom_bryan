## Read this before you call anyone

**Almost no price above is a quote.** Extended-stay hotels and furnished operators
deliberately hide monthly rates — you only get a real number by phone. Larkspur Landing is
the single exception that publishes a 30+ night rate ($85/night), and even that rate card
expired 2026-05-02. Everything marked *(est)* is a nightly rate multiplied by 30, which is
the **worst-case** number a one-night tourist pays. Real monthly quotes usually come in
**below** these.

**Book 30 nights, never 29 or 28.** Under [WAC 458-20-166](https://app.leg.wa.gov/wac/default.aspx?cite=458-20-166),
a guest becomes "nontransient" at 30 continuous days and lodging tax stops applying —
worth roughly **12–15%** on the Eastside, or $300–450/month. Two mechanics matter:

* The clock needs **unbroken** occupancy. One reservation. Checking out resets it.
* Some hotels drop the tax only from day 30 forward; others **refund it retroactively** on
  the first 29 days if you contract 30+ days up front. That difference is ~$350. Ask which.

A 29-day stay is the worst possible length: all the cost, none of the exemption.

**The budget is very achievable.** Contrary to the worry that everything is too expensive,
at least six options clear $3,300 on *estimated rack rates before any negotiation*. The
constraint is not money — it is **term length and furniture**.

## What the research actually found

**No conventional apartment building in Redmond, Bellevue, or Kirkland offers true
month-to-month.** The shortest conventional terms found anywhere: 2 months (The Luke,
Redmond Place, Villaggio, Avalon at Bear Creek), then 3 (Bell Marymoor, Madison Bellevue).
Real 30-day flexibility exists in only four places:

1. **Extended-stay hotels** — purpose-built for this, full kitchens, no lease at all.
2. **Furnished operators** — Landing, Blueground, AvalonBay Furnished+.
3. **55+ senior communities** — Merrill Gardens and MorningStar are genuinely month-to-month.
4. **Private owners** — CHBO, Furnished Finder, Craigslist, Nextdoor.

Two exceptions worth knowing: [Catalina](./locations/apartment/Catalina_Apartments.mdx)
publishes month-to-month as an actual policy with all utilities, cable, phone, internet
and parking bundled at $1,495–1,895, and [Bellevue Ridge](./locations/apartment/Bellevue_Ridge.mdx)
publishes an "MTM flat fee," which means month-to-month is a real product there. Those two
are the only conventional buildings in the whole search that advertise it outright.

**Unfurnished is a trap for a 2-month stay.** A cheap unfurnished 1BR needs furniture, and
[CORT's Redmond showroom](./locations/corporate_housing/CORT_Furniture_Rental.mdx) quotes
$240–295/month — but that is the *12-month* rate; a 1–2 month rental prices higher. Add
delivery, two moves, and utility setup. This is why the extended-stay hotels win: no
furniture, no lease, no utility accounts, walk in with suitcases.

**Airbnb really is too expensive — the assumption held up.** Median Bellevue entire-home
30-night all-in is **~$5,090**. Getting under $3,300 requires the bottom quartile
($129/night) *and* a host offering a 30–40% monthly discount, which lands ~$3,202. It's a
needle, not a market. [Vrbo is worse](./locations/short_term_rental/Vrbo.mdx) — its own site
advertises only ~10% average monthly savings vs Airbnb's 19%.

## Warnings

**Sonder is dead — do not contact them.** [Chapter 7 liquidation announced 2025-11-10](./locations/platform/Sonder.mdx)
after Marriott terminated its license. **Guests were locked out mid-stay and told to vacate
within hours.** That is precisely the failure mode to protect a senior from. Zeus Living is
also [defunct](./locations/platform/Zeus_Living.mdx) (Nov 2023).

**Two live Craigslist scams were confirmed during this research.** Both Kirkland "furnished
condo, $1,200/mo" ads are MLS-scrape fraud — one unit is actively *for sale* at $335,000 and
the ad copies the listing text verbatim; comparable units rent at $2,995. Details in
[Craigslist](./locations/sublet/Craigslist_Eastside.mdx). **Treat any Eastside furnished 1BR
under ~$1,800/month as presumptively fraudulent until seen in person.** Never wire, Zelle,
Venmo, or gift-card a deposit before walking the unit.

**Avoid [Landing Standby](./locations/platform/Landing_Standby.mdx)** despite the tempting
$1,795 flat rate — Landing picks the unit *and* the city, and can bump you with **3 days'
notice**. Landing's main service also has a documented record of reservation bumping and
late cancellations.

**Rule out the whole coliving category** — PadSplit, SpareRoom, Diggz, Tripalink, Bungalow,
[HomeRoom](./locations/platform/HomeRoom.mdx). Their attractive sub-$1,500 pricing *is* the
shared living Tom is avoiding.

**Income requirements are a real risk.** Most buildings want 2.5–3× rent in income, which
can trip up a retiree. The friendliest found: [Vue at Kirkland](./locations/apartment/Vue_at_Kirkland.mdx)
at 2×, [Bell Marymoor](./locations/apartment/Bell_Marymoor_Park.mdx) at 2.25×. Ask about
asset-based qualification. Hotels have no income test at all — another point in their favor.

## Recommended call order

Every one of these needs a phone call; none can be resolved online.

1. **[Larkspur Landing Bellevue](./locations/hotel/Larkspur_Landing_Bellevue.mdx) — (425) 373-1212.** The only published 30+ night rate, full kitchen, weekly housekeeping, free coin-less laundry, explicit over-30-night tax exemption. ~20 min to Redmond is its only real flaw.
2. **[ESA Seattle-Redmond](./locations/hotel/Extended_Stay_America_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) — (425) 885-6675.** Full kitchen, 9 min to Redmond, most likely to clear budget. Call the front desk, **not** the 800 number — central reservations cannot quote monthly.
3. **[WoodSpring Suites Redmond](./locations/hotel/WoodSpring_Suites_Seattle_Redmond.mdx) — (425) 598-7010.** Actually *in* Redmond, full kitchen, and the best-documented accessibility anywhere in this search: roll-in showers, 32" doors, braille elevator.
4. **[Landing](./locations/platform/Landing.mdx) — (415) 231-1701.** Ask a human to hold a Redmond unit. The website hides Redmond under the Seattle metro and units 404 within days.
5. **[Between Moves](./locations/corporate_housing/Between_Moves.mdx) — (425) 822-0533, ask for Coral.** A 25-year local owner-operator. Site says no units, but a one-person shop turns over by phone before anything gets posted. Ask her for referrals — she's the referral node in this market.
6. **[Merrill Gardens](./locations/senior_living/Merrill_Gardens_at_Kirkland.mdx) — (425) 828-2570.** True month-to-month, no buy-in, meals and housekeeping included, zero furniture cost. Sources disagree on price ($2,595 / $3,095 / $3,995) — worth resolving.

### Ask every caller the same five questions

1. What is the **all-in** rate for 30 nights, and for 60 nights?
2. Does lodging tax drop at day 30 — and do you **refund it retroactively** on the first 29 days if I contract 30+ nights up front?
3. Is parking free? What's the pet fee? How often is housekeeping?
4. Is there an **elevator**, and can I get a ground-floor unit or grab bars?
5. *(Apartments only)* What's the shortest term you'll actually write, and what is the exact short-term premium?

## Open questions

* **Tom's exact move-in date** drives every quote. Nothing can be priced without it.
* **Does Tom have a pet?** It rules out [Champagne at Bellevue](./locations/apartment/Champagne_At_Bellevue.mdx) entirely and changes the math elsewhere ([La Quinta](./locations/hotel/La_Quinta_Seattle_Bellevue_Kirkland.mdx) is free; Larkspur is $25/night = $750/month).
* **Does he need a full kitchen?** It's the deciding factor between La Quinta / Redmond Inn (micro + fridge only) and the ESA / WoodSpring / Larkspur tier.
* **How much does the 20-minute Redmond drive really matter?** Relaxing it opens Bothell's Residence Inn and the cheaper Bothell ESAs.
* **Accessibility needs?** Verified accessible units exist at WoodSpring, Bellevue Ridge (ADA studio, $2,000), Westwater, Bell Totem Lake, and The 101.
