====================================================================
BIG PICTURE - READ THIS FIRST
====================================================================

* The apartments in this folder are NOT all on one program. They split across
  several different government programs, and each program has its OWN income
  limit. So there is more than one income threshold. They are listed in their
  own sections below.

* ALL of the income limits are tied to King County "Area Median Income" (AMI),
  published every year by HUD. The programs differ mainly in WHICH percent of
  AMI they cap you at (30%, 50%, 60%, or 80%).

* The income that counts is the WHOLE HOUSEHOLD'S gross annual income from all
  sources - Social Security PLUS both pensions all count. They are added
  together.

* KEY TAKEAWAY (explained in detail at the bottom): Tom's estimated combined
  retirement income is roughly $90,000/year. That is ABOVE almost every limit
  below. So Tom most likely does NOT income-qualify for the subsidized units -
  he qualifies for the MARKET-RATE senior apartments (no income cap). This is
  the single most important conclusion in this document.


====================================================================
KING COUNTY INCOME LIMITS - THE NUMBERS (effective April 1, 2025)
====================================================================

These are the gross-annual-income caps. Tom alone = a 1-PERSON household. If he
moved in with someone, use the 2-person column.

  Percent of AMI       1 Person        2 Person
  ------------------   -----------     -----------
  30% AMI              $33,050         $37,750
  50% AMI              $55,000         $62,850
  60% AMI              $66,000         $75,420
  80% AMI (approx)     ~$84,150        ~$96,150

* 30% AMI = "Extremely Low Income"
* 50% AMI = "Very Low Income"
* 60% AMI = the common tax-credit ceiling
* 80% AMI = "Low Income" (the highest cap any of these programs uses)

(80% figures are approximate - HUD rounds them slightly differently year to year.
The 30/50/60% figures are the published King County numbers.)


====================================================================
SECTION 1 - PROGRAM: HUD SECTION 202 (Supportive Housing for the Elderly)
====================================================================

* What it is: Federal program, buildings built specifically for low-income
  seniors 62+. Rent is set at 30% of the tenant's monthly income (so rent goes
  up or down with income).

* Income limit: Must be at or below 50% AMI to get in.
    1 person: $55,000/yr     2 person: $62,850/yr

* Apartments in this folder on this program (or HUD-subsidized like it):
    - Emma McRedmond Manor (Redmond)
    - Issaquah Gardens (Issaquah)
    - Elbert House (Bellevue) - states "must be <= 50% median income"
    - Hutchison House (Issaquah)
    - Marcus Place / Catholic Housing Services (Bellevue)


====================================================================
SECTION 2 - PROGRAM: PROJECT-BASED SECTION 8 (HUD)
====================================================================

* What it is: The subsidy is attached to the building. Tenant pays about 30% of
  income for rent; HUD covers the rest. Some of these also accept a Section 8
  Housing Choice Voucher.

* Income limit: At or below 50% AMI to qualify (80% AMI is the absolute legal
  ceiling, but in practice these target 50% and below).
    1 person: $55,000/yr (50% AMI)     2 person: $62,850/yr

* Apartments in this folder on this program:
    - Kirkland Plaza (Imagine Housing) - "project-based Sec 8 <50% AMI"
    - Providence John Gabriel House (Redmond) - HUD, income <60% AMI
    - Bellepark East (Bellevue) - accepts Section 8
    - Greenbrier Heights (Woodinville) - accepts Section 8
    - Casa Juanita (Kirkland) - Section 8 accepted


====================================================================
SECTION 3 - PROGRAM: KCHA PUBLIC / SUBSIDIZED HOUSING
        (King County Housing Authority - the "206-574-xxxx" and kcha.org units)
====================================================================

* What it is: Housing the county housing authority owns or subsidizes. Rent is
  generally income-based (about 30% of income). Most of the income-based units
  in this folder are these.

* Income limit: Admission cap is 80% AMI, but these are TARGETED to very-low and
  extremely-low income, so realistically you need to be at or below 50% AMI to
  have a real chance, and many prioritize 30% AMI.
    Admission ceiling - 1 person: ~$84,150/yr (80% AMI)
    Realistic target  - 1 person: $55,000/yr (50% AMI) or below

* Apartments in this folder on this program (KCHA):
    - Patricia Harris Manor, Forest Glen, Forest Grove, Avondale Manor,
      Parkway (all Redmond)
    - Bellevue Manor, Spiritwood Manor (Bellevue)
    - Kirkland Place, Slater Park, Plum Court, Village Plaza, Kirkwood Terrace,
      Juanita Court, Cedarwood (Kirkland)
    - Eastridge House (Issaquah)
    - Vantage Point, Glenview Heights, Vista Heights (Renton)
    - Wells Wood (Woodinville)

* NOTE: Many of these have closed waitlists or are "not accepting applications."


====================================================================
SECTION 4 - PROGRAM: LIHTC TAX-CREDIT (Low-Income Housing Tax Credit)
====================================================================

* What it is: Privately owned "affordable" buildings financed with tax credits.
  Rent is a FIXED below-market amount (not 30%-of-income), but you must income-
  qualify to move in. This is the program with the most generous cap (60% AMI).

* Income limit: At or below 60% AMI (some units restricted to 50% or 30%).
    1 person: $66,000/yr (60% AMI)     2 person: $75,420/yr
    Lower-tier units: $55,000 (50%) or $33,050 (30%) - 1 person

* Apartments in this folder on this program:
    - Ashwood Court Senior Apartments (Bellevue) - tax-credit 62+
    - Woodlands at Forbes Lake (Kirkland) - income/age restricted
    - Ardea Senior Apartments (Kirkland) - 62+ tax-credit
    - Athene Senior Housing (Kirkland) - 30-60% AMI
    - Vasa Creek Woods (Bellevue) - income-restricted 62+
    - Greenbrier Heights (Woodinville) - 30/50/60% AMI tiers
    - Providence John Gabriel House (Redmond) - income <60% AMI


====================================================================
SECTION 5 - PROGRAM: ARCH INCOME-RESTRICTED (local Eastside affordable set-aside)
====================================================================

* What it is: "A Regional Coalition for Housing" - Eastside cities require some
  units in newer market-rate buildings to be set aside as affordable. Caps vary
  by building, usually 60% to 80% AMI.

* Income limit: typically 60% AMI, sometimes up to 80% AMI.
    1 person: $66,000/yr (60%) up to ~$84,150/yr (80%)

* Apartments in this folder on this program:
    - Reunion at Redmond Ridge (Redmond) - 285 of 321 units are ARCH income-
      restricted; the rest are full market-rate.


====================================================================
SECTION 6 - MARKET-RATE SENIOR APARTMENTS (NO INCOME LIMIT)
====================================================================

* What it is: Regular senior apartments with no government subsidy and NO income
  cap. Anyone who can afford the rent qualifies. Given Tom's income (below),
  THIS is the category he most likely actually qualifies for.

* Income limit: NONE.

* Apartments in this folder with no income cap:
    - Brandenwood Senior Apartments (Bellevue) - 62+, rent $1,655+
    - Crossroads Active Senior Community / SHAG (Bellevue) - 55+, $1,750-2,500
    - Park Plaza Apartments (Bellevue) - 55+, $3,700
    - Reunion at Redmond Ridge (Redmond) - the 36 non-ARCH market units


====================================================================
RESIDENCY REQUIREMENT - DOES TOM HAVE TO BE A WASHINGTON RESIDENT?
====================================================================

* NO long-time-state-residency requirement. You do NOT have to have lived in
  Washington (or King County) for any length of time to apply or qualify for
  these programs. KCHA confirms applicants can apply from anywhere - you are not
  required to already live in the county.

* Caveat - PREFERENCE vs REQUIREMENT: Some waitlists give a "local preference"
  (people who already live or work in the area move up the list faster). That is
  a tie-breaker that affects HOW FAST you get in, NOT whether you are eligible.
  Tom coming from out of state can still apply and qualify; he just may not get
  the local-preference bump on certain waitlists.

* Bottom line: Residency is NOT a barrier for Tom. Income (above) is the real
  gate.

TOM STARBUCK - ESTIMATED MONTHLY INCOME
====================================================================

These are ESTIMATES based on typical formulas, not Tom's actual statements. For
the real numbers we need his Social Security award letter and his two pension
statements. Assumptions: Tom is ~83, treated as a 1-person household.

* IMPORTANT NOTE ON CALIFORNIA LAW-ENFORCEMENT PENSIONS:
  Many California police/sheriff jobs did NOT pay into Social Security (the
  pension replaced it). That used to REDUCE the Social Security a person could
  draw (the "Windfall Elimination Provision"). That reduction was REPEALED by
  the Social Security Fairness Act (signed Jan 2025). So as of 2026 Tom's
  Social Security is NOT cut by his government pensions.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
1) SOCIAL SECURITY (assumed age 83)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* The 2026 average retired-worker benefit is about $2,071/month.
* If much of Tom's career was non-Social-Security-covered law enforcement, his
  own SS record may be thinner than average; if he had other covered work it
  could be near or above average.
* ESTIMATE: about $1,900/month (reasonable range $1,500 - $2,300/month).

--------------------------------------------------------------------
2) LA COUNTY SHERIFF'S DEPARTMENT - 23 years (LACERA pension)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* LACERA "safety" member. Older safety plans earn roughly 2% to 2.6% of final
  pay per year of service. 23 years -> roughly 50-60% of final average pay.
* Final pay for a retired deputy/sergeant (retiring decades ago) might have been
  ~$70,000-$90,000/year, then grown by years of cost-of-living raises.
* ESTIMATE today: about $3,800/month (range $3,300 - $4,600/month).

--------------------------------------------------------------------
3) OCEANSIDE POLICE DEPARTMENT - 13 years (CalPERS pension)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* CalPERS local-safety member. Safety formula ~2.5% to 3% per year of service.
  13 years -> roughly 33-39% of final average pay.
* Final pay ~$60,000-$85,000/year, then grown by cost-of-living raises.
* ESTIMATE today: about $2,000/month (range $1,700 - $2,600/month).

--------------------------------------------------------------------
TOTAL ESTIMATED MONTHLY INCOME
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* Point estimate:  ~$7,700/month  =  ~$92,000/year
* Reasonable range: ~$6,500 - $9,500/month  =  ~$78,000 - $114,000/year
